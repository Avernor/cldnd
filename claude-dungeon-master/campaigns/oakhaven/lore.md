# Δρυόχωρο (Oakhaven) — Lore & Worldbuilding

> **DM Note — Language**: This campaign is run in Greek at the table. All NPC and place names below are given in Greek as the primary form, with English glosses in parentheses for DM reference the first time they appear. All player-facing read-aloud text lives in the **Βιβλιοθήκη Αφήγησης** section at the bottom of this file. DM-facing structure, mechanics, and planning notes remain in English.

---

## Το Χωριό — Δρυόχωρο

A logging village of roughly 200 souls in a steep river valley on the road to Καίλεν (Kaelen). Timber houses with moss-dark shingles, bells at dawn and dusk, the smell of pine sap and woodsmoke. Cold and wet most of the year. The village clings to the river and slopes downhill toward the mill.

- **Population**: ~200
- **Economy**: Logging (primary), river trade, stop on the Καίλεν road
- **Government**: Informal — an Elders' Circle led by the village matriarch, with the mill owner as de facto economic power
- **Current season**: **Late autumn.** Cold nights, wet mornings, the last of the lumber coming downriver before Ο Μυελός freezes. Smoke in every chimney. **Το Χειμωνιάτικο Πανηγύρι** (the Winter Fair) is less than two weeks away.

### The year in Δρυόχωρο
- **Άνοιξη (Spring)**: The thaw. Ο Μυελός runs high and dangerous. Logging restarts.
- **Καλοκαίρι (Summer)**: Longest working months. Lumber floats downriver to Καίλεν. Village almost empty by day.
- **Φθινόπωρο (Autumn)**: Small harvest from the valley farms. The Winter Fair. Everyone returns.
- **Χειμώνας (Winter)**: Snowed in. The pass to Καίλεν closes. Whatever happens here, they face alone.

The snowed-in winter is a pressure tool: the party may face a deadline before the valley seals itself off from help.

## Γεωγραφία (Geography)

### Ο Παλαιόδρυμος (The Oldwood)
Dense pine forest climbing north into the foothills of the Nine Brothers. The lumber camps work its edges. The deep wood is not spoken of after dark. Hunters and trappers live along its fringe.

### Ο Μυελός (The Marrow)
The river that splits the valley. Turns Ο Μύλος του Βόσσου. Locals say it "carries things downstream that nobody remembers throwing in." Literally true during timeline shifts — edited objects sometimes wash up.

### Οι Εννιά Αδελφοί (The Nine Brothers)
A ring of nine jagged peaks crowning the valley to the north. Local legend says they were nine giants turned to stone for a crime nobody remembers. Each peak has a name in an old tongue no one still speaks fluently — Γερόντισσα Εστία Μύρων knows them, and strangely, so does the child Μικρή Ιώ.

- **Τα Σπλάχνα** (the Gut — the abandoned dwarven mine) tunnels into the foot of the tallest brother.
- **Το Μαύρο Δάχτυλο** (the Black Finger — the ruined obsidian tower) sits in a pass between two of the smaller brothers.

**Secret — the nine/nine rhyme**: Nine Brothers, nine standing stones on the hill above the village. They mirror each other. *One stone hides herself from mortal sight* — she is still there, whole and doing her work, but only children, the very old, or anyone who knows her verse can perceive her. Adults trying to count the stones get a headache and land on eight. Eventually the party may realize *one Brother hides too* — one peak is subtly wrong, not shorter, not absent, just *uncountable*. Vanya's work is tied to both concealments. She is trying to find the hidden ones because they are the ones still holding.

### Το Πέρασμα (The Crossing)
Where the Καίλεν road fords Ο Μυελός at the mouth of the valley. The first and last sight of Δρυόχωρο for travelers.

### Village layout — DM reference sketch

## Ιστορία — Η Πληγή του Κόσμου (History — The Wound in the World)

Long ago — or perhaps *outside* of when; even those who remember are unclear — **a being of time was mortally wounded**. It did not die in one place. It bled across the world as it fell, and where its ichor soaked the land, time went thin. These places are **τα λεπτά σημεία** (the thin spots).

- **Δρυόχωρο sits on the smallest known thin spot.** A single drop of ichor.
- **Καίλεν sits on a much larger one.** A capital city built unknowingly over a pool of it.
- **Somewhere in the region, the being finally fell.** That place is the wound itself. Nobody alive knows where.

The nine standing stones on the hill above Δρυόχωρο were placed by an ancient order — dwarves, druids, or a priesthood long forgotten — who knew what lay beneath and built a containment over the drop. The Nine Brothers are a natural echo: the land scarred into a mirror of the stones, petrified into the shape of what was being held.

The obsidian tower came later, but **not nearly as late as it looks.** It was built by a forgotten order as an *extension* of the containment — a structure to watch, diagnose, and reinforce the drop from above. Someone, centuries later, **attacked it.** The ritual hall's binding pillars were defaced with a hammer; the upper tower was broken; whoever did the damage meant to *open* the thin spot, not close it. **They are Vanya's predecessor** — the first mortal to try what she is trying, and the reason the containment is failing now. Their identity is unknown to all current NPCs.

Later still, a Circle of Five scholars — ancient, obscure, working from fragments of the original order's knowledge — found the broken tower and tried to *repair* it. They built new instruments to let a mortal stand inside the thin spot without dissolving: the **compass** (an anchor, keeping its bearer real), and a second instrument, the **conductor**, which would let the anchor's bearer speak to whatever was bleeding and ask it to heal. The ritual required five scholars standing in a sanctum ring. **The fifth broke ring** — lost his nerve, grabbed the conductor, and fled through Τα Σπλάχνα. He died in the mine clutching a backward-running watch. The collapsed ritual did not discharge; it pitched inward and froze the remaining four in the sanctum, radiating a **stasis field** outward through the lab. That field is still on. It is what the party walked into during the storm — and it is the only reason they remember Σίλας was alive two nights ago.

The compass is one of the Circle's surviving instruments. It is not a weapon. It is an **anchor**. The field and the compass are kin: the compass *resonates* with the field and carries a fragment of it wherever the compass goes, keeping its bearer real when the thin spot produces edits. The Circle built it for their own survival inside the ritual. The party inherited it by accident.

## Βάνια — What She Really Is

Βάνια (Vanya) is a grieving mother who learned, from a source not yet revealed, about the thin spots. She does not fully understand what she is doing, and she is learning as she goes.

What she has figured out: through a thin spot, she can push **θραύσματα** (fragments) of herself backward in time. Splinters of will. Each fragment is *almost* Vanya — her face, her voice, her tired journal, her grief — but partial. Each can test a change to the past without risking the whole of her. When a fragment succeeds or fails, the real Vanya — wherever and whenever she is — feels it through the thin spot and adjusts.

**The Vanya the party met at Το Κούφιο Ελάφι was a fragment.** Polite, tired, a probe. Erasing Σίλας was a test. Whatever she does next is a test. **Δρυόχωρο is her lab bench** — the smallest thin spot she could find. If she breaks it, she loses a village she never knew. She is practicing.

### Why the compass matters
- It is not only keeping the party real. It lets them *see fragments as fragments* — the wrongness of her, the flicker at the edges. Without it, Vanya is just a quiet woman with a journal.
- The bard began to fade because a fragment tested an edit requiring his nonexistence. The compass's field let him write the bootstrap letter and pull himself back.
- Every fragment Vanya burns teaches the real one. She is getting better.

### Escalation (the campaign spine)
- **Part 1 — Δρυόχωρο (lv 2–5)**: Discover the thin spot. Recognize the fragment. Learn there are *others*. Follow the trail to Καίλεν.
- **Part 2 — Καίλεν (lv 5–9)**: A far larger thin spot under the capital. Multiple fragments at once. Political stakes — whole neighborhoods vanishing quietly. Possibly the party exchanges words with the real Vanya through a mirror, a dream, or a letter, but never meets her in the flesh.
- **Part 3 — Regional (lv 10+)**: Find the wound itself. Confront whatever the real Vanya has become by then. The final choice is not "stop her." It is *what to do with the wound.*

### The horror underneath
Saving her child requires undoing the world. Literal. The thin spots are connected — all one being's blood — and every edit makes the wound bleed faster. Every fragment that fails teaches her. Eventually she will know exactly what to do, and it will cost everything.

The glimmer of hope: maybe she can be reached. Maybe the party arrives before she commits. Maybe her child is already beyond saving and the kindness is to tell her — and survive telling her.

## Τοπόσημα (Landmarks)

### Το Κούφιο Ελάφι (The Hollow Stag) — the inn
Two stories, smoke-blackened rafters, a stuffed stag's head over the hearth missing one antler. Best (and only) beer in the valley. Run by **Γερο-Αλέξης**, and — according to everyone in the village — always has been.

### Ο Μύλος του Βόσσου (Voss's Mill)
The lumber mill on Ο Μυελός. Owned by **Κάλχας Βόσσος**. Employs a quarter of the village. The economic heart of Δρυόχωρο.

### Οι Εννιά Φύλακες (The Nine Watchers) — the standing stones
Nine weathered stones on a low hill above the village. Older than the settlement. Locals leave small offerings — bread, a coin, a lock of hair — without knowing why. Γερόντισσα Εστία claims each stone has a name in a language she cannot fully translate. **One stone hides herself** — she is present but cannot be counted by adult villagers; children and Ιώ count nine without effort, adults land on eight and develop a mild headache if they try to check. Most villagers no longer notice the slip.

### Το Λεπτό Παρεκκλήσι (The Thin Chapel)
A one-room shrine to a modest harvest god. Tended by **Αδελφός Ορέστης**. A wooden altar, cheap candles, a small graveyard behind it.

### Το Νεκρό Χωράφι (The Burying Field)
The village cemetery behind Το Λεπτό Παρεκκλήσι. *Crucial to the horror.* New graves appear after timeline shifts. Old ones vanish. Αδελφός Ορέστης has started to notice.

### Τα Σπλάχνα (The Gut) — the abandoned dwarven mine
High on the flank of the tallest Brother. Locals call it Τα Σπλάχνα "because it eats men." Θωμάς was rescued here. The skeleton with the backward watch was found here.

### Το Μαύρο Δάχτυλο (The Black Finger) — the ruined obsidian tower
Visible on a clear day from the upper slopes, in a pass between two Brothers. Nobody goes there. The party has been, once, during the storm.

### Το Σιδεράδικο (The Forge)
At the river's edge. Owned and worked by **Αλκμήνη Βόσσου**, Κάλχας's younger sister. Widowed young, hard, fair. The fighter will like her — and she will quietly notice his heirloom blade.

### Τα Είδη της Μαρίνας (Marina's General Goods)
The village store. Dry goods, rope, lamp oil, boots. Run by **Μαρίνα**, a woman who has forgotten she ever had a husband — a quiet thin-spot casualty. Her wedding ring is in her drawer and she does not know why she owns it.

### Η Βρεγμένη Κουρούνα (The Wet Crow)
A smaller drinking-house by the mill. Where the mill hands go when they don't want to be seen at Το Κούφιο Ελάφι. No innkeeper — run by whichever mill hand's wife is sober that night.

### Ο Στάβλος του Κέλη (Kelis's Stable)
Between the inn and Το Πέρασμα. Horses, a cart or two. Run by **Γερο-Κέλης**, a taciturn old stablehand who writes everything down. His ledger is a goldmine for the party if they think to ask.

### Το Σπίτι της Νεφέλης (Nefeli's House)
A small cottage on the edge of the village near the chapel. Home of **Γριά Νεφέλη**, the village midwife and informal healer. Wise woman. Hedge-knowledge of herbs and a little charm-magic. Not a spellcaster — but she *notices* what others do not.

## NPCs

### Βάνια (Vanya) — fragment, Big Bad
A splinter of the real Vanya pushed backward through the Δρυόχωρο thin spot. Polite, tired, grieving, writing in a journal at Το Κούφιο Ελάφι. Not cruel. A probe testing edits. When a fragment is "defeated," it does not die — it unravels, and the real Vanya feels the result and learns. The party will meet more fragments, and eventually, the real one.
- **Hook**: The journal. Every fragment carries one. Stealing one would be a coup.

### Σίλας Ρέυν (Sylas Reyne) — *EDITED*
Was the innkeeper of Το Κούφιο Ελάφι in the original timeline: hard, angry, widowed. In the altered timeline the party now inhabits, **Σίλας died ten years ago in a tavern brawl.** Villagers remember him clearly — as a tragic figure who never recovered from his young daughter Κόρη's death and drank himself into the fight that killed him. His grave exists in Το Νεκρό Χωράφι, weathered, ten years old by every outward sign. The party is the only living evidence that he was behind the bar two nights ago. This is a subtler edit than erasure: Vanya did not remove him, she *relocated his death earlier in time*. Cheaper, smaller, and nearly impossible for anyone without the compass to notice. The first proven example of her technique — and a warning that what she is doing is not a blunt instrument. If the party loses the compass, they lose their original memory of Σίλας and accept the ten-years-ago version.

### Θωμάς Τεφρός (Thomas Ash)
Mill worker rescued from Τα Σπλάχνα in Session 1. Quiet, shaken. Since the rescue, sometimes sees "double" — flashes of lives he didn't live. Owes the party his life.
- **Hook**: He may be the first villager to start *remembering* Σίλας, if the party keeps him close. His time in the mine made him permeable to the thin spot.

### Μίρα Τεφρή (Mira Ash)
Θωμάς's pregnant wife. Sharp-eyed, practical. The party's first friend in the village.
- **Hook**: Her unborn child is plot-critical. Vanya's grief is for a lost child. Mira's child is *the thing Vanya wants* — in the abstract, and possibly in the literal. The real Vanya may eventually target this pregnancy. Handle with weight.

### Γερο-Αλέξης (Old Alexis)
The current innkeeper of Το Κούφιο Ελάφι. In the altered timeline, he has run the inn for the past ten years — since the night of the barfight that killed Σίλας. He was there that night; survived; and when no Ρέυν heir remained, the Elders' Circle handed him the keys. Villagers will confirm all of this without hesitation. He is harmless, slightly confused, gentle with drunks, and genuinely sad when Σίλας comes up. He thinks the party is touched in the head for insisting Σίλας was behind the bar two nights ago.
- **Hook**: He writes poetry on napkins without remembering doing it, and the poetry is surprisingly good — sharper than the man who writes it. Edits leak into him. One napkin, if the party finds it, carries a line in handwriting that isn't his: *«Ο πάγκος ήταν δικός μου.»* (The bar was mine.)

### Κάλχας Βόσσος (Kalchas Vossos)
Owner of Ο Μύλος του Βόσσου. Big man, quiet, careful with coin. Suspicious of outsiders. Economic power in the village.
- **Secret**: His grandfather kept journals about the Oldwood's "Ήσυχες Μέρες" (Quiet Days). Pages the party can find — if they earn his trust or steal them.
- **Hook**: He resists change violently. Not evil, but will side with stability over truth every time.

### Γερόντισσα Εστία Μύρων (Elder Estia Myron)
Village matriarch, 70s. Sharp memory for old things, confused about recent ones — early thin-spot symptom. Knows the names of all Nine Watchers in a language nobody else speaks fluently.
- **Hook**: If she is alone with Μικρή Ιώ, they sometimes *hum* together — the same wordless tune, the same rhythm, without coordinating. Neither remembers afterward.

### Αδελφός Ορέστης (Brother Orestis)
Priest of Το Λεπτό Παρεκκλήσι. Gentle, alcoholic, loves the village. Has started finding extra graves in Το Νεκρό Χωράφι and is terrified. Will confide in a sympathetic PC.
- **Hook**: He has begun keeping a private count of how many people are in the village each morning. The number keeps changing.

### Ο Κόρακας (The Rook)
Hunter/trapper, 40s, lives alone in a cabin at the Oldwood's edge. Saw "a woman in a hood standing in two places at once." Dismissed as drunk. He wasn't.
- **Hook**: He has seen Τα Άφτιαχτα. He calls them **τα χωρίς πρόσωπα** (the faceless). He does not go into the deep Oldwood alone anymore, and he will not tell the party why unless pressed hard.

### Μικρή Ιώ (Little Io)
A child, 8, who sings a rhyme no adult taught her. The rhyme names the Nine Watchers in the same forgotten language Γερόντισσα Εστία uses. She dreams of *«η κυρία με τα κουρασμένα μάτια»* (the lady with the tired eyes).
- **Hook**: She is *unnaturally calm* around the compass. If the party lets her hold it, she will look at the bard and say — quietly, factually — *«Εσύ είσαι ο άντρας που γράφει το γράμμα»* (You are the man who writes the letter). Then she will ask for her mother.

### Λιάνα (Liana) — Ιώ's mother
A thin, tired, warm woman in her early thirties. Washes linen at Ο Μύλος during the day, cleans the chapel at dusk for a few coins from Αδελφός Ορέστης, raises Ιώ alone. Her husband died on the river two years ago — a log jam broke, a rope took him under, his body was found downstream. **In the original timeline, he would have died differently — slower, in bed, from a lung sickness, with his daughter at his side.** Vanya's predecessor altered the circumstance of his death as an early experiment (possibly centuries ago, waiting on Λιάνα's particular life to trigger it; the thin spot's edits don't have to be chronological). The family never knew there was a different version. Λιάνα simply remembers the river.

- Λιάνα does not know anything about thin spots, fragments, or Vanya. She is a regular village woman raising a strange child.
- She is *grateful* that Ιώ is not afraid of the world the way she is afraid of it.
- She lets Ιώ wander the village because Ιώ always comes back, and because the village has always watched Ιώ for her.
- **Hook for Session 2+**: If the party befriends Ιώ and speaks kindly to Λιάνα, they earn a quiet ally with no leverage — she cannot help them investigate anything, but she will feed them, and she will tell them honestly anything she remembers about the village, and she will believe them when they are ready to tell her what is wrong.
- **Payoff later**: If the party eventually rolls back Vanya's edits, Λιάνα's husband's death may be one of the ones they can quietly *un-edit* — giving her back the version where she sat beside him. This is a small, finite, human prize for the party to fight for. Not a plot-critical NPC. A **reason.**

### Αλκμήνη Βόσσου (Alkmini Vossou)
The smith. Κάλχας's younger sister, widowed young. Hard, fair, good with iron.
- **Hook**: If the fighter brings her his heirloom sword for any reason, she will recognize the style of its forging. *«Αυτό είναι παλιά δουλειά. Παλαιότερη απ' το χωριό.»* She cannot say how she knows.

### Μαρίνα (Marina)
General goods shopkeeper. Has forgotten she ever had a husband. A wedding ring in her drawer she cannot explain.
- **Hook**: She is the first NPC the party can *prove* was edited — if they find the ring, match it to a name in Γερο-Κέλης's ledger, and confront her gently.

### Γερο-Κέλης (Old Kelis)
The stablehand at the Crossing end of the village. Taciturn, old, writes *everything* down. His ledger covers forty years of comings and goings, weather, strange events. If the party ever needs to check whether someone existed, the ledger is the answer.
- **Hook**: His own entries sometimes contradict themselves in handwriting he does not remember using.

### Γριά Νεφέλη (Old Nefeli)
The village midwife and informal healer. Sixty-something, small, thin as winter wire. Knows herbs, simple charms, every family secret. Not a spellcaster — she *notices*. Lost her own daughter in childbirth forty years ago and has delivered every baby in Δρυόχωρο since. Lives alone in a cottage full of drying plants and cats she refuses to name.

**Speech pattern**: Slow, matter-of-fact, pauses mid-sentence to look at things other people don't see. Addresses everyone as *«παιδί μου»* regardless of age. Never raises her voice. When she is worried, she becomes *quieter*, not louder.

**Sample lines**:
- *«Κάθισε, παιδί μου. Τσάι. Όχι, δεν σε ρώτησα αν θέλεις — θα πιεις τσάι.»*
- *«Υπάρχουν πράγματα που δεν είναι άρρωστα. Είναι απλώς... λάθος. Καταλαβαίνεις τη διαφορά;»*
- *«Το χωριό μου μιλάει περισσότερο απ' ό,τι νομίζει ο καθένας. Ακούω.»*
- *(Looking at the compass without being shown it)* *«Κρύβεις κάτι ζεστό στην τσέπη σου. Όχι, μην το βγάλεις. Εγώ το είπα, εσύ δεν είπες τίποτα.»*

**What she notices in Session 2** (choose whichever lands — do not use all three):
1. If the party brings the compass into her cottage, a dried bundle of thyme over her hearth begins to turn slowly, as if in a wind there isn't. She looks at it. She looks at the party. She says nothing.
2. *«Η Μίρα Τεφρή κουβαλάει ένα κοριτσάκι. Το ξέρω από την όγδοη εβδομάδα. Αλλά τις τελευταίες μέρες... η αίσθηση κουνιέται. Σαν να μη θέλει να κάτσει ήσυχη. Δεν μου αρέσει αυτό.»* — the first hint that Vanya may already have her eye on the pregnancy.
3. If a PC accepts her tea, she takes their wrist (not their hand) and holds it for a moment. *«Έχεις ένα λεπτό σκίσιμο στη μνήμη σου. Μικρό ακόμη. Πρόσεχε.»* — she senses compass-exposure on them.

- **Hook**: She delivered Μίρα's mother and will deliver Μίρα's child. If Vanya moves on the pregnancy, Νεφέλη will sense it first — hours before anyone else. She is the party's early-warning system for the most important thing in the village, if they earn her respect.

### Θάλεια (Thalia) — Άνδρος's wife
A mill-hand's widow who does not know she is a widow. Thirties, wiry, red-raw hands from laundry. Works shifts washing linen for Ο Μύλος and Το Κούφιο Ελάφι. Lives alone in a small room at the back of Τα Είδη της Μαρίνας — Μαρίνα took her in "when the room came free," though neither woman can quite remember when that was or why the room was free.

**Her edit**: Her husband **Άνδρος** was one of Vanya's earliest experiments — a smaller, quieter test than Σίλας. He was not relocated in time. He was *unmade* — erased so completely the village closed over the gap. But because the edit was imperfect, a remnant of him now walks Ο Παλαιόδρυμος as an Άφτιαχτο (**Thread 7**). Θάλεια remembers nothing: no husband, no wedding, no name. But she dreams of a man she cannot describe, wakes crying, and cannot explain why.

**Tells** (for the party to find if they think to look):
- She flinches whenever someone says the name *Άνδρος* casually, then apologizes and says she doesn't know why.
- She has a single man's wool shirt folded in her chest of drawers that is the wrong size for her, that she has never been able to throw away. She tells herself it was her father's. It wasn't.
- She drinks her tea from a chipped wooden cup carved with a symbol she cannot read — a lumberjack's guild mark. Μαρίνα notices but does not ask.

**If the Άφτιαχτο grace-note lands in Thread 7** and Άνδρος names himself: the party now carries a message from a dead man to a woman who does not remember he existed. *«Πες της ότι δεν ήταν λάθος της.»* This is a seed for Session 3 or 4 — one of the campaign's signature grief beats. Handle with weight. Θάλεια will not believe them at first. The wool shirt is what will break her.

- **Hook**: The party's choice of whether, when, and *how* to tell her is a character moment, not a puzzle. There is no right answer. Γριά Νεφέλη may help, if asked.

### Περρίν Βάσκος (Perrin Vaskos) — *off-screen, Καίλεν*
Tax collector from Καίλεν. Thin, tired, ink-stained. Visits once a year in late summer. The party missed him this year. He would not want to come back — but he may be summoned if Part 1 escalates into something the capital cannot ignore.

### Αριστόδημος Κρέων (Aristodemos Kreon) — *fate unknown, 20–30 years ago*
A scholar from Καίλεν's **Ακαδημία των Ωρών** (Academy of Hours), who spent a season in Δρυόχωρο twenty-odd years ago "studying the stones." Γερόντισσα Εστία remembers him fondly. The Vossos grandfather's journal records him as *«εξαφανίστηκε»* (vanished) before answering any questions — which reads more ominously than it should.

**What he actually did**: He found the Black Finger, climbed into the cellar, read the Circle of Five's frozen notes, and realized what the dormant ritual had been for. He is **not** a member of the Circle — the Circle is centuries older than him. He set up his own workstation in the abandoned fifth slot, worked alone for a season, wrote a confession letter admitting he could not finish without a sixth *willing* to die in the ring, placed the letter in an iron lockbox in the tower's observatory, and left for Καίλεν to fetch help from the Ακαδημία. His notes survive somewhere in the capital. Whether he ever made it home is an open question. **He is the bridge from Part 1 to Part 2.**

## Factions

- **Ο Μύλος** (The Mill) — Κάλχας Βόσσος and his foremen. Economic power. Values stability over truth.
- **Το Παρεκκλήσι** (The Chapel) — Αδελφός Ορέστης. Quiet moral voice.
- **Ο Κύκλος των Γερόντων** (The Elders' Circle) — Εστία and two or three others. Keepers of old lore they no longer fully understand.
- **Οι Άνθρωποι του Δρυμού** (The Oldwood Folk) — Hunters, trappers, Ο Κόρακας. Live outside the village proper. See more than villagers do.

No village faction is evil. Δρυόχωρο's failure is denial, not malice. Vanya is the only true antagonist.

## Ο Παλαιόδρυμος — What Lives There

Three layers of threat in the Oldwood:

### Natural
Wolves, bears, a resident pack of dire wolves deeper in. Winter hunger makes them bold. Standard 5e monsters.

### Folk — Οι Λυκάνθρωποι του Δρόμου (The Road Wolves)
A small band of bandits — deserters from a forgotten war — living in the deep wood. They prey on the Καίλεν road when they're hungry. Led by **Δάφνος**, a former Καίλεν guardsman with a bad eye and a code of honor. Use him as the party chooses to find him — he can become an ally, an enemy, or a dead end.

### Wrong — Τα Άφτιαχτα (The Unmade)
When Vanya's edits cut someone or something out of existence, and the compass's field is *almost but not quite* strong enough to hold them, the edit leaves a **remnant** — a half-real thing wandering Ο Παλαιόδρυμος trying to remember what it was. Some were people. Some were animals. One might have been a building.

- They do not attack on sight.
- They *follow.*
- They *ask*  — wordless at first, then with borrowed voices they cannot quite hold.
- If you answer wrong, they take what they are missing from you.

**Τα Άφτιαχτα are the signature monster of Part 1.** Vanya does not know she is making them. Every fragment she burns leaves one or two more in the woods. The Oldwood is quietly filling up. Ο Κόρακας calls them τα χωρίς πρόσωπα.

**DM stats reference (approximate, CR 1–2 aberration)**:
- AC 12, HP 30, Speed 30 ft
- Psychic touch attack: +4 to hit, 1d8 psychic + DC 12 Wis save or 1 point Wisdom drain (returns on long rest)
- Advantage to avoid being tracked; resistance to non-magical damage; vulnerable to radiant
- **Compass interaction**: within 10 ft of the compass, disadvantage on all rolls and cannot flee
- **Grace note**: if a PC uses Lay on Hands, Healing Word, or Bardic Inspiration *and* speaks the name of the thing the Unmade was (even guessed), it is momentarily restored — a quiet horror beat, then it fades. Reward creative play with this.

## Δρυόχωρο & Καίλεν — The Capital's Reach

Short version: **Καίλεν barely knows Δρυόχωρο exists.**

- Καίλεν sends Περρίν Βάσκος once a year in late summer to collect a cut of the lumber revenue. He does not linger. The party missed him this year.
- No garrison, no guards. The village enforces its own peace through the mill foremen and an informal village watch (three or four men with spears and no training).
- Lumber is floated down Ο Μυελός to a Καίλεν holding depot at a river-meet downstream. The village rarely sees the money.
- Politically, Δρυόχωρο is a dot on a map. Whatever Vanya does here, Καίλεν will not notice for a long time. That is why she chose it.

**The loose thread to Part 2**: Αριστόδημος Κρέων, the dead scholar from Η Ακαδημία των Ωρών, spent a season here twenty years ago studying the Watchers. His notes survive somewhere in the capital. When the party leaves Δρυόχωρο, his trail is what they follow.

## Θρύλοι & Δεισιδαιμονίες (Legends & Superstitions)

What villagers believe, in the form of stories and habits. They do not know about thin spots, fragments, or dying time-beings.

- **«Οι Εννιά Φύλακες προσέχουν.»** *(The Nine Watchers are watching.)* The stones are holy in a vague, unexamined way. On the first night of Το Χειμωνιάτικο Πανηγύρι, every villager walks up the hill and leaves a small offering at a stone of their choice. Nobody knows why. They just always have.

- **«Μην ονοματίζεις τον Αδελφό που λείπει.»** *(Never name the missing Brother.)* A children's rule. Nobody can remember why, or which Brother it is. Trying to count the peaks gives adults a headache. Μικρή Ιώ does it without thinking.

- **Οι Ήσυχες Μέρες** *(The Quiet Days)*. A few times a year, Δρυόχωρο has a day where nothing feels quite right — voices sound flat, the dogs won't go outside. Afterward, things are slightly different. A scar. A tree that has moved. Nobody talks about it. These are days when the real Vanya is working the thin spot from the outside.

- **«Μη σφυρίζεις στον Δρυμό μετά το σούρουπο.»** *(Do not whistle in the Oldwood after dark.)* Ο Κόρακας taught this. He will not say why.

- **«Ο Μυελός παίρνει και δίνει.»** *(The Marrow takes and gives.)* Things wash up that shouldn't — a child's shoe matching no child, a knife with a name no villager bears. Locals bury these finds in Το Νεκρό Χωράφι without asking questions.

## The Hidden Layer — Investigation Tiers

1. **Easy** — Old folk misremember dates. Graves don't match headstones. Children sing a rhyme no adult taught them. Μαρίνα has a wedding ring and no husband.
2. **Medium** — Κάλχας's grandfather's journals. Αδελφός Ορέστης's new graves. Ο Κόρακας's "woman in two places." Γερο-Κέλης's self-contradicting ledger. All point to *something* happening recurrently in the valley.
3. **Hard** — The Nine Watchers mark the boundary of a thin spot. The obsidian tower is its exploit, not its origin. The compass is an anchor from scholars who died trying. Vanya is not the first to try this — she is the first to nearly succeed.
4. **Buried** — Nine/nine mirror. One Watcher missing, one Brother missing. The containment is failing because the real Vanya is prying it open from *outside* time, widening the drop so her splinters can do more with each probe. **The Vanya they met is not the real one. There are others.**

## Βιβλιοθήκη Αφήγησης (Read-Aloud Library)

Ready-to-read Greek narration for key moments in Δρυόχωρο. Use verbatim or adapt to the moment.

### Φτάνοντας στο Δρυόχωρο *(Arriving at Oakhaven)*
> Η στροφή του δρόμου ανοίγει και το Δρυόχωρο απλώνεται από κάτω σας — ένα χωριό γαντζωμένο στις πλαγιές μιας στενής κοιλάδας, κάτω απ' τις κορυφές των Εννιά Αδελφών. Μυρίζει ρετσίνι, καπνό, υγρασία. Ο Μυελός κυλάει αργά ανάμεσα στα ξύλινα σπίτια με τα σκουρόχρωμα κεραμίδια. Κάπου μακριά, ένα σφυρί χτυπά πάνω σε αμόνι. Σε έναν λόφο πάνω απ' το χωριό, εννιά πέτρες στέκονται σε κύκλο — παλιές, φθαρμένες, σιωπηλές.

### Το Κούφιο Ελάφι *(The Hollow Stag)*
> Η πόρτα τρίζει καθώς την ανοίγετε. Μέσα, το τζάκι καίει πνιχτά· ο καπνός έχει μαυρίσει τα δοκάρια της οροφής. Πάνω απ' το τζάκι, ένα βαλσαμωμένο κεφάλι ελαφιού — λείπει το ένα του κέρατο, σπασμένο προ πολλού. Πίσω απ' τον πάγκο, ένας γέρος με μαλλιά σαν χιόνι σκουπίζει ένα ποτήρι με μια πετσέτα που ήταν κάποτε λευκή. Σας χαμογελά αργά, σαν να προσπαθεί να θυμηθεί πού έχει δει το πρόσωπό σας.

### Οι Εννιά Φύλακες *(The Nine Watchers)*
> Στην κορυφή του λόφου στέκονται οι πέτρες. Εννιά. Ή μήπως οκτώ — κάτι στο μέτρημα δεν βγαίνει καθαρό. Ο αέρας εδώ πάνω μυρίζει περίεργα, σαν βροχή που δεν έχει ακόμη πέσει. Οι πέτρες είναι φθαρμένες αλλά όχι σπασμένες, και στη βάση της καθεμιάς βρίσκονται μικρές προσφορές: ένα νόμισμα, μια φέτα ψωμί, μια μπούκλα μαλλί. Κανείς δεν σας έχει πει ποτέ γιατί. Οι χωρικοί το κάνουν επειδή το έχουν πάντα κάνει.

### Ο Παλαιόδρυμος *(The Oldwood)*
> Το δάσος ονομάζεται Παλαιόδρυμος, και είναι παλαιότερο απ' όσο θα έπρεπε. Τα δέντρα πυκνώνουν γρήγορα γύρω σας, και ο ήχος αλλάζει — τα πουλιά σιωπούν, τα βήματά σας ακούγονται μακρινά, σαν να τα κάνει κάποιος άλλος. Κάπου βαθιά στο δάσος, ένα κλαδί σπάει. Μετά, τίποτα.

### Συνάντηση με Άφτιαχτο *(Encountering an Unmade)*
> Κάτι στέκεται ανάμεσα στα δέντρα. Δεν σας επιτίθεται. Δεν μιλάει — ή μάλλον, προσπαθεί να μιλήσει, αλλά το στόμα του δεν θυμάται πώς να σχηματίζει λέξεις. Το πρόσωπό του αλλάζει αργά, σαν να ψάχνει ποιο ήταν κάποτε. Και μετά, πολύ απαλά, σας ρωτάει κάτι που δεν καταλαβαίνετε.

### Τα Σπλάχνα *(The Gut — the mine)*
> Το άνοιγμα της στοάς χάσκει στην πλαγιά του βουνού σαν πληγή. Μυρίζει υγρή πέτρα, σίδερο, και κάτι παλαιότερο — σαν αέρα που έχει κλειστεί εδώ μέσα πολύ περισσότερο απ' όσο θα έπρεπε. Απ' το εσωτερικό δεν ακούγεται τίποτα. Ούτε νερό. Ούτε σταλαγμός. Ούτε αέρας. Μόνο η απουσία.

### Το Μαύρο Δάχτυλο *(The Black Finger — the tower, during the storm)*
> Η θύελλα σας έχει ρίξει από το μονοπάτι, και μπρος σας υψώνεται μια σκιά που πρώτα νομίζετε ότι είναι βουνό. Δεν είναι. Είναι ένας πύργος — σπασμένος στη μέση, γυαλιστερός σαν μαύρο γυαλί, πληγωμένος απ' τον αέρα εδώ και αιώνες αλλά ακόμη όρθιος. Κανένα φως δεν καίει μέσα του. Η βροχή κυλάει πάνω του χωρίς να βρέχει την πέτρα του.

### Το Νεκρό Χωράφι *(The Burying Field, with Brother Orestis)*
> Ο Αδελφός Ορέστης σας οδηγεί ανάμεσα στους τάφους με το φανάρι του να τρέμει. Σταματά μπροστά σε μια πέτρα που φαίνεται φρέσκια — το χώμα ακόμη μυρίζει σκάψιμο. «Αυτός ο τάφος», ψιθυρίζει, «δεν υπήρχε εχθές. Και δεν ξέρω ποιον θάψαμε.»

### Η Μικρή Ιώ Κρατά την Πυξίδα *(Little Io Holds the Compass)*
> Το κοριτσάκι παίρνει την πυξίδα στα χέρια της σαν να είναι πουλί. Δεν κλαίει. Δεν τρομάζει. Κοιτάζει τον βάρδο για πολλή ώρα, ήσυχα, και μετά λέει, με φωνή που δεν ταιριάζει στην ηλικία της: «Εσύ είσαι ο άντρας που γράφει το γράμμα.» Και μετά: «Μπορώ να δω τη μάνα μου τώρα;»
