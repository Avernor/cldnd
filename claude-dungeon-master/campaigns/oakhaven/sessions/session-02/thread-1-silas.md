# Thread 1 — Σίλας: Ο Άνθρωπος που Ποτέ Δεν Ήταν
*(Sylas: The Man Who Never Was)*

> **Premise**: The party remembers Σίλας Ρέυν as the inn's hard-edged, widowed innkeeper who served them beer two nights ago. Everyone in the village now remembers him as a man who died in a barfight ten years ago. The investigation reveals that Vanya's edit was a **temporal relocation** — not an erasure. She did not just rewrite memory; she killed him to make the lie true.

**Entry**: The party almost certainly pushes this themselves. They remember him. Nobody else does.

---

## NPCs in this thread

### 🔑 Σίλας Ρέυν (Sylas Reyne) — *the edited man*

**Description (read aloud — for flashback memory or grave-side recognition)**:
> Άντρας στα μέσα της πενηντάρας του, φαρδύς στους ώμους, αρχίζει να παχαίνει στη μέση. Σκούρα μαλλιά που πισωγυρίζουν, γκρίζα στους κροτάφους, τριών ημερών αξύριστη γενειάδα. Βαρύ, καιροδαρμένο πρόσωπο με μια μόνιμη γραμμή θυμού ανάμεσα στα φρύδια. Σκληρά γκρίζα μάτια — και πίσω τους, θαμμένη, μια θλίψη που ποτέ δεν επέτρεπε να φανεί. Φορούσε ένα βρώμικο λινό πουκάμισο σηκωμένο μέχρι τους αγκώνες, μια δερμάτινη ποδιά λεκιασμένη με μπύρα και λίπος, απλά μάλλινα παντελόνια, γερές μπότες. Στον λαιμό του, ένα μικρό μπρούντζινο κλειδί κρεμασμένο από δερμάτινο κορδόνι.

**State (this session)**: Dead. Buried in Το Νεκρό Χωράφι. To everyone in the village he died in a tavern brawl ten years ago, stabbed under the ribs after his daughter Κόρη's death broke him. **The party's memory of him alive is the only proof otherwise.** If they dig the grave (Thread 3), they will find his body recently killed, in the same shirt they saw him in, with the cellar key in his pocket.

**What he was, in the original timeline**: A hard, angry, widowed innkeeper. Fair to a fault — gave back more change than he should have. Loved his daughter, who died young. Drank too much. Did not laugh easily. The party drank from his tap two nights ago and exchanged maybe twelve words with him. They never knew he had a daughter.

### Γερο-Αλέξης (Old Alexis) — current innkeeper, ten years and counting

**Description (read aloud)**:
> Ένας γέρος στα τέλη της εξηντάρας του, μικρός και λεπτός. Λευκά μαλλιά σαν φρέσκο χιόνι, μια λεπτή λευκή γενειάδα. Καλόψυχα, υδαρή γαλάζια μάτια. Φοράει μια φαρδιά τουνίκα που κάποτε ήταν λευκή, ένα πλεκτό γιλέκο, ξεφτισμένα μάλλινα παντελόνια. Τα χέρια του τρέμουν λίγο όταν κουβαλάει ποτήρια. Ένα μικρό κηλίδι μελάνι στον αντίχειρά του που δεν θυμάται πώς απέκτησε.

**Voice (read aloud as needed)**: gentle, slightly distant, addresses everyone as *«αγαπητοί μου»*. *«Αχ, καημένε μου.»* / *«Καθίστε. Φάτε.»* / *«Θα σας φέρω άλλη μια.»* / *«Δεν θα πεθάνω σήμερα. Ίσως αύριο.»*

**Goals**: Keep the inn warm. Mourn Σίλας quietly. Survive the winter. He has been the innkeeper for ten years in his memory — since the night of the barfight he was sitting two stools down from when it happened. The Elders' Circle handed him the keys that night because no Ρέυν heir was left.

**Knows**:
- Σίλας died ten years ago in a tavern brawl over a debt. *Believes this completely.*
- His daughter Κόρη died about a year before her father, of a fever.
- He, Αλέξης, was present at the barfight, survived, was given the inn.
- He does not remember the party staying there before tonight (the altered timeline accepts them as "having had a hard night on the road").

**Doesn't know**:
- That Σίλας served the party beer two nights ago.
- That he writes poetry on napkins (he leaves them in a small pile by the bar; they are notably better than the man who writes them).
- Where his cellar key came from. He has been using it for "ten years" without thinking. *It is a duplicate; the original is in Σίλας's pocket in the grave.*

**If pressed hard about Σίλας being alive**: gentle but firm. Becomes worried for the party's health. Suggests they visit Γριά Νεφέλη.

### Γερο-Κέλης (Old Kelis) — stablehand and ledger-keeper

**Description (read aloud)**:
> Γέρος στα τέλη της εξηντάρας του, ψηλός, αδύνατος, καιροδαρμένος. Φαλακρός στο κεφάλι, με λευκά τσουλούφια στις άκρες των αυτιών, λευκή γενειάδα ξεφυτρωμένη. Μακρύ, λεπτό πρόσωπο. Βαθουλωτά γκρίζα μάτια. **Μια πήλινη πίπα μόνιμα στη γωνία του στόματος.** Φοράει ένα φθαρμένο καφέ μάλλινο παλτό πάνω από σκούρο λινό πουκάμισο, λασπωμένες μπότες.

**Voice (read aloud as needed)**: laconic, dry, slow to start and slower to stop once he starts. *«Τι θέλετε;»* / *«Γράφω ό,τι βλέπω. Δεν φταίω εγώ που το χωριό ξεχνάει.»* / *«Δεν εμπιστεύομαι κανέναν που μπαίνει στον στάβλο μου χωρίς λόγο. Έχετε λόγο;»* / *«Αυτό το χέρι... το ξέρω. Και όμως δεν το ξέρω.»*

**Goals**: Keep the horses fed. Watch the road. Write everything down. Be left alone except by people who matter.

**Knows**:
- Forty years of comings and goings, weather, strange events — all in his ledger.
- Every visitor to Δρυόχωρο since he was twenty.
- That his ledger has entries he cannot remember writing — and they are *in his hand.*
- Names: every villager, every regular trader, every name on a stone in the burying field.

**Doesn't know**:
- That a *different hand* — which the party will recognize as his own — has been annotating his ledger with *«διεγράφη»* (erased) beside certain names. He cannot see this hand as different until the party shows him.
- What «διεγράφη» means or who is doing it.

**If trusted**: he will let the party consult the ledger any time. If shown the «διεγράφη» annotations gently, he goes very pale and quiet, then says *«Αυτό το χέρι... το ξέρω. Και όμως δεν το ξέρω.»* He becomes a quiet long-term ally.

### Μαρίνα — shopkeeper, edit casualty

**Description (read aloud)**:
> Γυναίκα στα τέλη της τριάντα της. Στιβαρή, πρακτική, απλή. Καστανόξανθα μαλλιά πιασμένα σε απλό κότσο. Ήρεμα μάτια στο χρώμα του μελιού — με ένα ίχνος κενότητας στο βάθος, μια θλίψη που δεν μπορεί να ονοματίσει. Φοράει ένα καθαρό σκούρο μάλλινο φόρεμα, μια λευκή λινή ποδιά, σκουφάκι. Τα χέρια της κινούνται γρήγορα και ικανά — μετράει νομίσματα χωρίς να κοιτά. Ένα μικρό, λυπημένο χαμόγελο που έρχεται και φεύγει.

**Voice (read aloud as needed)**: even, businesslike, then occasionally drifting. *«Πόσο θέλετε;»* / *«Δεν ξέρω γιατί κρατάω αυτό το πράγμα.»* / *«Δεν ήμουν ποτέ παντρεμένη. Και όμως...»*

**Goals**: Run the shop. Be reliable. Live with the small grief she cannot name.

**Knows**:
- That she has a wedding ring in her drawer she cannot remember acquiring.
- That she has never been married — *she is certain of this.*
- That this contradiction makes her uneasy and she avoids thinking about it.

**Doesn't know**:
- Her husband was Ηλίας Μαρίνου, a mill worker who was «διεγράφη» seven months ago. The thin spot erased him from existence; the ring stayed because edits are not perfect.
- That she has a tenant in her back room (Θάλεια) whose own husband was edited similarly. They live within twenty feet of each other and do not know they share the same wound.

**If treated kindly**: she will show them the ring (Persuasion DC 11). If given the proof of Ηλίας's existence (Κέλης's ledger entry), she will not believe — there is nothing in her mind to believe with — but she will hold the ring differently, and let the party take it to be buried (see Payoff Beat below).

## Avenues of investigation

### A. Ask Γερο-Αλέξης at the inn
**Where**: behind the bar at Το Κούφιο Ελάφι. He's there from morning to night.

He remembers Σίλας *clearly* — just not the way the party does. He is not lying.
- *«Ο Σίλας; Ο Θεός να τον συχωρέσει. Δέκα χρόνια τώρα. Ήμουν εδώ εκείνη τη νύχτα — καθόμουν δυο σκαμνιά μακριά. Ο καβγάς άρχισε απ' το τίποτα, όπως αρχίζουν όλοι οι κακοί καβγάδες. Ένα μαχαίρι, κι ο Σίλας στο πάτωμα, και κανένας Ρέυν να κληρονομήσει τον πάγκο. Η Γερόντισσα Εστία μου έδωσε τα κλειδιά το ίδιο βράδυ. Γιατί με ρωτάτε τώρα, μετά από τόσα χρόνια;»*
- **Insight (DC 14)**: his memory is *sincere*. He is genuinely grieving a man he watched die a decade ago. Something is wrong with reality, not with him.
- If the party insist that Σίλας served them beer two nights ago, he is gentle but firm, and becomes worried for their health — suggests they see **Γριά Νεφέλη**.
- **If the party later find his napkin poetry** (Perception DC 13 on a stack of napkins by the bar): one line, in handwriting that isn't his — *«Ο πάγκος ήταν δικός μου.»* (The bar was mine.) Γερο-Αλέξης stares at it and cannot explain it. **Save this napkin** — it's a take-home keepsake of the edit.

### B. Ask regulars or passersby at the inn
**Where**: at any time of day at Το Κούφιο Ελάφι — see the inn's "who is here" schedule in [village-atlas.md](./village-atlas.md).

Everyone remembers a Σίλας — but a dead one. The stories almost agree, and the small disagreements are the interesting part.
- One old man at a corner table: *«Τον Σίλας τον έθαψαν πριν δέκα χρόνια. Τον μαχαίρωσε ένας ξυλοκόπος απ' τον Μύλο σε καβγά για ένα χρέος.»*
- A mill hand at the next bench frowns: *«Όχι, δεν ήταν ξυλοκόπος. Ήταν περαστικός. Κανένας δεν τον ήξερε. Έφυγε την ίδια νύχτα.»*
- An old woman knitting: *«Το φτωχό το κορίτσι του είχε πεθάνει πρώτα. Αυτό τον σκότωσε, όχι το μαχαίρι. Το μαχαίρι ήρθε μετά.»*
- **Insight DC 13** on listening to them together: they all *believe* they remember, but no two stories match on the attacker, the weapon, or the year before — only on *Σίλας dead, ten years, a barfight, his daughter gone first.* The core of the edit is solid; the details are the land scabbing over.
- If the party insists they drank from Σίλας's tap two nights ago, one old woman crosses herself and mutters *«Το Δρυόχωρο θυμάται τους δικούς του. Δεν ξέρω τι θυμάστε εσείς.»*

### C. Check Γερο-Κέλης's ledger at Ο Στάβλος

**This is the high-payoff route.** Γερο-Κέλης is taciturn but has the best memory in the village — because he writes everything down.

**Where**: Κέλης's stable, on the south road end of the village. He is there sitting on his stool from dawn to dusk.

**Read-aloud (Greek) — entering the stable**:
> Ο Στάβλος μυρίζει άλογο, άχυρο και καπνισμένη πίπα. Ο Γερο-Κέλης κάθεται σε ένα σκαμνί δίπλα στην πόρτα και καπνίζει χωρίς να σηκώσει το βλέμμα όταν μπαίνετε. Έχει στα γόνατά του ένα χοντρό, δερμάτινο βιβλίο με φθαρμένα φύλλα. «Τι θέλετε;»

**The investigation**:
- **Persuasion DC 12** (or DC 10 if they are honest about *why* — *«θέλουμε να ξέρουμε αν κάποιος ονόματι Σίλας υπήρξε εδώ»*) gets him to let them read the ledger.
- **Investigation DC 13** in the ledger reveals an entry four autumns ago: *«Σίλας Ρέυν, αγόρασε χόρτο για το άλογό του. 3 χαλκοί.»* The entry is plain, unannotated, in Κέλης's own undisputed hand. **The paradox**: the whole village insists Σίλας died ten years ago. Κέλης's own ledger has him alive and buying hay *four* years ago. Κέλης stares at it and cannot explain it. *«Εγώ το έγραψα. Αυτό είναι το χέρι μου. Και όμως ο άνθρωπος ήταν στο χώμα δέκα χρόνια πριν το γράψω αυτό.»* He reads it three times, closes the ledger, opens it again to check the page has not moved.
- **Further search (another Investigation DC 13)** reveals three more suspicious entries across the past eighteen months. Each one is a name annotated *«διεγράφη»* in a different hand — a hand Κέλης cannot identify but the party recognizes instantly as *his own*:
  1. *«Ηλίας Μαρίνου»* (7 months ago) — *Μαρίνα's erased husband*
  2. *«Μάριος Λιβέρης»* (14 months ago) — *one of the graves in [thread-3-graves-orestis.md](./thread-3-graves-orestis.md)*
  3. *«[όνομα ξεθωριασμένο, αδιάβαστο]»* (2 months ago) — *deliberately unknown; a hook for later sessions*

- **The Σίλας entry is a different *kind* of edit** from the three «διεγράφη» ones. The three were people *removed.* Σίλας was a person *moved in time.* If any player notices and says so aloud, **reward them** — this is the beginning of understanding Vanya's craft has more than one tool. (Inspiration.)

- **🌱 Conductor seed (recommended deeper search)**: if the party reads further (Investigation DC 14, or prompted by Κέλης himself once he trusts them), an entry from his grandfather's hand, maybe forty years old:
  > *«Ήρθε ένας ξένος απ' την Καίλεν. Αγόρασε δύο μέτρα ασημένιο σύρμα κι έναν γυάλινο χωνοειδή σωλήνα με μόλυβδο στη βάση. Πλήρωσε σε ασήμι. Δεν ρώτησα τι τα ήθελε. Αριστόδημος τ' όνομά του.»*

  The party will not know yet what this means. **DM-only**: Αριστόδημος was buying parts for a replacement *conductor* — the lost second instrument of the Circle of Five. This pays off in Session 3 at the obsidian tower.

- **Γερο-Κέλης, staring at his own ledger with entries he did not write**, turns very pale and says nothing for a long moment. Then: *«Αυτό το χέρι... το ξέρω. Και όμως δεν το ξέρω.»* If the party explains kindly, he becomes their quiet ally. He will let them consult the ledger whenever they need.

### D. Visit Το Νεκρό Χωράφι looking for a grave

**Where**: behind Το Λεπτό Παρεκκλήσι. See [village-atlas.md](./village-atlas.md) for the full description of the field.

**The grave is there.** *«Σίλας Ρέυν — αναπαύθηκε εδώ και δέκα χειμώνες.»* The stone is weathered, moss on its shoulders, the earth around it long grown over. By every outward sign it has been there a decade.

**Beside it**, a smaller, older stone: **Κόρη Ρέυν**, age 9, died about a year before her father.

If the party stands at these two stones and understands what they are looking at, **that is the emotional core of the session** — the edit is not a gap, it is a *displacement*, and the village grieved a man who was serving them beer two nights ago.

**Cross-pollinates with [thread-3-graves-orestis.md](./thread-3-graves-orestis.md)** — Αδελφός Ορέστης may be in the chapel and will accompany them to the field if they ask. The full physical investigation (lichen mismatch, "if the party digs" contingency) is in Thread 3.

### E. Confront Μαρίνα at Τα Είδη της Μαρίνας

**Where**: Marina's general goods shop. She is there from dawn to dusk.

She does not know Σίλας. She is also uneasy for reasons she cannot explain.

If the party is kind (**Persuasion DC 11**), she mentions that she has a wedding ring in her drawer she cannot remember putting there. She has never been married. **Cross-pollinates with the ledger** (Ηλίας Μαρίνου — her erased husband).

#### 🌟 Payoff beat — the small memorial

If the party returns to her later with the ledger entry (*«Ηλίας Μαρίνου — διεγράφη»*) and speaks gently, she will not fully believe them — there is nothing in her mind to believe with — but she will hold the ring differently. If a PC offers to take it to Αδελφός Ορέστης and have it buried in Το Νεκρό Χωράφι in a small unmarked grave, she lets them. Do not push her to cry; she cannot mourn someone she does not remember. She simply says *«Αν τον ξέρατε… ευχαριστώ.»*

Orestis, if told, will quietly dig the hole himself. No ceremony. The party has just buried a man who was never born, in a village that does not remember him, beside a stone they will not carve. **This is the session's second grace-note alongside Thread 7.** Award inspiration to whichever PC led it.

**Θάλεια** (see [thread-7-aftiakto.md](./thread-7-aftiakto.md)), who lives in a back room of Μαρίνα's shop, watches from a doorway without knowing why she is watching. She will dream about the moment for weeks. Do not mention her by name to the players unless they have already met her in Thread 7.

## Payoff

- **Concrete proof Σίλας was edited, not forgotten** — and evidence that the edit is of a *different kind* from the «διεγράφη» removals. Vanya has more than one tool.
- Four known recent edits in the village: Σίλας (relocated), Ηλίας Μαρίνου, Μάριος Λιβέρης, and the unreadable name. A *pattern* with a telling inconsistency.
- Γερο-Κέλης becomes a repeatable investigation resource.
- Μαρίνα cross-link established; possible memorial grace note.
- The bard's napkin from Γερο-Αλέξης (a take-home object: *«Ο πάγκος ήταν δικός μου.»*).
- The conductor seed planted, payable in Session 3.

## Consequence if ignored

Memory of Σίλας slowly fades for whichever PC spends the most time outside the compass's 10 ft radius — small details first (his voice, the color of his eyes, what he said to them on Night 1). The bearer holds onto every detail. Non-bearer PCs who walk apart from the bearer for hours at a time lose the most. Reversible by deliberate remembering at the bearer's side. A quiet horror beat, not a punishment.
