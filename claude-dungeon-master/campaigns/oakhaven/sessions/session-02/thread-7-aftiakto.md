# Thread 7 — Το Πρώτο Άφτιαχτο
*(The First Unmade)*

> **Premise**: An Άφτιαχτο — a remnant of someone Vanya erased, half-real and wandering — appears at the edge of the Oldwood at dusk. It does not attack. It *waits*. The party has three resolution paths: combat (signature monster debut), the grace note (the campaign's signature emotional beat), or running. If the grace note pays off, the creature names itself **Άνδρος** and asks the party to deliver a message to his wife — who lives in Marina's back room and does not remember he ever existed.

**Entry triggers** — any one of these activates it:

1. The party enters the Oldwood (hunting, seeking Ο Κόρακας deeper, chasing a rumor)
2. The party sleeps at the edge of the village with the compass actively out
3. **The dusk trigger** *(strongly recommended)*: At the end of the session's afternoon, as the light begins to fail, one of the party looks toward the treeline and sees *something.*

**Why dusk trigger**: horror works best when you control the lighting. It also lets the encounter close the session at a time of relative safety, which fits the soft-close Scene 2 framing.

## NPCs in this thread

### 🔑 Το Άφτιαχτο (The Unmade — once a man named Άνδρος)

**What it is**: When Vanya's edits cut someone out of existence, and the compass-field at the moment of the edit is *almost but not quite* strong enough to hold them, the edit leaves a **remnant** — a half-real thing wandering Ο Παλαιόδρυμος trying to remember what it was. **This Άφτιαχτο was once Άνδρος, a mill worker in his thirties with a wife named Θάλεια.** He was edited several months ago. He has been in the woods since.

**Description in its current Άφτιαχτο form (read aloud)**:
> Μια ανθρώπινη μορφή, θολή στις άκρες, σαν να την βλέπετε μέσα από παλιό, παραμορφωμένο γυαλί. **Πρόσωπο ασχημάτιστο** — η υπόνοια ματιών, ένα στόμα που αλλάζει σαν υγρός πηλός που προσπαθεί να θυμηθεί ένα σχήμα. Δεν προχωράει προς εσάς. Περιμένει. Σαν να σας περίμενε.
>
> Στέκεται γύρω στα πενήντα μέτρα μακριά, ανάμεσα σε δύο πεύκα στην άκρη του δάσους.

- **Insight DC 13**: it is not hostile. It is *asking* for something.

**Voice (when it speaks — Path B)**: **a borrowed voice the party recognizes** — the voice of a passerby seen yesterday at the inn, the voice of a logger who greeted them on the road, the voice of someone the party half-knows. The voice is wrong because the wrong throat is using it.
- *«Τι... ήμουνα... εγώ;»* (the first thing it says, if approached for speech)
- *«Με έλεγαν... Άνδρο. Νομίζω.»* (if the grace note completes)
- *«Πες στη γυναίκα μου — αν μπορέσεις να τη βρεις — πες της ότι δεν ήταν λάθος της.»* (its last words, if it remembers)

**Goal (in its current state)**: figure out what it was. Find a face it can wear. Stop being half.

### 🔑 Άνδρος (Andros) — *the man he was*

**Description if he is restored briefly during Path B (read aloud)**:
> Το πρόσωπο σταθεροποιείται. Είναι ένας άντρας στη μέση της ζωής του — ψηλός, λεπτός, με μια μικρή ουλή πάνω απ' το ένα φρύδι, παλιό σημάδι από ξύλινη πινακίδα που έπεσε. Ντυμένος με ρούχα ξυλοκόπου, μάλλινα και δερμάτινα. Τα μάτια του είναι κουρασμένα και ευγενικά. Σας κοιτάζει σαν να σας ζητάει συγνώμη που υπάρχει.

**Who he was**: a mill worker in his thirties. Married to Θάλεια. They had no children. They worked. They were quiet. He was kind to the foreman's dog. He drank one beer on Sundays. **A small life, well-loved.** Vanya erased him for a test edit several months ago. The thin spot left a remnant.

If the party speaks to him kindly enough that he remembers (Path B with restoration), this is the man they will meet for one minute before he fades.

### 🔑 Θάλεια (Thalia) — Άνδρος's forgotten wife

> ⚠ **Do not introduce Θάλεια to the players unless the grace note pays off.** She is the seed for what the party does *with* the message Άνδρος gives them. If they don't get the message, they will not encounter her this session. (She lives in Marina's back room and is mentioned in the [village-atlas.md](./village-atlas.md) and [thread-1-silas.md](./thread-1-silas.md) memorial beat without being named to the players.)

**Description (read aloud)**:
> Γυναίκα στα μέσα της τριάντα της. Λεπτή, παρατηρητική, με μια θλίψη που δεν ξέρει να εξηγήσει. Κοκκινοκάστανα μαλλιά πιασμένα σε χαμηλό κότσο. Χλωμό δέρμα με φακίδες. **Πράσινα μάτια λίγο πιο ορθάνοιχτα απ' το κανονικό**, σαν να είναι πάντα μισοαιφνιδιασμένη. Φοράει ένα καφέ μάλλινο φόρεμα με μακριά ποδιά, σκουφάκι, γερές μπότες. Τα χέρια της κόκκινα κι ωμά απ' τη συνεχή πλύση ρούχων. Στη ζώνη της κρέμεται από κορδόνι ένα μικρό σκαλισμένο ξύλινο κύπελλο — δεν θυμάται γιατί. Συχνά φοράει κάτω απ' το φόρεμά της ένα φαρδύ ανδρικό μάλλινο πουκάμισο για ζεστασιά, που της κάθεται άσχημα. Τον λέει «του πατέρα μου». Δεν είναι.

**Voice (read aloud as needed)**: low, polite, a beat slower than other people's. Often pauses mid-sentence to look at the floor.
- *«Συγγνώμη — έχω δουλειά. Τι χρειάζεστε;»*
- *«Όχι, δεν υπήρξε ποτέ άντρας μου. Γιατί ρωτάτε;»*
- *(if shown the wool shirt of his pulled from her drawer)*: *«Το ξέρετε αυτό; Πώς το ξέρετε αυτό;»*

**Goals (this session, even if she does not appear)**: do her work. Sleep without dreaming of the man she does not know. Survive.

**Knows**: nothing. She does not know she is missing him. She has dreamed of him for months and does not know who he is. She wakes crying and cannot explain why.

**Tells the party can find** (if they later look for her — likely Session 3, maybe end of Session 2 if everything aligns):
- She flinches whenever someone says the name *Άνδρος* casually, then apologizes and says she doesn't know why.
- She has a single man's wool shirt folded in her chest of drawers that is the wrong size for her, that she has never been able to throw away.
- She drinks her tea from a chipped wooden cup carved with a symbol she cannot read — a lumberjack's guild mark.
- Μαρίνα notices but does not ask.

**If the party brings her the message**: she will not believe at first. There is nothing in her mind to believe with. **The wool shirt is what will break her.** Show her the shirt and tell her her dead husband sent his apology. Let the moment land. **Do not push her to cry.** *(This is likely a Session 3 payoff, not Session 2 — but if the party rushes there at end of session, you can play it.)*

## Branches — three paths

### Path A — Attack (combat)

If the party attacks on sight, the Άφτιαχτο does not defend itself for one full round. **It just *looks* at them.** Any PC who strikes it in that round feels, for a heartbeat, a memory of *their own life* flicker and almost vanish — **DC 12 Wis save**, failure means they forget something small for the next hour (their childhood home's color, their mother's middle name — DM's choice). Reversible.

Then the creature defends itself.

#### Stat block (CR 2, level-3 party of three: Medium-leaning-Hard)

- **AC** 13, **HP** 45, **Speed** 30 ft
- **Multiattack**: two Touch of the Unmade attacks per turn
- **Touch of the Unmade** (melee, +5 to hit, reach 5 ft): 1d8+2 psychic damage and target makes a **DC 13 Wisdom save** or takes 1 point of Wisdom drain (recovers on long rest)
- **Unmaking Gaze** (recharge 5–6, 1/combat): one creature within 30 ft makes a **DC 13 Wisdom save** or is frightened until the end of its next turn and loses one memory (see Path A text above for what kind)
- **Resistances**: non-magical bludgeoning/piercing/slashing; psychic
- **Vulnerabilities**: **radiant damage (double)** — Divine Smite, radiant cantrips, Sacred Flame, Guiding Bolt
- **Compass aura**: within 10 ft of the compass, the Άφτιαχτο has disadvantage on all rolls and **cannot flee**
- **Iron nail**: a thrown nail within 10 ft gives disadvantage on its next attack (uses one of Rook's nails)

**Encounter math**: CR 2 ≈ 450 XP; for three PCs of level 3 this sits at Medium (450 threshold). The compass aura and radiant vulnerability give the party real tools — reward engagement. Without them, bump HP to 55 or call it Hard.

#### Tactics

It does not behave like a monster. It moves *toward the bearer of the compass*, slowly, as if drawn — the compass is the closest thing it has felt to *being remembered*. It will pass an unarmed PC to reach the bearer if forced to choose. Its Touch attacks aim **for the face** whenever possible — it does not understand that is monstrous; it is trying to learn what a face is. If radiant damage hits it, it flinches and pauses one round before attacking again — this is the closest it will come to fear. **It does not flee even at low HP** (the compass aura locks it). At ~10 HP it begins to lose coherence visually — its outline thins, its borrowed voice cracks. **Last-round opportunity for the grace note**: if a PC speaks to it kindly *as it is dying*, even mid-combat, you may switch to Path B in the moment. Reward this.

It fights to "death." On "death" it dissolves into fine gray ash that smells faintly of pine smoke and old rain. The party has killed a man who never quite was.

### Path B — Speak (the grace note, strongly encouraged)

#### Read-aloud (Greek)

> Το πράγμα ανοίγει το στόμα του. Κάτι προσπαθεί να βγει — ένας ήχος που δεν είναι λέξη, που δεν θυμάται πώς να γίνει λέξη. Μετά, με μια φωνή δανεισμένη που είναι τρομαχτικά *γνώριμη* — η φωνή ενός περαστικού που είδατε χθες στο πανδοχείο, η φωνή ενός ξυλοκόπου που σας χαιρέτησε στο δρόμο — σας ρωτάει:
>
> *«Τι... ήμουνα... εγώ;»*

#### If the party answers with honesty and compassion — even a guess — the thing *changes*

Its face resolves briefly. It remembers. Suggested dialogue from the party:
- *«Δεν ξέρουμε ποιος ήσουν. Αλλά κάποτε ήσουν κάποιος. Κάποιος σε αγαπούσε.»* → the thing trembles.
- *«Ίσως ήσουν ξυλοκόπος. Ίσως δούλευες στον μύλο.»* → its face resolves into the face of a middle-aged man with a scar over one eyebrow. Its mouth opens. *«Ναι. Ναι, αυτό ήμουν.»*

**The truth rule from Ο Κόρακας** (Thread 6) is what tells the party this is the path. Without Thread 6, a Wisdom (Insight) DC 15 in the moment may give a PC the same instinct.

#### If additionally a PC uses Lay on Hands, Healing Word, Bardic Inspiration, or any kind touch while speaking to it

The restoration is complete for **one full minute**. The man thanks them in a voice they almost know. He tells them his name — *«Με έλεγαν... Άνδρο. Νομίζω.»* He remembers his wife. He weeps, once. Then he fades.

#### Read-aloud (Greek) — the grace-note ending

> Ο Άνδρος σας κοιτάζει με μάτια που είναι για μια στιγμή καθαρά. «Ευχαριστώ», λέει. «Νομίζω ότι... νομίζω ότι θυμάμαι. Πες στη γυναίκα μου — αν μπορέσεις να τη βρεις — πες της ότι δεν ήταν λάθος της.» Και μετά, σιγά σιγά, σαν ομίχλη στο πρώτο φως, ξεθωριάζει.

**🌟 Give the player who leads this beat inspiration.** This is one of the best moments available in Part 1, and whichever PC plays it well has *earned* a campaign-level character beat.

**Note for later**: **Άνδρος had a wife.** She is in the village. She does not remember him. **This is a seed for Session 3 or 4** — see the Θάλεια NPC profile above. Do not name Θάλεια to the players in this session. Let them carry the message and discover its destination.

### Path C — Run

The thing does not pursue. But one PC (the one holding the compass *least*, or randomly) feels something briefly missing the next morning — a favorite color, a childhood song, a scar they don't remember getting or not getting. Reversible by deliberate remembering over time, or by reclaiming a similar moment.

**No combat XP.** No item drop. The grace note is forfeited; the next encounter (Session 3) will not be this tender.

## What the party sees beyond the encounter

If they look past the Άφτιαχτο into the trees behind it: nothing. It is alone tonight. Tomorrow night, two might be there. The Oldwood is filling up quietly. **They will not see this in Session 2.** Foreshadow with a single beat: as the encounter ends, the bard's PC (or the PC with highest Wisdom) hears, far back in the trees, *another* attempt at a borrowed voice — a single syllable that was almost a word — and then silence.

## Payoff

- **Signature monster debut.** The party knows what an Άφτιαχτο is now, in their bones, not just from Ο Κόρακας's description.
- Players learn the Άφτιαχτα's mechanics through play — the compass aura, radiant vulnerability, iron nails.
- **The compass's effect on them is revealed in action.**
- **The grace note** — a moment of genuine dark-fantasy beauty. Worth more than any XP.
- **A seed**: Άνδρος's wife Θάλεια, somewhere in the village. The party carries his message.
- One named edit-victim with a face and a story — humanizes Vanya's craft.

## Consequence if not triggered

- The Oldwood's population of Άφτιαχτα grows quietly.
- **Next session**, they are bolder: three of them at the treeline at dawn, and the party is no longer facing a solo encounter they can handle with roleplay. **Combat becomes necessary.**
- The grace note is *not available* with a group of three. **The first time the party sees an Άφτιαχτο in grief is the only time it is this tender.**
