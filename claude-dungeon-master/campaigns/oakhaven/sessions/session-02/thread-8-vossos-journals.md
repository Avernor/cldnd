# Thread 8 — Τα Ημερολόγια του Βόσσου
*(The Vossos Journals)*

> **Premise**: Κάλχας Βόσσος, the mill owner, inherited three thick journals from his grandfather. He has read parts of them. He does not like what is in them. The journals record **ninety years** of "Quiet Days" — moments when the village felt wrong — and the *frequency is accelerating sharply*. Buried in them: a mention of **Αριστόδημος Κρέων**. Tucked in the back cover: a single warning sheet in an unknown hand. **The chest has recently been disturbed by someone who is not Κάλχας.**

**Entry**: This is the **hardest thread to reach.** Three ways in. The party must pursue this themselves — Κάλχας will not volunteer.

## NPCs in this thread

### 🔑 Κάλχας Βόσσος — mill owner, conservative, watchful

**Description (read aloud)**:
> Άντρας στα σαράντα του, μεγάλος και σιωπηλός, φαρδύς στους ώμους και στο στήθος, αρχίζει να μαλακώνει στη μέση. Σκούρα μαλλιά και πυκνή σκούρα γενειάδα, και τα δύο κουρεμένα προσεκτικά. Σκούρα μάτια, παρατηρητικά — αργά στις κινήσεις, γρήγορα στον υπολογισμό. Φοράει ένα βαρύ δερμάτινο παλτό πάνω από μάλλινη χιτώνα, σκούρα παντελόνια, καλοφτιαγμένες μπότες. **Στο μεσαίο δάχτυλο του αριστερού του χεριού, ένα δαχτυλίδι σφραγιδοφόρο.** **Όταν στέκει ακίνητος, τα χέρια του είναι πάντα σταυρωμένα.** Ήρεμη φωνή, καμία περιττή χειρονομία. Είναι ο τύπος του ανθρώπου που οι άλλοι σέβονται ενστικτωδώς χωρίς να ξέρουν γιατί.

**Voice (read aloud as needed)**: low, deliberate, no padding. Will let silence sit longer than is comfortable. Says exactly what he means.
- *«Τι θέλετε;»*
- *«Δεν είμαι σίγουρος ότι σας πιστεύω. Αλλά συνεχίστε.»*
- *«Ο πατέρας μου δεν θα ήθελε να σας τα δώσω. Αλλά ο πατέρας μου είναι νεκρός και η Μίρα ζει. Πάρτε τα.»*
- *«Μία νύχτα. Και μετά τα θέλω πίσω. Δεν συζητείται.»*

**Goals**:
- Keep the mill running. The valley depends on it.
- Keep his sister Αλκμήνη and his cousin Μίρα safe.
- Avoid attention from Καίλεν.
- Pretend the village's strangeness is not happening, because admitting it will not help him saw lumber.

**Knows**:
- His grandfather kept journals, three thick volumes, locked in a cedar chest in his upstairs study.
- He has read parts of them. He stopped because they unsettled him.
- That his grandfather called certain days "wrong" and marked them with a small circled X.
- That he, Κάλχας, has felt some of those days himself but does not say so.
- His sister Αλκμήνη does most of the village's iron work. His cousin Μίρα is a sharp judge of character. He trusts both more than he trusts most.

**Doesn't know** (and the party should not tell him in Session 2):
- The journals' deeper pattern (the acceleration).
- That **Αριστόδημος Κρέων** appears by name in his grandfather's hand.
- That the last ten pages of the third journal have been **torn out recently** by someone who broke into his chest.
- That a warning sheet in unfamiliar handwriting is tucked inside the back cover.
- That a thin spot lies under his village.

**If pressed for what he thinks of the village's strangeness**: long silence. Then: *«Σταθερότητα. Αυτό ζητώ. Όχι αλήθεια. Αλήθεια δεν τρώγεται. Σταθερότητα τρώγεται. Αν θέλετε να με βοηθήσετε, μη χαλάτε τη σταθερότητα.»* He will not be moved on this — but he respects the party for asking honestly.

**He has Mira's note** — if she sent one. He reads it slowly. Twice. Then he changes posture.

### Αλκμήνη Βόσσου — Κάλχας's sister, smith, available but not central this thread

**Physical and voice**: see [village-atlas.md](./village-atlas.md) → "Important NPCs."

**Role in this thread**: she is at the forge during the day. **If the party tries to approach Κάλχας through Αλκμήνη**, she will hear them out (low DC, Persuasion 10, or just being polite). She will not directly press her brother for the journals — *«Δεν είμαι ο φύλακας του»* — but she will tell them honestly: *«Αν η Μίρα σας έστειλε, πηγαίνετε. Άλλο τίποτα. Αν όχι, μην προσπαθείτε. Δεν θα ακούσει.»*

If the fighter shows her the heirloom sword (a separate thread, not Session 2 critical), she recognizes the forging style. *«Αυτό είναι παλιά δουλειά. Παλαιότερη απ' το χωριό.»* Save this beat for Session 3 unless a player initiates it organically.

### Two foremen at the mill (background, only relevant for Path C)

- **Ορίωνας** — Κάλχας's right hand. Tall, thick-armed, careful. Sleeps in an outbuilding by the mill at night. Light sleeper.
- **Δρόσος** — younger foreman, restless. Patrols the mill yard until midnight then naps.

**Stats if combat happens** (it should not, but in case): use **Guards** from MM/SRD — AC 16, HP 11, +3 to hit, 1d6+1 club. **Non-lethal** — they will club and hold intruders, not kill them. Κάλχας has been clear with his men about this.

## Three paths in

### Path A — Earn Κάλχας's trust directly

The party visits the mill, asks about the village's history, engages Κάλχας as a person rather than as a resource. He is suspicious, watchful, calculating — an outsider-wary man protecting the engine of his village.

**Where**: the mill yard during the day. He is there from dawn to dusk. His upstairs study is private; daytime visits happen at the loading dock or in his small office attached to the mill.

#### Read-aloud (Greek) — first meeting at the mill

> Ο Κάλχας Βόσσος στέκεται στην αυλή του μύλου με τα χέρια σταυρωμένα. Είναι ένας μεγάλος άντρας, αργός στις κινήσεις αλλά γρήγορος στα μάτια. «Οι ξένοι που έσωσαν τον Θωμά», λέει ήρεμα. «Εντάξει. Τι θέλετε;»

#### DCs

- **Persuasion DC 16** to get him to speak about his family or his grandfather cold.
- **Persuasion DC 13** if they have a genuine in-world reason (*«πιστεύουμε ότι κάτι δεν πάει καλά στο χωριό και θέλουμε να βοηθήσουμε»*).
- If they try to bribe him: **automatic fail.** *«Δεν εξαγοράζομαι. Φύγετε.»* He will not be bought.

### Path B — Μίρα vouches *(recommended, if Thread 2 went well)*

If the party helped Θωμάς with Thread 2 — **actually helped, not just showed up** — then Μίρα will, on her own initiative, send them to Κάλχας with a small handwritten note:

> *«Εμπιστέψου τους. Η Μίρα.»*

Κάλχας is Μίρα's cousin by marriage (her mother and his father were siblings). They are not close, but the blood is real, and Κάλχας respects her judgment.

This drops the DC to **11** (or no check if they roleplay it well).

#### Read-aloud (Greek) — Κάλχας with the note

> Ο Κάλχας διαβάζει το σημείωμα. Το ξαναδιαβάζει. Το διπλώνει με προσοχή και το βάζει στην εσωτερική τσέπη του παλτού του. «Η Μίρα δεν εμπιστεύεται εύκολα», λέει αργά. «Καλώς. Ελάτε μέσα.»

He takes them upstairs to his study.

### Path C — Steal them

The mill house is Κάλχας's private residence attached to the mill. **His study is on the second floor.** Two foremen work nearby during the day; one sleeps in an outbuilding at night.

#### DCs

- **Stealth DC 15** to enter the house unseen
- **Sleight of Hand DC 14** to pick the study's door
- **Sleight of Hand DC 16** to pick the cedar chest itself
- **Failure cascade**: If caught, Κάλχας turns the foremen loose (non-lethal — they'll club and hold the intruders). Κάλχας closes the mill's doors to the party permanently. Word reaches the village by evening. **Μίρα is horrified if she learns. Thread 2 may break depending on timing.**

#### What's where

- **Κάλχας's study (read aloud if they enter)**:
  > Στον δεύτερο όροφο της οικίας του μύλου, μέσω εσωτερικής σκάλας απ' την κουζίνα. Η κουζίνα έχει ένα μικρό αναμμένο τζάκι και κάποιες φορές την κατοικεί μια νεαρή οικονόμος, **η Ρένα** (στις αρχές της εικοσαετίας, ελαφρύς ύπνος, θα ουρλιάξει). Το γραφείο μυρίζει καπνό πούρου και παλιό χαρτί. Έχει ένα βαρύ ξύλινο τραπέζι, δύο καρέκλες, ράφια με μυλικά αρχεία, ένα μικρό τζάκι, και κάτω απ' το παράθυρο, το **κέδρινο σεντούκι**.
- The chest has a simple lock (DC 16) and contains the three journals plus some old family papers (deed to the mill, his father's will, etc.).

## What the journals contain

Ninety years of spotty entries from Κάλχας's grandfather (whose name was **Αρίστιππος Βόσσος** — never spoken aloud in this session, but available if asked). Weather. Lumber prices. Family notes. And, **interleaved, descriptions of Ήσυχες Μέρες** that he witnessed and recorded.

Key findings, in order of difficulty to piece together:

### 1. Easy (reading the first journal)
The grandfather used a small **circled X** in the margin to mark any day that "felt wrong." There are dozens of them across the three volumes.

### 2. Intelligence (Investigation) DC 14 — the acceleration
Charting the circled X's across all three journals reveals a **pattern of acceleration**:
- Earliest journal (~90 years ago): Quiet Days every **three years** on average.
- Middle journal: every **year**.
- Current journal's most recent entries (~15 years ago): every **three or four months**.

**The thin spot is bleeding faster.** Three generations of evidence. *This is what is wrong with Δρυόχωρο, in a single chart.*

### 3. Intelligence (History) DC 13 — Αριστόδημος appears
Buried in one entry from roughly 30 years ago:
> *«Ήρθε ένας νέος άντρας από την Καίλεν σήμερα. Αριστόδημος τ' όνομά του. Ρώτησε καλές ερωτήσεις. Εξαφανίστηκε πριν μπορέσει να απαντήσει καμία.»*

**Cross-references with [thread-5-io-estia.md](./thread-5-io-estia.md) → Εστία's Αριστόδημος memory.** Two independent sources. The bridge to Καίλεν is now confirmed historical, not speculative.

### 4. Investigation DC 13 — the recent tear
The **last ten or so pages of the third journal have been torn out.** The tear is recent — the paper edge is clean, not yellowed. **Κάλχας did not do this.** Someone has been in this chest within the last month.

If the party tells Κάλχας: he goes very still. Then he opens the chest himself, examines the lock with a light touch, and curses quietly. *«Ξέρω ποιος είχε αντίγραφο του κλειδιού. Η μάνα μου. Πέθανε πριν τρία χρόνια. Δεν δίνει σε άλλον.»* He will not say more this session. **Investigation thread for Session 3+.**

### 5. Hidden (Investigation DC 16, or a lucky player check) — the warning sheet
Tucked inside the back cover of the third journal, a single loose sheet of paper in a completely different hand, written recently:
> *«Μην ψάξετε την ένατη πέτρα. Θα σας βρει εκείνη.»*

(*"Do not search for the ninth stone. She will find you."*)

Unsigned. The handwriting is unfamiliar — it does not match Αρίστιππος, Κάλχας, or anyone the party has met. **Whoever left this was warning someone — maybe the grandfather, maybe someone who came after him, maybe the party themselves.**

The "she" reads as feminine — consistent with the ninth Watcher being *«αυτή που κρύβει τον εαυτό της»* (she who hides herself), though the party will not yet have the framing to confirm this unless they have done [thread-5-io-estia.md](./thread-5-io-estia.md) deeply enough that Εστία's translation has surfaced.

## Payoff

- **Historical depth**: The thin spot has been bleeding for at least three generations. This isn't new.
- **Acceleration**: It is getting worse. Fast. The Vossos data is the *quantitative* anchor for the qualitative weirdness the party has been feeling all session.
- **Αριστόδημος Κρέων confirmed** as a historical name (cross-references with Thread 5 if the party hit both).
- **New mystery**: Someone — not Κάλχας, not anyone living the party knows — is *actively interfering* with the Βόσσου family's records. A second fragment? A current villager complicit? The real Vanya? A third party the DM has not introduced yet? *(DM-only: this is Session 3+ content.)*
- **The loose sheet** — an unknown ally, or an unknown enemy, warning someone off the ninth stone.
- **Κάλχας as a guarded ally** — he will not be a friend, but he will not close the door if the party returns the journals on time and treats his trust with respect.

## Consequence if ignored

- Κάλχας does not think about the journals again. He is busy.
- **Next session**: if the party finally goes for them, the chest is *empty*. The journals are gone. Κάλχας swears on his father's grave he did not touch them. Someone — whoever has been interfering — has taken them. **They are lost permanently as a resource.**
- This is the thread with the **highest permanent-loss stakes**. Players who skip it lose a whole vein of lore.
