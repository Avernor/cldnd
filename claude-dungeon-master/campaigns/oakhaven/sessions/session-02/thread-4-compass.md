# Thread 4 — Η Πυξίδα Ξυπνά
*(The Compass Awakens — passive observation, always on)*

> **Premise**: The compass is not just a protective anchor — it is also an **instrument**. It reacts to people, places, and moments throughout the day, and a paying-attention party can use it to map out the thin spot's wounds in the village. This thread runs in the background of every other thread; it is a *reward for attention,* not a separate scene.

**Entry**: The compass acts throughout the day. No specific scene — weave it into whichever other threads the party pursues.

**🚨 Bearer rule**: this entire thread requires the compass to be on a PC's person, not in a backpack. **See [README.md](./README.md) → "The compass rule" for the full mechanic.** A compass stowed in a pack ticks to no one and warms an empty canvas — the whole thread goes dark and the party gets a memory-fade cost instead. Let the loss be felt.

## DM beats

- **Reward players who carry the compass deliberately.** If one PC holds it all session, make a point of mentioning its behavior each scene.
- **Do not narrate what the reactions mean.** Let them draw the inferences. The compass is a *language* the party is learning to read.
- **Passive Perception 13** catches the reactions without a check. Below 13, only narrate when they look at it (or when the reaction is dramatic — the cold spell at Σίλας's body, the silence in the chapel).
- **Bearer required.** All of this only works for a PC actively carrying the compass on their person — see the [README.md](./README.md) compass rule. If the party stashes it, the whole thread goes dark and they get a memory-fade cost instead. Let the loss be felt.
- **Other PCs within ~10 ft** share only partial effects: they notice the compass reacting if they look at it, but they do not feel the warmth and do not get the passive perception catch. Non-bearers outside 10 ft are effectively ordinary villagers for the duration. **This should guide tactical spacing during investigations** — who walks into Νεφέλη's cottage, who stays at the door, who takes point with the bearer when approaching the standing stones.

## What the party should learn through play (without being told)

By session's end, an attentive party can map the compass's "language":

1. **Warmth + tick = wrong here.** The thing or person is touched by the thin spot.
2. **Strong warmth + steady tick = a person the compass wants to be near** (an edit victim or thin-spot-permeable person).
3. **Cold = the compass's protection has been triggered or invoked deliberately.** Either the thin spot is *acting* (the horror beat) or something is shielding the compass (the chapel mystery).
4. **Irregular ticking = unstable boundary.** Thin spots, edit moments, sudden timeline activity.
5. **Silent and cold = special.** Either shielded or sacred. The chapel sits in this category and will matter later.

**Do not list these for the party.** Let them theorize. Reward correct guesses.

## The chapel mystery — flag it for Session 3+

When the party first enters Το Λεπτό Παρεκκλήσι and the compass goes silent and cold, **let the bearer notice and react.** This is the only place in the village where the compass *stops*. Why?

**DM-only**: The chapel sits over a small pocket of the original containment — a tiny "patch" the ancient order placed centuries ago, before the village existed. The faded harvest-god icon on the altar is, in fact, much older than its visible paint suggests; under the wood is a stone slab carved with a Watcher's binding rune. Orestis does not know. The Elders' Circle has forgotten. **This will be a Session 4 or 5 reveal.** For Session 2, just plant it.

If a player asks Orestis why his chapel feels so still, he will say only: *«Δεν ξέρω. Πάντοτε έτσι ήταν. Γι' αυτό έγινα ιερέας, νομίζω. Εδώ μέσα ο νους μου ησυχάζει.»* Honest. Untranslated.

## Payoff

- **The compass is revealed to be an *instrument*, not just protection.** The party learns to use it investigatively.
- The chapel's unexplained silence becomes a lingering mystery for Session 3+.
- **Tactical spacing** becomes a real consideration — who is the bearer, who is at the bearer's side, who walks ahead.

## Consequence if ignored

**None — the thread is always available.** This is a reward, not a punishment.

**However**: if the party stows the compass in a pack and refuses to wear it, **most of the rest of the session goes dark.** Threads 1, 3, 4, and 7 all rely on the bearer's perception. The party will lose original-timeline memories within hours and not understand why. **Re-read the bearer rule and run the "works its way out" beat from Scene 0.** Do not let the session start without one PC visibly carrying it.
