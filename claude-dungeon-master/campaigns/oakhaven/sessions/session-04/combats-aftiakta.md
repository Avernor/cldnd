## Stat block (CR 1-2 aberration)

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| AC | 12 |
| HP | 30 |
| Speed | 30 ft |
| STR / DEX / CON / INT / WIS / CHA | 10 / 14 / 12 / 8 / 14 / 6 |
| Saving throws | Wis +4 |
| Damage resistance | Bludgeoning, piercing, slashing from non-magical attacks |
| Damage vulnerability | Radiant |
| Senses | Darkvision 60 ft, Passive Perception 12 |
| Languages | Understands but cannot speak. Tries to. |

### Actions

**Multiattack** — two psychic touch attacks per turn

**Psychic Touch** — Melee attack, +4 to hit, reach 5 ft.
> Hit: 1d8 psychic damage AND target makes DC 12 Wisdom save or loses 1 point of Wisdom (returns on long rest). At 0 Wis, target is unconscious until 1 point is restored.

**Take What's Missing** (recharge 5-6) — Ranged psychic, 30 ft.
> Target makes DC 13 Wisdom save. Fail: target loses an arbitrary memory the DM picks (a name, a face, a detail of childhood). Reversible *only* by re-exposure to the source of the memory. Do not use this offensively to remove plot-critical info — pick small things that hurt the *character*, not the player.

### Special abilities

- **Compass aura**: within 10 ft of the compass, the Άφτιαχτο has **disadvantage on all rolls and cannot flee.**
- **Radiant vulnerability**: takes double damage from radiant sources (Lay on Hands, Sacred Flame, Cure Wounds at the cost of healing — *if* a player has the cleverness to use a cure spell offensively, allow it).
- **Iron nails** (if Ο Κόρακας was visited and gave them — Session 4+ probably): one-use, gives the Άφτιαχτο disadvantage on its next attack.

## The encounter

### Read-aloud (Greek) — when it appears

> Ο αέρας πέφτει. Όλος. Ξαφνικά.
>
> Από ανάμεσα στα δέντρα — ή απ' το πέρα του δρόμου — ή μέσα απ' το ίδιο το πέρασμα — βγαίνει **κάτι.**
>
> Στέκεται σε δύο πόδια. Έχει το ύψος ενός άντρα. Φοράει ρούχα — ή κάτι που ήταν ρούχα. Το πρόσωπό του *κουνιέται.* Σαν να ψάχνει σε ποιανού πρόσωπο μοιάζει σήμερα. Δεν επιτίθεται.
>
> Σας **κοιτάζει.**
>
> Και μετά — με στόμα που δεν θυμάται πώς να σχηματίζει λέξεις — προσπαθεί να πει κάτι. Ένας ήχος που δεν είναι λέξη. Μετά, με μια φωνή που δεν είναι δική του και δεν είναι μία:
>
> *«...ξέρω εσένα...»*

Pause. **Let the players choose.** Combat is one option. The grace note is another. They should *feel* the choice.

## The Grace Note (the alternative to combat)

If a PC uses **Lay on Hands, Healing Word, Cure Wounds, or Bardic Inspiration** AND **speaks the name** the Άφτιαχτο was — even guessed — the creature is momentarily restored. Quiet horror beat, then it fades.

### Which name is this Άφτιαχτο?

**Use Άνδρος Πετρόπουλος** if the party has any reasonable lead toward him (Ορέστης's list mentions him in Thread 3; Θάλεια's wool shirt is in Marina's back room). If they have *not* connected to that thread yet:

**Fallback names** (DM choice):
- **Στέφανος** — the "drowned" man from Ορέστης's list
- **Καλλιόπη / Κόρη** — Sylas's daughter, if she was edited too (long shot)
- **An unnamed person**: the Άφτιαχτο tries to say its name but cannot. *«Με... με...»* The grace note still works if the PC attempts something — the *gesture* matters.

### Read-aloud (Greek) — if the grace note succeeds

> Καθώς λες το όνομα και ακουμπάς το πλάσμα με [ξόρκι / τραγούδι / έμπνευση], το πρόσωπό του σταματάει να κουνιέται.
>
> Για μια στιγμή, βλέπεις **έναν άντρα.**
>
> Είναι λίγο πιο νέος απ' ό,τι θα έπρεπε. Έχει ξυλουργικά χέρια. Μυρίζει πεύκο και ποτάμι. Σε κοιτάζει στα μάτια και στο πρόσωπό του υπάρχει η πιο τρομερή ευγνωμοσύνη που έχεις δει ποτέ.

>
> Ο αέρας ξαναγυρίζει.

## Combat path

If they fight — fight. The party is level 3 and one Άφτιαχτο is a fair fight.

### Pacing
- Round 1: it strikes. The melee character it touches takes psychic damage and probably loses 1 Wisdom. *Make the Wisdom loss feel weird:* "You suddenly cannot remember your father's voice. Just for a second. It comes back." Plant the cost.
- Round 2-3: it focuses on whoever is *not* near the compass. The bearer is its worst matchup.
- Round 4: if it's losing badly, it tries **Take What's Missing** as a desperation move. The DM picks a small *flavor* memory — never a plot-critical fact. *"You forget the name of your first dog."*
- Round 5+: if reduced to 0 HP, it does not die — it **fades.** Gives a final wordless apology look. **Plant**: even killing it doesn't feel like victory. Something was *taken.*

### Loot
None. Άφτιαχτα carry nothing. They wear what they wore when they were unmade, and even that fades when they do. **Reward is XP and lore, not items.**

## After the encounter — what to drop

Whichever path was taken, drop **one** of these atmospheric beats afterward to seal the moment:

1. **The compass cools.** Goes back to its normal warmth. *«Σαν να αναπνέει με ανακούφιση.»*
2. **Ο Κόρακας's wisdom (if the party met him): a single iron nail in the Fighter's pocket they don't remember putting there.** A gift the woods left them.
3. **A moth.** The same too-late-in-the-year moth from Session 2. Lands on someone, opens its wings, the pattern is *almost a face.* Flies away. Free horror beat.
4. **The wind picks up again, and somewhere — too far to be the village — a woman is crying.** They cannot find the source. It stops as suddenly as it started.

## DM reminders

- **It does not attack on sight.** Open with intent ambiguity. Let the party choose.
- **It is not evil.** Play it as *desperately confused.* Sympathy is the right tone.
- **Don't pull punches if they fight.** A psychic-damage hit and a Wisdom save should *land.* The horror is in the cost, not the danger.
- **The grace note is the better story.** Subtly steer toward it without forcing — give them the lever (the radiant attack, the name, the Inspiration option) and let them pick it up.
- **One Άφτιαχτο only this session.** Don't use a pack. The Oldwood is filling up but the party is not yet level 5.
