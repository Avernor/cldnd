## Entry

Triggered at breakfast in Scene 1: Γερο-Αλέξης mentions quietly, while wiping a glass:
> *«Ο Ορέστης χθες βράδυ... δεν ήταν καλά. Έλεγε πράγματα. Σήμερα δεν έχει ανοίξει το παρεκκλήσι. Δεν είναι του χαρακτήρα του.»*

He won't elaborate. He's worried but won't gossip. If pressed, he'll just say *«Πηγαίνετε να δείτε. Σας ξέρει.»*

---

## What happened last night (DM only)

After the party left him, Ορέστης went back to the chapel alone. Sat with the records. Opened a bottle. Then another. Sometime after midnight, he walked to the inn — *did not go inside* — stood outside the door and shouted toward the upstairs windows where he thought the party slept.

Three or four villagers heard. The words were unclear, but they included *«τάφοι»* and *«ψέματα»* and *«Σίλας»*. Αλέξης came out, walked him home. Put him to bed.

The chapel did not open this morning. The bell did not ring. **This is the first time in fourteen years.**

---

## Where to find him

In the small back room of Το Λεπτό Παρεκκλήσι — his living quarters. Bed unmade. Wine bottle on its side. Vomit in a chamber pot. He is sitting on the floor with his back against the bed. Not asleep. Not crying. Just sitting.

### Read-aloud (Greek) — first sight

> Η πόρτα του πίσω δωματίου είναι μισάνοιχτη. Μέσα μυρίζει κρασί ξινό και ίδρωτα. Ο Ορέστης κάθεται στο πάτωμα, με την πλάτη ακουμπισμένη στο κρεβάτι. Δεν σηκώνει το βλέμμα. Δεν σας έχει ακούσει — ή προσποιείται ότι δεν σας έχει ακούσει.
>
> Στο τραπέζι, μια εκκλησιαστική κουκούλα, διπλωμένη. Δίπλα της, ένα μαχαίρι κουζίνας. Καθαρό. Όχι από ψωμί.

**The knife is real.** He has not used it. He was thinking about it. Don't draw attention to it. If a player notices and acts, that's a powerful character moment.

## The conversation

He will not look at them at first. When he speaks, his voice is dry:

> *«Φύγατε χθες και έμεινα μόνος μου με αυτά που μου είπατε. Με τα δύο σώματα. Με το ψεύτικο μωρό. Με τον σκελετό. Με την πυξίδα σας. Με όλα τους.*
>
> *Και κατάλαβα κάτι. Δεν είστε εσείς οι τρελοί. Εγώ είμαι. Όλο το χωριό είναι. Σαράντα χρόνια θάβω ανθρώπους που δεν έζησαν ποτέ. Σαράντα χρόνια λέω προσευχές για ψυχές που δεν υπήρξαν. Και ο θεός μου με αφήνει να το κάνω. Γιατί;*
>
> *Σήμερα το πρωί δεν χτύπησα την καμπάνα. Πρώτη φορά. Δεν ήξερα ποιον θα καλούσε.»*

### What he needs
- To be told he is *not* crazy. They saw the same things he did.
- To be told that his work — the real burials, the real prayers — *mattered* before the edits. The dead he buried *were* real, even if the records lie now.
- To be physically gotten up. Get him on his feet. Get him drinking water, not wine.
- A reason to keep going.

### Skill checks (suggestive, not strict)

| Approach | Check | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Persuasion DC 14 — *you are not alone in this* | Pass: he stands. He drinks water. He listens. | Fail: he weeps. They have to try again or another approach. |
| Insight DC 12 — read what he actually needs | Pass: see the knife and *the real thing he needs is permission to be afraid.* | Fail: he reads as just hungover. Misread. |
| Religion DC 13 — speak to his faith | Pass: he hears them. *«Ο θεός μου σώπασε. Αλλά εσείς δεν σωπαίνετε. Αυτό είναι κάτι.»* | Fail: he laughs bitterly. *«Ποιος θεός; Ποιος θεός θα έπαιρνε αυτό σοβαρά;»* |
| Bardic Inspiration / song / poem | Auto. The bard recovers him better than anything. The chapel has not had music in a long time. |
| Lay on Hands — heal his hangover | Auto. He is briefly stunned. *«Δεν είμαι πληγωμένος. Είμαι... ευχαριστώ.»* |

## What he gives them if they save him

A list. He's been keeping it. **Hidden in a copy of the chapel's burial registry, in a drawer.** Eight names. Each one is a grave that appeared without him digging it. With dates carved that he knows are wrong. With descriptions of the deceased that no living villager can confirm.

| Name on grave | What Ορέστης suspects |
|---|---|
| Σίλας Ρέυν | Confirmed (Session 2). Relocation. |
| Ηλίας Μαρίνου | Marina's "husband" she has forgotten — wedding ring matches |
| Άνδρος Πετρόπουλος | Lumberjack. Θάλεια in Marina's back room flinches at the name. **Erasure.** |
| Καλλιόπη Ρέυν (Κόρη) | Σίλας's daughter. Ten years old when she died. **Real** — actually died, but the date may be wrong |
| ανώνυμο μωρό (1) | The fake he and the party dug up. **Invention.** |
| ανώνυμο μωρό (2) | A second fake grave. Smaller. Same year. He has not opened it. He cannot. |
| Στέφανος Παπαϊωάννου | Listed as "drowned 8 years ago." Kelis's ledger has him *registered as still alive* in the same year. Contradiction. |
| Μάριος (no last name) | Dug last week by Ορέστης himself. He doesn't remember whose son the boy was. The villagers say the boy was always a fixture in the village. Ορέστης has no memory of him. |

**This is a goldmine.** Each name is a thread. Some lead to NPC encounters (Θάλεια, Marina), some lead to old records, some lead nowhere. *Do not unpack them all in Session 3.* Treat the list as a slow-burn resource for Sessions 4-8.

## What if they ignore him entirely

He drinks more. He goes to the chapel. He rings the bell **at the wrong hour** — three times, just before sunset, when no service is scheduled. The whole village comes out, alarmed. He stands on the chapel steps and tells them, slurred:

> *«Δέκα χρόνια έχουμε χτίσει αυτό το χωριό πάνω σε τάφους που δεν είναι αληθινοί. Ρωτήστε τους ξένους που ήρθαν. Ξέρουν. Ξέρουν.»*

**Now the village suspects the party.** Κάλχας Βόσσος (the mill owner) is going to come asking questions tomorrow. Some villagers will refuse to serve them. The Elders' Circle will summon them.

This is *not* a campaign-ender. But it changes the social texture of Δρυόχωρο meaningfully. The party can no longer move freely. They have a clock now: prove what they're saying, or be run out of the village.

**If you want this — let it happen.** It's a great escalation. But it should be a *consequence of player choice*, not a default.

---

## What if they ignore him and he kills himself

If they have actively dismissed him, mocked him, or told him to keep quiet without offering support — and he was in deep enough that the knife was on the table:

He doesn't make it through the night. Found in the morning by the mill foreman's son. The chapel bell doesn't ring.

**This is a real consequence.** Do not soften it. The campaign continues. The village mourns. Κάλχας takes over running the chapel temporarily. The party knows what they did or didn't do. The grace-note villager NPCs notice — Νεφέλη in particular will look at the party differently from then on.

**Inspiration penalty for the rest of the campaign**: any PC who refused to help Ορέστης loses 1 inspiration the next time they would gain one. Once.

## DM tone notes

- Ορέστης is not a quest-giver. He is a *person.* Play him as a man drowning, not a story-beat.
- He is **not unlikable.** He is funny when sober, kind when he can be, quietly observant. The drink is recent and triggered by what he learned. Do not play him as a generic alcoholic.
- The chapel is his life's work. The bell not ringing is, for him, the worst thing he has ever done.
- If a player gives him *anything* — a drink of water, a hand up, a song, a verse from a holy text — he will remember it for the rest of the campaign. He is starved for human contact that doesn't include him telling lies for a living.
