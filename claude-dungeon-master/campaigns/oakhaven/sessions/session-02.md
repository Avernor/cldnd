# Session 2 — Το Χωριό που Ξέχασε
*(The Village That Forgot)*

> **Format note**: This is a sandbox session. Only the opening is fixed. After that, the party drives. Eight threads are available; the players choose. Run threads in whatever order they engage. The session ends softly at nightfall — with a single clear pull toward Session 3.

---

## Session 1 Recap — Read-Aloud (Greek)

Open Session 2 with this. Read slowly, let it breathe, then begin Scene 0.

> Πριν από δύο νύχτες, ένας αγγελιοφόρος σας σταμάτησε στον δρόμο προς την Καίλεν. Είχε ένα γράμμα. Ήταν γραμμένο με το χέρι του βάρδου και σφραγισμένο με τη σφραγίδα του κλαν του παλαντίνου — και οι δύο ορκίζονταν ότι ποτέ δεν το είχαν γράψει. Το μήνυμα ήταν σύντομο: *«Ελάτε στο Δρυόχωρο. Ο χρόνος σπάει.»*
>
> Μια θύελλα που δεν έπρεπε να υπάρχει σας έριξε από τον δρόμο και σας οδήγησε σε έναν σπασμένο πύργο από μαύρο γυαλί. Στο υπόγειό του, ένα παλιό εργαστήριο, και μέσα του μια **πυξίδα** — ζεστή, που τικ-τακάει, που δεν δείχνει τίποτα. Κάτι σαν πεδίο σχηματίστηκε γύρω σας καθώς την αγγίξατε. Δεν ξέρατε τότε τι ήταν.
>
> Φτάσατε στο Δρυόχωρο. Κανένας δεν αναγνώρισε το γράμμα. Κανένας δεν αναγνώρισε την πυξίδα. Στο πανδοχείο *Το Κούφιο Ελάφι* σας υποδέχτηκε ο **Σίλας Ρέυν** — σκληρός, θυμωμένος, χήρος, ζωντανός. Στην ίδια αίθουσα καθόταν μια κουρασμένη γυναίκα με κουκούλα και γραφόταν σε ένα ημερολόγιο. Τη λέγανε **Βάνια**. Δεν της δώσατε σημασία.
>
> Το πρωί, η **Μίρα** — έγκυος, φοβισμένη — σας παρακάλεσε να βρείτε τον άντρα της, τον **Θωμά**, που είχε χαθεί στο παλιό νάνικο ορυχείο πάνω απ' τον μύλο. Μπήκατε στα **Σπλάχνα** με το φως της ημέρας. Τον σώσατε. Δίπλα του, σε μια ξεχασμένη στοά, βρήκατε τον σκελετό ενός άντρα που κρατούσε ακόμα ένα ατσάλινο **ρολόι** — ένα ρολόι που γύριζε ανάποδα. Όταν βγήκατε στην επιφάνεια, ήταν νύχτα. Δεν θα έπρεπε να ήταν.
>
> Επιστρέψατε τον Θωμά στη γυναίκα του. Πήγατε για ύπνο.
>
> Σας ξύπνησε η πυξίδα — καυτή τώρα, να τικ-τακάει σαν καρδιά σε πανικό. Κατεβήκατε. Το πανδοχείο ήταν *άλλο*. Ο Σίλας είχε φύγει· πίσω απ' τον πάγκο στεκόταν ένας γέρος που δεν είχατε ξαναδεί. Όταν ρωτήσατε για τον Σίλας, σας κοίταξε με λύπη: *«Ο Σίλας; Ο καημένος. Δέκα χρόνια τώρα έχει που τον θάψαμε — πέθανε σε καβγά, εδώ μέσα, πάνω σ' αυτόν τον πάγκο.»* Και το εννοούσε.
>
> Ένα σκίσιμο άνοιξε στον αέρα — σε μέγεθος γράμματος. Ο βάρδος άρχισε να ξεθωριάζει. Τα χέρια του έγιναν διάφανα. Τότε καταλάβατε. Γράψατε το γράμμα — *«Ελάτε στο Δρυόχωρο, ο χρόνος σπάει»* — με το ίδιο το χέρι του βάρδου, το σφραγίσατε με τη σφραγίδα του κλαν του παλαντίνου, και το στείλατε μέσα απ' το σκίσιμο. Στους εαυτούς σας. Πριν από δύο νύχτες.
>
> Και τώρα το σκίσιμο αρχίζει να κλείνει.

## ⚠ The single rule that breaks this session if you forget it

**The compass must be on a PC's person, not in a backpack.** If the party stows it, half the threads stop working — Threads 1, 3, 4, and 7 all rely on the bearer feeling the compass react. **Establish this in Scene 0** using the boxed compass rule (full text in Scene 0 below). Run the "works its way out of the pack" beat the first time anyone tries to stow it. Do not let the session start without one PC visibly carrying it.

If you only remember one thing from this prep document, it's this.

## Scene 0 — Η Εκκρεμότητα του Γράμματος

Pick up from exactly where Session 1 ended. The bard has just written *«Ελάτε στο Δρυόχωρο, ο χρόνος σπάει»* on the letter and is holding it before the rift.

### Read-aloud (Greek)

> Ο βάρδος αφήνει το γράμμα να φύγει απ' τα χέρια του. Ρουφιέται μέσα στο άνοιγμα — ένα λεπτό σκίσιμο στον αέρα, όχι μεγαλύτερο από μια παλάμη. Για ένα δευτερόλεπτο. Για δύο. Τίποτα. Και μετά το άνοιγμα κλείνει με έναν ήχο σαν αναπνοή που κρατιέται πολλή ώρα και επιτέλους αφήνεται.
>
> Ο βάρδος αισθάνεται τα πόδια του ξανά. Το βάρος του κορμιού του. Το στομάχι του, άδειο. Είναι εδώ. Είναι πραγματικός.
>
> Η πυξίδα στο χέρι όποιου την κρατάει παύει να τικ-τακ. Γίνεται κρύα. Κρύα σαν πέτρα που την ξέχασαν στο χιόνι.
>
> Το πανδοχείο είναι ήσυχο. Πολύ ήσυχο.
>
> Πίσω απ' τον πάγκο, ένας γέρος με μαλλιά σαν χιόνι σηκώνει το κεφάλι από ένα ποτήρι που σκουπίζει με μια πετσέτα που ήταν κάποτε λευκή. Σας χαμογελά αργά, σαν να προσπαθεί να θυμηθεί πού σας ξέρει από.
>
> «Μα... θέλετε κάτι, αγαπητοί μου; Είναι αργά η ώρα.»

### DM beats

- Let the players sit with it. The bard's player especially. *Do not rush this.*
- Anything they try to do physically works — the altered reality has "accepted" them.
- **The compass will not be stowed.** Establish this *now*, in the first ten minutes of the session, before the party has a chance to stuff it in a backpack and forget it. See the boxed rule below — surface it organically through play.
- If anyone asks Γερο-Αλέξης about Σίλας, his face softens with old sadness: *«Σίλας; Ο Σίλας ο Ρέυν; Αχ, καημένε μου. Δέκα χρόνια έχει που τον θάψαμε — πέθανε σε καβγά, εδώ μέσα, πάνω σ' αυτόν τον πάγκο. Δεν ήταν ο ίδιος άνθρωπος αφότου έχασε τη Κόρη. Εγώ κρατάω το πανδοχείο από εκείνη τη νύχτα. Γιατί ρωτάτε τώρα, αγαπητοί μου;»* Insight (DC 14): he *believes* what he is saying completely, and he is genuinely grieving. There is no lie to catch.
- The stag's head over the hearth is the same — still missing the one antler. Anchor detail for the players: *some things persist.*
- If they try to go outside: the village is asleep. Wet streets, a single lantern down the lane, the river in the distance. Let them walk if they want. Nothing attacks.
- Eventually they will want to sleep. The night passes. Go to Scene 1.

### The compass rule — establish in Scene 0

**Η Πυξίδα does not want to be stored.** This is the single most important mechanical-fiction rule for Session 2 and everything that follows. Introduce it *gently but firmly* in Scene 0, before the party moves on, so it never gets buried in a backpack where it can't do its work.

**What the party discovers (play it out, do not lecture)**:

1. **It is warm.** Noticeably so. On a cold late-autumn morning in a shocked inn, a warm object in a pocket is a *comfort.* Whichever PC holds it last at the end of the cliffhanger feels it press against their palm like a small live thing.
2. **It will not stay in a pack.** Within about ten minutes of being stowed, the compass **works its way out.** Read-aloud, the first time someone tries:
   > Ο [όποιος] σφίγγει τον ιμάντα του σακιδίου και αρχίζει να περπατά. Ένα λεπτό. Δύο. Στο τρίτο, νιώθει κάτι στο ύψος της μέσης του — ένα χτύπημα, σαν χέρι που ζητάει προσοχή. Όταν κοιτάζει, η πυξίδα είναι πάλι έξω, πιασμένη στη ζώνη του, ζεστή. Δεν θυμάται να την έβγαλε.
   Repeat the beat once or twice if players insist — each attempt is more urgent. On the third try it **thumps once**, hard enough to feel through canvas, and then they stop trying. The compass wants to be worn. It will be worn.
3. **Its protection has a range.** Whoever is physically carrying it (in hand, belt, pocket, against skin — *not* in a closed pack) is the **bearer.** Bearer benefits are real and physical: memories of the original timeline stay vivid; the compass's passive ticking and warmth communicate information; other PCs within about **10 ft** share partial protection but not the full perception. Outside 10 ft, a non-bearer PC begins to forget original-timeline details within an hour (reversible on re-proximity). **This is the mechanical spine of Thread 1 and Thread 4.**
   - **"Stowed" vs. "set down"**: the compass refuses being *closed away* (in a pack, a box, a chest). It tolerates being *set down in plain view* — on a table during a conversation, on a counter, on a bed beside a sick man. The field still operates from where it sits; it just cannot follow until picked up again. The Θωμάς cottage scene (Thread 2.A) and the Νεφέλη cottage visit assume the compass on a table — that is fine.
4. **Only one bearer at a time.** The compass chooses with physical warmth — it is noticeably warmer in one person's hand than in any other's at a given moment. Bearer can change by deliberate hand-off, but the compass "settles" over a few minutes. Establish this so the party can decide who carries it and why.

**Why it works this way** (DM only — do not explain unless asked): the compass is an **anchor**, and an anchor needs a person to anchor. Stowed in a pack, it still protects *the pack and its contents* — but the compass-field needs living flesh to radiate through for the protective properties to extend to perception and memory. The party will not know this in Session 2. Let them learn by experimentation and consequence.

**How to surface the rule in play**:
- Scene 0: the bearer's pocket feels warm as they walk downstairs. Narrate it.
- Scene 0: if anyone tries to stow it, run the "works its way out" beat above once. If they insist, run it again, harder. Do not let them stow it.
- Scene 1 Breakfast: when another PC picks it up and the first PC sets it down, narrate the warmth transferring. *«Τώρα είναι ζεστή στο δικό σου χέρι. Το άλλο έχει κρυώσει.»* The party learns the hand-off is real.
- First time a non-bearer PC walks more than 10 ft away from the bearer during a scene, narrate a small memory-flicker — the non-bearer forgets, for a heartbeat, the name of the old man they're looking at. It comes back when they step closer. This is how Thread 4 pays off without becoming exposition.

**If a player still insists on stowing it**: let them. The thin spot punishes this quickly and concretely — within the hour, that PC forgets an original-timeline detail they will later need (Σίλας's eye color, the exact words of the bootstrap letter, the smell of the tower cellar). Reversible, but only by deliberate re-exposure. **Do not make this punitive.** Make it *legible.* The party should feel the compass wants to be worn and should feel the cost of refusing.

### If a player asks "what just happened"

In character, the bard — or anyone who handled the compass during the rift — can sense, faintly: *«Το γράμμα που γράψαμε ήταν το γράμμα που λάβαμε.»* The paradox closed on itself. Time has healed over the cut. But they are the only ones who remember the cut was ever there.

### DM-only: why the letter *actually* saved the bard

Have this ready if a player presses. Do not volunteer it — it's a payoff for the sharp question.

The fragment Vanya was running an edit test: *what if the bard never existed?* She pushed the change into the thin spot and the bard began to unravel. But the bard grabbed the compass, and inside the compass's aura he and the party wrote the letter **that had already delivered them to Oakhaven two nights earlier**. The letter is causally *upstream* of Vanya's edit — without the letter, the party never comes to Oakhaven, the bard is never at the inn for Vanya to erase, and her fragment never has an edit to make. The bootstrap loop is self-insulating: you cannot erase a person whose erasure would prevent your own arrival. Her fragment felt the loop close and retreated — the edit was causally impossible once the letter landed.

This is not magic the party controls. It is a trick of the thin spot's own logic, and it only works because the compass was present to let them write a letter that the thin spot had to respect. **They cannot do this again on demand.** If a player wants to use "just write a letter to the past" as a general tool, make the cost real: each attempt without a bootstrap loop already in place is a new edit the thin spot does not owe them, and it bleeds — Άφτιαχτα multiply, a Quiet Day lands, someone vanishes.

## Scene 1 — Πρωινό στο Δρυόχωρο

### Read-aloud (Greek)

> Το πρωί έρχεται αργά στο Δρυόχωρο. Το φως στρώνεται πάνω στα ξύλινα σπίτια με τα σκουρόχρωμα κεραμίδια σαν να μην είναι σίγουρο αν πρέπει να έρθει. Ο αέρας μυρίζει μουδιασμένο τζάκι και βρεγμένο ξύλο. Κάτω από το παράθυρό σας, δύο γυναίκες μιλούν για το πλύσιμο. Ένα σκυλί γαβγίζει κάπου μακριά, τρεις φορές, και μετά σταματά.
>
> Το χωριό είναι ακριβώς όπως χθες — και ταυτόχρονα, όχι.

### DM beats

The purpose of this scene is to surface 3–4 threads *organically* within the first twenty minutes of real-time play, then step back and let the party declare what they do next.

Use these **ambient triggers** naturally as they take their morning:

- **Breakfast at the inn**: Γερο-Αλέξης serves them. He asks if they slept well "after their hard night on the road" — which is what he remembers of their arrival. At a neighboring table, an older couple is talking, and one of them says quietly: *«...άλλος τάφος εμφανίστηκε στο χωράφι. Ο Ορέστης πίνει πάλι.»* → **Thread 3 trigger**.
- **Footsteps at the door**: Around mid-morning, Μίρα Τεφρή arrives at the inn looking for them. Pale, pregnant, frightened. → **Thread 2 trigger**.
- **The compass stirs**: Whoever carries the compass notices it warming slightly in their pocket as they pass Τα Είδη της Μαρίνας on the street. → **Thread 4 trigger** (passive).
- **Children in the square**: If they walk through the village square, they hear the rhyme being sung by Μικρή Ιώ. → **Thread 5 trigger**.

The other four threads (Σίλας investigation, Rook, Άφτιαχτα, Βόσσου journals) require the party to pursue them. Do not hand-deliver. Let them ask questions.

### Atmospheric beats to drop in (not threads — texture)

Use one, two, or all of these as the day unfolds. They are free. They cost nothing mechanically but build pressure.

1. **Winter Fair deadline.** Γερο-Αλέξης, wiping a glass, mentions the Χειμωνιάτικο Πανηγύρι is less than two weeks away — and adds, half to himself, *«Αν δεν στρώσει ό,τι τρέχει στο χωριό, δεν θα έχουμε Πανηγύρι φέτος. Και τα παιδιά το περιμένουν.»* He will not elaborate. He does not know what he means. The players should feel the clock.

2. **A Quiet Day lands for ten minutes.** Pick a moment in the afternoon, preferably mid-thread. The village dogs go silent. A woman washing linen in the river stops mid-motion and cannot remember whether she already wrung the shirt in her hands. The compass ticks irregularly — three beats, a pause, one beat, a pause, four beats — as if it is reading *something happening outside the room*. Then the dogs start again and the woman laughs nervously and everything is normal. **Do not explain it.** This is the real Vanya working the thin spot from wherever she is. The party feels the edge of her hand on their world for the first time. **Passive Perception 13** catches the compass oddness. No check needed to notice the silence.

3. **The graves that don't match.** If the party walks past Το Νεκρό Χωράφι, even briefly, any PC with Passive Perception 14+ notices one old headstone (not one of the three Orestis flags) whose carved date says thirty winters ago but whose lichen is a year or two at most. Cross-reference for Thread 1.C/3 investigators.

4. **A moth that should not be alive in late autumn** lands on the paladin's shoulder at some point during the afternoon. Pale, slow, unseasonal. When it opens its wings, the pattern on them is almost a face. It flies away before any PC can really look at it. This is a remnant fragment of *something* the party will not identify. Free horror beat; no payoff required.

### Soft time structure

Oakhaven's day, for DM reference:

| Time | Village state |
|---|---|
| Πρωί (morning) | Village wakes, market stirs, mill crew heads out |
| Μεσημέρι (midday) | Quiet — everyone at work, in the mill or the woods |
| Απόγευμα (afternoon) | Children underfoot, travelers at the Crossing, Orestis drunk |
| Σούρουπο (dusk) | Mill crew returns, lanterns in windows, Oldwood felt at the village edge |
| Νύχτα (night) | Closed doors, the Marrow loud in the quiet, compass behavior changes |

A day fits 4–5 threads comfortably. Θωμάς's condition (Thread 2) deteriorates hour-by-hour if not addressed.

### Between threads — what to narrate

When the party finishes one thread and pauses to decide what to do next, drop one of these (or improvise similar) so the village stays alive without dragging:

- **A villager passing by** with one detail of unfinished business — a man carrying a half-built cradle past the inn, two old women arguing about whose chickens those are, a child running with a bucket of dirty water and almost knocking the paladin over. No story. Just texture.
- **A small compass beat** — a half-second tick in someone's pocket as they pass an alley they hadn't planned to walk down.
- **The weather shifts** — a colder breeze, a light rain that stops as fast as it started, a moment of unexpected sun.
- **A bell** — the chapel bell, struck once for a service nobody attends. Or three quick mill bells when a foreman calls his crew to a problem.
- **One named NPC visible in the middle distance** — Νεφέλη walking back from gathering moss, Αλκμήνη wiping soot off her arms outside the forge, Κέλης on his stool watching the road. Available, never demanded.

These keep the village from feeling like a video-game hub between quest markers.

## Thread 1 — Σίλας: Ο Άνθρωπος που Ποτέ Δεν Ήταν

**Entry**: The party almost certainly pushes this themselves. They remember him. Nobody else does.

### Avenues of investigation

#### A. Ask Γερο-Αλέξης at the inn
He remembers Σίλας *clearly* — just not the way the party does. He is not lying.
- *«Ο Σίλας; Ο Θεός να τον συχωρέσει. Δέκα χρόνια τώρα. Ήμουν εδώ εκείνη τη νύχτα — καθόμουν δυο σκαμνιά μακριά. Ο καβγάς άρχισε απ' το τίποτα, όπως αρχίζουν όλοι οι κακοί καβγάδες. Ένα μαχαίρι, κι ο Σίλας στο πάτωμα, και κανένας Ρέυν να κληρονομήσει τον πάγκο. Η Γερόντισσα Εστία μου έδωσε τα κλειδιά το ίδιο βράδυ. Γιατί με ρωτάτε τώρα, μετά από τόσα χρόνια;»*
- **Insight (DC 14)**: his memory is *sincere*. He is genuinely grieving a man he watched die a decade ago. Something is wrong with reality, not with him.
- If the party insist that Σίλας served them beer two nights ago, he is gentle but firm, and becomes worried for their health — suggests they see **Γριά Νεφέλη**. This is a quiet second hook — visiting Νεφέλη is always a good decision; she will *notice* the compass without the party mentioning it, and quietly watch them afterward.
- **If the party later find his napkin poetry** (Perception DC 13 on a stack of napkins by the bar): one line, in handwriting that isn't his — *«Ο πάγκος ήταν δικός μου.»* Γερο-Αλέξης stares at it and cannot explain it.

#### B. Ask regulars or passersby
Everyone remembers a Σίλας — but a dead one. The stories almost agree, and the small disagreements are the interesting part.
- One old man at a corner table: *«Τον Σίλας τον έθαψαν πριν δέκα χρόνια. Τον μαχαίρωσε ένας ξυλοκόπος απ' τον Μύλο σε καβγά για ένα χρέος.»*
- A mill hand at the next bench frowns: *«Όχι, δεν ήταν ξυλοκόπος. Ήταν περαστικός. Κανένας δεν τον ήξερε. Έφυγε την ίδια νύχτα.»*
- An old woman knitting: *«Το φτωχό το κορίτσι του είχε πεθάνει πρώτα. Αυτό τον σκότωσε, όχι το μαχαίρι. Το μαχαίρι ήρθε μετά.»*
- **Insight DC 13** on listening to them together: they all *believe* they remember, but no two stories match on the attacker, the weapon, or the year before — only on *Σίλας dead, ten years, a barfight, his daughter gone first.* The core of the edit is solid; the details are the land scabbing over.
- If the party insists they drank from Σίλας's tap two nights ago, one old woman crosses herself and mutters *«Το Δρυόχωρο θυμάται τους δικούς του. Δεν ξέρω τι θυμάστε εσείς.»*

#### C. Check Γερο-Κέλης's ledger at Ο Στάβλος

This is the **high-payoff route**. Γερο-Κέλης is taciturn but has the best memory in the village — because he writes everything down.

Greek read-aloud:
> Ο Στάβλος μυρίζει άλογο, άχυρο και καπνισμένη πίπα. Ο Γερο-Κέλης κάθεται σε ένα σκαμνί δίπλα στην πόρτα και καπνίζει χωρίς να σηκώσει το βλέμμα όταν μπαίνετε. Έχει στα γόνατά του ένα χοντρό, δερμάτινο βιβλίο με φθαρμένα φύλλα. «Τι θέλετε;»

- **Persuasion DC 12** (or DC 10 if they are honest about *why* — "θέλουμε να ξέρουμε αν κάποιος ονόματι Σίλας υπήρξε εδώ") gets him to let them read the ledger.
- **Investigation DC 13** in the ledger reveals an entry four autumns ago: *«Σίλας Ρέυν, αγόρασε χόρτο για το άλογό του. 3 χαλκοί.»* The entry is plain, unannotated, in Κέλης's own undisputed hand. **The paradox**: the whole village insists Σίλας died ten years ago. Κέλης's own ledger has him alive and buying hay *four* years ago. Κέλης stares at it and cannot explain it. *«Εγώ το έγραψα. Αυτό είναι το χέρι μου. Και όμως ο άνθρωπος ήταν στο χώμα δέκα χρόνια πριν το γράψω αυτό.»* He reads it three times, closes the ledger, opens it again to check the page has not moved.
- **Further search (another Investigation DC 13)** reveals three more suspicious entries across the past eighteen months. Each one is a name annotated *«διεγράφη»* in a different hand — a hand Κέλης cannot identify but the party recognizes instantly as *his own*:
  1. *«Ηλίας Μαρίνου»* (7 months ago) — *Μαρίνα's erased husband*
  2. *«Μάριος Λιβέρης»* (14 months ago) — *one of the graves in Thread 3*
  3. *«[όνομα ξεθωριασμένο, αδιάβαστο]»* (2 months ago) — *deliberately unknown; a hook for later*

- The Σίλας entry is a different *kind* of edit from the three «διεγράφη» ones. The three were people *removed.* Σίλας was a person *moved in time.* If any player notices and says so aloud, reward them — this is the beginning of understanding Vanya's craft has more than one tool.
- **Conductor seed (new, optional but recommended)** — if the party reads deeper (Investigation DC 14, or prompted by Κέλης himself once he trusts them), an entry from his grandfather's hand, maybe forty years old: *«Ήρθε ένας ξένος απ' την Καίλεν. Αγόρασε δύο μέτρα ασημένιο σύρμα κι έναν γυάλινο χωνοειδή σωλήνα με μόλυβδο στη βάση. Πλήρωσε σε ασήμι. Δεν ρώτησα τι τα ήθελε. Αριστόδημος τ' όνομά του.»* The party will not know yet what this means. **You (DM) know**: Αριστόδημος was buying parts for a replacement *conductor* — the lost second instrument of the Circle. This line is the single breadcrumb that pays off in Session 3 at the tower, when they learn the conductor's name.
- Γερο-Κέλης, staring at his own ledger with entries he did not write, turns very pale and says nothing for a long moment. Then: *«Αυτό το χέρι... το ξέρω. Και όμως δεν το ξέρω.»* If the party explains kindly, he becomes their quiet ally. He will let them consult the ledger whenever they need.

#### D. Visit Το Νεκρό Χωράφι looking for a grave
**The grave is there.** *«Σίλας Ρέυν — αναπαύθηκε εδώ και δέκα χειμώνες.»* The stone is weathered, moss on its shoulders, the earth around it long grown over. By every outward sign it has been there a decade. Beside it, a smaller, older stone: **Κόρη Ρέυν**, age 9, died about a year before her father. If the party stands at these two stones and understands what they are looking at, that is the emotional core of the session — the edit is not a gap, it is a *displacement*, and the village grieved a man who was serving them beer two nights ago. → cross-pollinates with **Thread 3**. Αδελφός Ορέστης may be there.

#### E. Confront Μαρίνα at Τα Είδη της Μαρίνας
She does not know Σίλας. She is also uneasy for reasons she cannot explain. If the party is kind (Persuasion DC 11), she mentions that she has a wedding ring in her drawer she cannot remember putting there. She has never been married. Cross-pollinates with the ledger (Ηλίας Μαρίνου — her erased husband).

**Payoff beat — the small memorial.** If the party returns to her later with the ledger entry (*«Ηλίας Μαρίνου — διεγράφη»*) and speaks gently, she will not fully believe them — there is nothing in her mind to believe with — but she will hold the ring differently. If a PC offers to take it to Αδελφός Ορέστης and have it buried in Το Νεκρό Χωράφι in a small unmarked grave, she lets them. Do not push her to cry; she cannot mourn someone she does not remember. She simply says *«Αν τον ξέρατε… ευχαριστώ.»* 

Orestis, if told, will quietly dig the hole himself. No ceremony. The party has just buried a man who was never born, in a village that does not remember him, beside a stone they will not carve. **This is the session's second grace-note alongside Thread 7.** Award inspiration to whichever PC lead it.

Θάλεια (Άνδρος's wife — see lore.md), who lives in a back room of Μαρίνα's shop, watches from a doorway without knowing why she is watching. She will dream about the moment for weeks. Do not mention her by name to the players unless they have already met her in Thread 7.

### Payoff
- **Concrete proof Σίλας was edited, not forgotten** — and evidence that the edit is of a *different kind* from the «διεγράφη» removals. Vanya has more than one tool.
- Four known recent edits in the village: Σίλας (relocated), Ηλίας Μαρίνου, Μάριος Λιβέρης, and the unreadable name. A *pattern* with a telling inconsistency.
- Γερο-Κέλης becomes a repeatable investigation resource.
- Μαρίνα cross-link established.

### Consequence if ignored
Memory of Σίλας slowly fades for whichever PC spends the most time outside the compass's 10 ft radius — small details first (his voice, the color of his eyes, what he said to them on Night 1). The bearer holds onto every detail. Non-bearer PCs who walk apart from the bearer for hours at a time lose the most. Reversible by deliberate remembering at the bearer's side. A quiet horror beat, not a punishment.

## Thread 2 — Η Μίρα Έρχεται για τον Θωμά

**Entry**: Μίρα arrives at the inn mid-morning. This is the only thread with **real in-world harm** for neglect.

### Read-aloud (Greek)

> Η Μίρα στέκεται στο κατώφλι του πανδοχείου και κρατάει το σάλι της σφιχτά γύρω της, αν και δεν κάνει κρύο. Το πρόσωπό της είναι χλωμό. Τα μάτια της έχουν τα σκοτεινά του αϋπνίας. «Συγγνώμη που σας ενοχλώ τόσο νωρίς», λέει ήσυχα. «Ο Θωμάς... δεν είναι καλά. Από τη νύχτα που τον φέρατε πίσω. Σας παρακαλώ, ελάτε μαζί μου. Δεν ξέρω σε ποιον άλλον να μιλήσω.»

### The scene at the Τεφρός cottage

A small two-room cottage at the edge of the lumber district. Clean, sparse, a cradle half-built in the corner. Θωμάς sits at the kitchen table staring at a cup of tea. It is half-full. He poured it ten minutes ago, saw himself in two places, set the kettle down, and forgot the tea was his.

Greek read-aloud:
> Ο Θωμάς σηκώνει το βλέμμα καθώς μπαίνετε. Για μια στιγμή, το πρόσωπό του φωτίζεται — σας αναγνωρίζει. Μετά γίνεται αβέβαιο. «Είστε... ήρθατε για το τσάι;» Η Μίρα κλαίει ήσυχα δίπλα στον νεροχύτη.

### Branches

#### A. Speak with Θωμάς with the compass nearby
The compass warms. Θωμάς leans toward it like a man leaning toward fire in the cold.
- With the compass on the table between them, Θωμάς can hold a thread of memory for ~30 seconds at a time.
- If the party asks him about the mine, he *remembers it correctly* for the first time since they rescued him.
- If they ask about Σίλας — watch his face. He closes his eyes. When he opens them, both memories are in his head at once and the dissonance hurts. *«Τον είδα χθες βράδυ. Μου έδωσε μια μπύρα. Μου είπε ότι ο γιος του Λιβέρη χρωστάει χρήματα και ότι δεν θα τα ξαναδεί. Και τώρα η Μίρα μού λέει ότι τον έθαψαν πριν δέκα χρόνια. Και τα θυμάμαι και τα δύο. Και τα δύο είναι αλήθεια. Τι μου κάνετε;»* He begins to cry quietly. The compass is the only reason he can hold both truths at once.

#### B. Push him too hard
**Wisdom (Insight) DC 13** to notice he is paying a cost for each attempt. He comes back from each "remembering" paler. If they push past three or four attempts in quick succession, he faints. **Wisdom (Medicine) DC 12** or any healing spell stabilizes him. On waking, he looks at the party and for ten full seconds does not know who they are. Μίρα sees this happen.

#### C. Comfort Μίρα
She is unraveling. Persuasion or simply kindness (no check needed — play it out) steadies her. If the party commits to helping Θωμάς over the coming days, she gives them her quiet, total trust. This unlocks the *low-DC path* into **Thread 8** (Κάλχας's journals — Μίρα is Κάλχας's cousin by marriage, and a word from her opens his door).

#### D. Mention Σίλας to Θωμάς off-compass
Θωμάς remembers him the way everyone else does — dead ten years in a barfight, the tragic widower who lost his daughter Κόρη first. His voice softens. *«Ο Σίλας; Ο Θεός να τον έχει καλά. Ήμουν παιδί όταν έγινε ο καβγάς, αλλά το θυμάμαι. Η Μίρα λέει ότι έκλαιγε όλη η πλατεία.»* No flicker. No dissonance. The altered-timeline memory is what his mind reaches for first.

The party should feel the difference between this and 2.A as a *mechanic of the compass.* Without it, Θωμάς is just another villager. With it, he is the only other witness who can hold both truths. Make that contrast legible in play.

### Payoff
- Θωμάς becomes the party's first non-compass witness who can *perceive* edits.
- Μίρα becomes a reliable ally with a stake in everything.
- Unlocks lower-DC access to Thread 8.
- Roleplay gold.

### Θωμάς deterioration clock

Θωμάς degrades whether or not the party visits — but the compass (brought within 10 ft for a sustained period, or set on the table during the conversation in Thread 2.A) *holds* his decline. Lock the clock when they are actively with him; resume it when they leave. Each stage is a ready-to-run snapshot.

| Time | State | Read-aloud hook | Mechanical notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Πρωί (morning) — when Μίρα arrives** | *Permeable.* He recognizes them, loses the thread every ~30 seconds, catches it again. Poured himself tea he doesn't remember pouring. Calm, gentle, frightened. | *«Είστε... ήρθατε για το τσάι;»* (lore line) | Baseline. No check needed to speak to him. Insight DC 13 notices the cost of each "remembering." |
| **Μεσημέρι (midday) — ~3 hrs later** | *Drifting.* Has started to mistake Μίρα for his mother once or twice and then apologize, horrified. Will not eat. Sits by the window and watches the road as if waiting for someone he cannot name. | *«Περιμένω κάποιον. Ξέρω πως περιμένω κάποιον. Αλλά δεν θυμάμαι το όνομα.»* — *«Με σπρώχνει έξω. Κάτι με σπρώχνει έξω απ' το σπίτι μου.»* | Insight DC 12 (easier — it is plain now). If the party brings the compass he stabilizes to morning levels for as long as it stays. Without it, any conversation costs him **1 level of exhaustion** (no save). |
| **Απόγευμα (afternoon)** | *Fading.* Lies down of his own accord "just for a little while" and cannot be roused without effort. Speaks in short fragments. Says the word *«Σίλας»* once, then forgets he said it. Μίρα is openly crying now. | *«Μίρα... ποια είσαι;»* (he says it quietly, as if asking a question he already knows the answer to and is ashamed of) | **Exhaustion 2.** Medicine DC 13 or any healing spell halts further decline for one hour. Lay on Hands heals exhaustion at this stage (per DM ruling — reward the paladin). |
| **Σούρουπο (dusk) — Μίρα's second visit** | *Detaching.* If the party has done nothing, Μίρα arrives at wherever they are, dry-eyed and terrible. *«Έρχεστε; Ή πρέπει να τον κρατήσω μόνη μου τη νύχτα;»* Do not let this moment be easy. This is the last free save. | *«Αν φύγει απόψε, θα πάει μόνος του. Σας παρακαλώ.»* | **Exhaustion 3.** If the party still refuses or delays, mark it on paper. Μίρα's trust is now on a one-way door. |
| **Νύχτα (night)** | *Gone-ish.* Not dead. Not present. Eyes open. Does not speak. Responds to touch but not to names — not even his own. Μίρα sits with him in the dark. | No line. Silence is the line. | **Exhaustion 4.** Magical healing (Lesser Restoration, Greater Restoration, or a full long rest with the compass on the bedside table) will pull him back from here — *once*. A second slide to this stage cannot be reversed. |

**If the party engages**: Each hour they spend with him (compass within 10 ft, set on the table or held nearby) he rolls back one stage toward Πρωί. A full long rest adjacent to the compass restores him to baseline, with a new **permanent trait** — he is the party's first non-compass witness who can *perceive* edits. This is their reward.

### Consequence if ignored

**This is the only thread with real in-world harm for neglect.**

The party has been asked directly for help by a pregnant woman whose husband they already rescued once. If they refuse or deprioritize:

- **By dusk**: Θωμάς is at Exhaustion 3 (see clock). Μίρα comes to find them a second time. If they refuse her again — mark it. She will not trust them with anything ever again.
- **By end of session**: If they never check on him, Θωμάς is at Exhaustion 4. Μίρα is alone with a man who does not know her.
- **Between sessions (if utterly abandoned)**: Θωμάς dies in his sleep. Μίρα miscarries from grief within ten days. The party has lost their first friend in the village — and they were warned. **This is dark fantasy's rule: choices have weight.** Do not apply this lightly; only if the players *actively* abandon Θωμάς after being directly asked twice.

| Check | DC |
|---|---|
| Insight (reading Θωμάς's cost) | 13 |
| Medicine (stabilize after collapse) | 12 |
| Persuasion (Μίρα — gentle) | 10 or no check |

## Thread 3 — Οι Επιπλέον Τάφοι του Αδελφού Ορέστη

**Entry**: Village gossip mentions "too many graves lately." Or they follow Σίλας to the burying field. Or they visit Το Λεπτό Παρεκκλήσι directly.

### Read-aloud — entering the chapel (Greek)

> Το Λεπτό Παρεκκλήσι είναι ακριβώς αυτό — μια μόνο αίθουσα με ένα ξύλινο βωμό, μερικά κεριά που καίνε αργά, και μια φτηνή εικόνα ενός θεού της σοδειάς που έχει ξεθωριάσει μέχρι να μη φαίνεται πια το πρόσωπό του. Ο Αδελφός Ορέστης κάθεται στον πρώτο πάγκο με τα χέρια στο πρόσωπο. Μυρίζει φθηνό κρασί, αν και είναι ακόμα πρωί. Όταν σας ακούει, σηκώνει το κεφάλι — και για μια στιγμή φαίνεται ανακουφισμένος που κάποιος, επιτέλους, ήρθε.

### Orestis's state

- Half-drunk, exhausted, terrified, *desperate* to tell someone.
- Wept in his sleep last night.
- Counts the village every morning. The number changes.
- **He is waiting for permission to break.** A kind word from the party and he spills everything.

### The burying field

Greek read-aloud:
> Ο Αδελφός σας οδηγεί ανάμεσα στους τάφους με το φανάρι του να τρέμει, αν και είναι πρωί. Σταματά μπροστά σε τρεις πέτρες. Δείχνει την πρώτη, τη δεύτερη, την τρίτη. «Αυτές ήταν εχθές. Και αυτές. Και αυτές.» Τα χώματα είναι φρέσκα. Οι επιγραφές είναι καθαρές. «Δεν θυμάμαι να έσκαψα κανέναν από αυτούς. Και σκάβω όλους τους τάφους του χωριού εγώ ο ίδιος, εδώ και δεκατέσσερα χρόνια.»

### The graves Orestis is pointing at

Orestis flags **three** graves as "new" — stones he did not carve but that now stand in his field, in earth he did not dig:

1. **Σίλας Ρέυν** — *died ~10 years ago, barfight.* Orestis stares at the stone and his face goes the color of old paper. *«Δεν... δεν θυμάμαι να τον έθαψα. Και όμως όλοι μου λένε ότι εγώ τον έθαψα. Σκάβω εδώ και δεκατέσσερα χρόνια. Πώς δεν θυμάμαι μια νύχτα με μαχαίρι στο πανδοχείο;»* This is the party's hardest-hitting confirmation: Orestis is *supposed* to have buried the man, and doesn't remember doing it, because in the unedited timeline he never did.
2. **Μάριος Λιβέρης** — *died ~5 years ago.* Cross-references the ledger entry in Thread 1. Orestis does not know the name at all and is ashamed.
3. **Unnamed** — *a small grave with dates only.* Orestis calls it *«του παιδιού χωρίς όνομα.»* He made the headstone himself, three days ago, and does not remember doing it. The dates suggest a child less than a year old.

**And beside Σίλας's stone**, if the party look (Perception DC 10 — easy, they are already staring), a smaller, older, weathered stone Orestis walks past without flagging because it has "always been there": **Κόρη Ρέυν**, *age 9, died about a year before her father.* Orestis accepts her as normal. The party should not. **Σίλας had a daughter** — this is the emotional revelation of the session. Vanya, a mother who lost a child, chose the inn of a father who lost a child. The parallel is not an accident.

### Investigation checks

- **Religion DC 12**: The graves are real. No necromancy. No undead. Just graves. Which makes them *worse.*
- **Investigation DC 13**: The earth is disturbed consistently, like proper burial — but the grass around each grave matches the grass *between* them, as though the lawn grew over the edits to hide them.
- **History or Arcana DC 15**: This is not a ritual. This is the land *healing over* something being inserted.
- **Passive Perception 12** (or any PC who lingers): **Σίλας's headstone is wrong in a way that looks right.** The carved date says ten winters ago. The lichen on the stone is two or three winters' worth at most — the kind of pale green crust that takes a few seasons, not a decade. The stone was *placed* when the edit landed, not when he died. Orestis does not notice — the lichen matches his memory of how old the stone is supposed to be, and memory beats eyes. If the party flag it to him, he touches the lichen, goes very quiet, and says *«Πώς δεν το είδα;»* This beat is the party's first *physical* evidence that edits leave fingerprints. Once they know what to look for, they can find more in later sessions.

### If the party digs

**This is the scenario you should be most ready for.** A sharp party will not just look at the graves — they will want to know what's *in* them. The answer matters, because the two edit-types (relocation vs. erasure) leave physically different traces, and the discovery will reshape the party's understanding of what Vanya actually does to the world.

**Social cost first.** Digging up graves in a tight village is desecration. To do it safely they need either: (a) Orestis's consent, which they can get with honesty (Persuasion DC 12 once he trusts them — he is *already* terrified and wants the truth), or (b) to do it at night in secret (Stealth DC 14 for the whole party; failure means a witness, and by morning every villager will know, and the party's welcome in Δρυόχωρο is effectively over). Make the choice weigh.

**Physical work.** Each grave is 2 hours of digging for one person, 1 hour for two, 30 minutes for three. Dirty, real labor. Make them feel it. Athletics DC 10 to avoid 1 level of exhaustion per grave worked.

#### Σίλας Ρέυν's grave — the edit's true price

There is a body. It is Σίλας.

But his body is *wrong in a way that should not be possible:*

- **The flesh.** Not a decade-old corpse. Not even weeks-old. **Medicine DC 12**: this man died *within the last two or three days.* The skin has begun to cool and stiffen but not to decompose properly. He is in a state a body should not be in under ten feet of settled earth marked by a ten-winter stone.
- **The wounds.** A stab wound under the ribs, clean-edged, and three defensive cuts across the left forearm. **Medicine DC 14**: consistent with a tavern brawl — a blade pulled at close range, hands raised too late. The wounds were inflicted *shortly before death*, and death was recent.
- **His clothes.** The same rough linen shirt the party saw him in two nights ago at Το Κούφιο Ελάφι, behind his own bar. *Exactly* the same. One of the PCs may recognize a small detail — a stain, a patched elbow.
- **In his pocket.** A small brass key, warm to the touch. It fits the cellar door of Το Κούφιο Ελάφι — the door Γερο-Αλέξης now uses without thinking. Γερο-Αλέξης has no idea where *his* key came from. If the party tries both keys in the lock, Σίλας's key turns cleanly; Αλέξης's key is the duplicate.

**What this means (for the DM, and for the party to piece together):**

Vanya's edit did not merely rewrite memory. It **killed him to match the edit.** When the temporal-relocation edit landed, the thin spot reached into reality and made the lie *true* — it took Σίλας out of the world at the moment the edit fired (the cliffhanger in Session 1) and dropped his body into a grave ten years in the past to make everyone's new memories physically consistent. The lichen on the stone is recent because the stone is recent. The body is recent because he was killed *recently*. The grave "is ten years old" only in the minds of everyone who accepted the edit.

**The party drank with a man who is now dead because of it.** They may feel guilty. They should not be. But they will.

**The horror beat**: at the moment any PC voices the realization — *"this body is days old, not years"* — **the compass (wherever it is, whoever the bearer is) goes suddenly *cold* for ten full seconds, then warm again.** If the bearer is at the graveside, they feel it directly. If the bearer is somewhere else in the village (the Νεφέλη cottage, Κέλης's stable), they feel a chill in their pocket and turn around. The thin spot noticed them notice. The real Vanya, wherever she is, now knows that someone in Δρυόχωρο is catching her work in the act. She will move accordingly in the next session.

#### Μάριος Λιβέρης's grave — the other kind of edit

**The grave is empty.** Not disturbed, not robbed — empty. The earth has been turned and packed exactly as if a body were placed in it, but there is no body, no shroud, no bones, no dust. Just soil, all the way down.

**Medicine / Investigation DC 13**: There is no trace that a body was *ever* here. No oils, no rot-ring in the earth, no coffin splinters, nothing organic has decayed into this soil. The grave is a shape without a content.

**What this means**: Μάριος was «διεγράφη» — fully erased. The thin spot's erasure edits are *cheaper* than relocations: no body to fabricate, no age to fake, just a lie the land holds for as long as the lie is held. Dig and the lie shows its bottom.

**Cross-reference payoff**: the party now has physical proof of **two distinct edit types**. Σίλας was killed to make the lie real. Μάριος was wiped and the grave is a prop. This is the deepest single revelation available in Session 2 — it tells the party Vanya's craft has a *cost curve* and a *menu*. She chooses how much to spend. That's worth 100 bonus XP and inspiration for everyone present.

#### Κόρη Ρέυν's grave — the heartbreaker

The grave holds **real, old bones.** A child of eight or nine, dead some nine or ten years by honest decomposition. No trace of edit. **Religion DC 10**: this is a real burial, honestly mourned at the time, and whoever laid her here loved her.

**Κόρη was never edited.** She really did die, a decade ago, a year before her father should have. Σίλας's grief was real in the original timeline too — the village just forgot *him*, not her. Vanya's edit landed cleanly on Σίλας *because the grief-shaped hole for him was already there.* Her daughter's grave is the reason the edit worked. The party has just uncovered the moral weight of what it means to pick a target who is already broken.

If a PC is religious, they should feel the urge to say a word over her stone. Let them.

#### The unnamed child's grave — the wrong kind of empty

The smallest grave. Dig it and find, wrapped in a strip of undyed linen, a **small bundle of rags in the rough shape of an infant**, with a shaped lump of river clay where the head should be. No bones. No body. No decomposition. A folk-memory of a baby, not a baby.

**Arcana / Religion DC 14**: This is what an edit looks like when the thin spot tries to fabricate a death *that never happened at all*. Not a relocation (no body to move). Not an erasure (there was no one to erase). An **invention** — a death the thin spot tried to plant to see if the village would grow to fit it. Orestis carved the headstone in his sleep because the thin spot pushed him to.

**Why it matters**: the real Vanya is not just editing people who exist. She is testing whether the thin spot will let her *invent a loss* and insert it into a family who never had one. If she can do this, she can invent a *gain* too — and that is the shape of her real plan. She is looking for a way to invent her dead child back into the world without borrowing the child from anyone else. **This is the campaign's deepest buried clue**, and it's in a rag doll under a foot of dirt. A clever party deserves to find it.

Show Αδελφός Ορέστης the rag doll and he will vomit. Quietly. Then he will take it from them with shaking hands and ask to be left alone with it. Do not push him. He will be different for the rest of the session.

### Consequences of digging — reputation

- **With Orestis's consent**: no village fallout. He will cover for them. The work still costs them part of an afternoon or evening and emotional weight.
- **Without consent, unwitnessed**: no immediate fallout, but the earth-disturbance is obvious at dawn and villagers will gossip. Mira, Kalchas, and Nefeli all hear. Mira is hurt; Kalchas closes ranks (Thread 8 gets harder); Nefeli is *impressed* and trusts them more, because she understands.
- **Without consent, witnessed**: the village turns cold by noon. Γερο-Αλέξης will not meet their eyes. Winter Fair invitations get quietly withdrawn. The party has made a choice to be strangers here. Let that be real.

### Orestis, if enlisted

If the party offers comfort and brings him into their confidence (Persuasion DC 12 or good roleplay), Orestis becomes a **hidden asset**:
- He agrees to start hiding small objects connected to each strange grave — a bit of cloth from near the headstone, a pressed flower, a chip of the stone itself. So that if the graves disappear in another edit, some trace remains.
- He agrees to keep his count secret from the other villagers, who would call him mad.
- He will pray quietly for the party. In a valley with a wound in the world, this is not nothing.

### Payoff
- **Κόρη Ρέυν** as the emotional revelation. Σίλας lost a child. That is why Vanya — a mother who lost a child — chose *his* inn.
- **Σίλας's displaced grave** as the signature proof of the temporal-relocation edit: a man Orestis never buried, in ground Orestis never broke.
- Cross-reference confirmation of **Μάριος Λιβέρης** from the ledger.
- Orestis as a long-term ally with a specific job.
- A growing catalogue of known edits: Σίλας (relocated), Ηλίας Μαρίνου, Μάριος Λιβέρης, the unnamed child, and the fourth unknown name. Κόρη Ρέυν sits apart — she is *collateral,* not a target.

### Consequence if ignored
- Orestis drinks through the afternoon. By dusk he is blind-drunk in the chapel, crying openly.

- Lost: the Κόρη Ρέυν revelation, the grave-artifact preservation strategy, and Orestis as an ally.

## Thread 4 — Η Πυξίδα Ξυπνά *(passive observation, always on)*

**Entry**: The compass acts throughout the day. No specific scene — this thread is a *reward for attention.* Weave it into whichever other threads the party pursues.

### The compass's reactions

| Location / NPC | Reaction | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| Near **Μαρίνα** at Τα Είδη | Warms, faint tick | She is an edit victim (thin-spot casualty) |
| Near **Θωμάς** | Warms strongly, steady tick | He is permeable to the thin spot |
| At **Οι Εννιά Φύλακες** | Irregular ticking, unsteady needle | Thin spot boundary |
| Visible **Το Μαύρο Δάχτυλο** from the ridge | Faint pull toward it (sunset/dawn only) | Tower is a secondary anchor point |
| Inside **Το Λεπτό Παρεκκλήσι** | *Silent and cold* | The chapel is somehow shielded — a mystery for later |
| Near **Μικρή Ιώ** | Warms, gentle tick | She is *something,* not yet defined |
| Near **Γερόντισσα Εστία** | Warms briefly when she hums the old verses | The language is tied to the containment |

### DM beats

- Reward players who carry the compass deliberately. If one PC holds it all session, make a point of mentioning its behavior each scene.
- **Do not narrate what the reactions mean.** Let them draw the inferences.
- **Passive Perception 13** catches the reactions without a check. Below 13, only narrate when they look at it.
- **Bearer required.** All of this only works for a PC actively carrying the compass on their person — see the Scene 0 compass rule. A compass stowed in a pack ticks to no one and warms an empty canvas. If the party stashes it, the whole thread goes dark and they get a memory-fade cost instead. Let the loss be felt.
- **Other PCs within ~10 ft** share only partial effects: they notice the compass reacting if they look at it, but they do not feel the warmth and do not get the passive perception catch. Non-bearers outside 10 ft are effectively ordinary villagers for the duration. This should guide tactical spacing during investigations — who walks into Νεφέλη's cottage, who stays at the door, who takes point with the bearer when approaching the standing stones.

### Payoff
- The compass is revealed to be an *instrument*, not just protection.
- Players learn to use it investigatively.
- The chapel's unexplained silence becomes a lingering mystery worth pursuing in Session 3 or later.

### Consequence if ignored
None — always available. This is a reward, not a punishment.

## Thread 5 — Το Τραγούδι της Μικρής Ιώς → Η Γερόντισσα Εστία

**Entry**: In the village square during the morning or afternoon.

### Read-aloud (Greek)

> Στην πλατεία του χωριού, τρία ή τέσσερα παιδιά παίζουν ένα παιχνίδι με βότσαλα. Το μικρότερο απ' αυτά — ένα κορίτσι με καστανές πλεξούδες, ίσως οκτώ χρονών — τραγουδάει καθώς παίζει. Οι στίχοι δεν είναι σε καμία γλώσσα που αναγνωρίζετε. Είναι εννιά στίχοι. Ή μήπως οκτώ. Το μέτρημα σας ξεφεύγει.

### Approaching Ιώ

She stops and looks at them. She is not afraid of adults. The other children scatter — this one does not.

Greek read-aloud:
> Η Ιώ σας κοιτάζει ήσυχα, σαν να περίμενε να σας δει. «Εσείς είστε οι καινούριοι.» Δεν είναι ερώτηση.

- If they are friendly, she asks to see "τη λάμψη" — the compass. She can sense it without seeing it.
- **If they let her hold the compass**:

Greek read-aloud:
> Το κοριτσάκι παίρνει την πυξίδα στα χέρια της σαν να είναι πουλί. Δεν κλαίει. Δεν τρομάζει. Κοιτάζει τον βάρδο για πολλή ώρα, ήσυχα, και μετά λέει, με φωνή που δεν ταιριάζει στην ηλικία της:
>
> «Εσύ είσαι ο άντρας που γράφει το γράμμα.»
>
> Και μετά, πιο απλά: «Μπορώ να δω τη μάνα μου τώρα;»

- **Give the bard's player inspiration.** Let this land. This is one of the best moments available in Session 2.
- Ιώ cannot explain the rhyme. She says *«Η γιαγιά Εστία ξέρει τα τραγούδια.»* (She does not mean literally her grandmother. Εστία is the village matriarch, loved by all.)
- Ιώ also dreams of *«η κυρία με τα κουρασμένα μάτια»* — Vanya. If asked, she describes the dream lady as *«κλαίει πολύ, αλλά δεν θέλει να τη φοβάμαι.»*

### Visiting Γερόντισσα Εστία

She lives in a small stone house at the edge of the village, near the chapel. She welcomes them if they show respect — Persuasion DC 10 or simply good manners. She serves herbal tea.

Greek read-aloud:
> Η Γερόντισσα Εστία σας κοιτάζει πάνω απ' το τσάι της με μάτια που έχουν δει ογδόντα χειμώνες. «Λοιπόν», λέει αργά. «Είστε οι ξένοι για τους οποίους μιλάει η Ιώ στον ύπνο της.» Δεν το εννοεί ως αστείο.

### What Εστία knows

- **The rhyme is old.** Older than the village. Her own grandmother taught it to her, who learned it from her grandmother.
- She can translate **seven** of the nine verses — each names one of the Watchers as *«φύλακας του πρώτου βήματος»*, *«φύλακας της στερνής αναπνοής»*, *«φύλακας εκείνου που δεν έγινε ποτέ»*, etc. Poetic-ritual.
- **The eighth verse**: she remembers the words but cannot translate them. *«Οι λέξεις είναι εκεί. Η σημασία τους έχει φύγει. Σαν κλειδί σε μια πόρτα που δεν υπάρχει πια.»*
- **The ninth verse**: she cannot remember at all.

Greek read-aloud — the critical line:
> «Υπάρχουν εννιά πέτρες», λέει ήσυχα, σαν να το παραδέχεται στον εαυτό της για πρώτη φορά. «Εγώ θυμάμαι οκτώ.»

### Unlocking Αριστόδημος

- **Persuasion DC 14** (or a well-roleplayed gentle moment with no check) gets Εστία to dig deeper into her memory.
- She remembers **Αριστόδημος Κρέων** — a young man from Καίλεν who came "many years ago" (she thinks twenty; it was closer to thirty). A scholar. He asked about the stones. He was *kind*. He left and never returned.

Greek read-aloud:
> «Ναι... ένας νέος άντρας από την Καίλεν. Έμεινε μια άνοιξη. Ρώτησε πολλά για τις πέτρες — τόσα που με κούρασε. Τον βρήκα συμπαθητικό. Έφυγε και... δεν ξαναγύρισε. Πόσα χρόνια έχουν περάσει τώρα; Είκοσι; Τριάντα; Δεν ξέρω πια.»

- If asked where he was from: *«Από μια σχολή που τη λέγανε Ακαδημία. Των Ωρών, νομίζω. Δεν ξέρω αν υπάρχει ακόμη.»*

### Εστία at the Watchers

If the party asks her to come to the stones with them, she goes. She walks slowly, leaning on a cane. At the circle, she touches the eighth stone and hums the eighth verse.

Greek read-aloud:
> Καθώς η Εστία τραγουδάει τον όγδοο στίχο, ο αέρας γύρω σας *αλλάζει*. Δεν είναι τρομαχτικό. Είναι σαν να μπαίνεις σε ένα δωμάτιο όπου κάποιος ακριβώς έχει σταματήσει να μιλάει. Η πυξίδα, όπου και να είναι, χτυπάει δυνατά — τρεις, τέσσερις, πέντε φορές — και μετά σταματάει. Η Εστία σταματάει το τραγούδι. Κοιτάζει την εννιά — ή μήπως την όγδοη — και η έκφρασή της είναι αδιάβαστη.
>
> «Κάτι λείπει», λέει σιγά. «Αλλά δεν ήξερα ότι το ήξερα μέχρι τώρα.»

She will not speak on the walk back. Respect that.

### Payoff
- **A missing Watcher confirmed** — the first concrete piece of proof about the thin spot's structure.
- **Αριστόδημος Κρέων surfaced** as a named lead to Καίλεν. Bridge to Part 2 established.
- **The Academy of Hours** named. Players can begin imagining the capital.
- Εστία as a trusted lore-source.
- The bard's private moment of being *known* — a character beat that will echo for the rest of the campaign.

### Consequence if ignored
- Εστία is old. She will be there next session. But the specific bridge — Αριστόδημος and the Academy — may not surface organically again. The party's path to Part 2 will have to come through Κάλχας's journals (Thread 8) or something you improvise later.
- The bard's *moment* with Ιώ is lost forever. You cannot replay the first time.

### Skill DCs
| Check | DC |
|---|---|
| Persuasion (meet Εστία with courtesy) | 10 or none |
| Persuasion (unlock Αριστόδημος memory) | 14 |
| Religion or History (recognize rhyme as ritual shape) | 13 |

## Thread 6 — Ο Κόρακας του Παλαιόδρυμου

**Entry**: Gossip at Η Βρεγμένη Κουρούνα or Το Κούφιο Ελάφι, or asking Γερο-Κέλης about strangers. *«Αν θες να ξέρεις για το δάσος, βρες τον Κόρακα. Μισότρελος. Μένει στην άκρη. Μη σφυρίξεις κοντά στην καλύβα του — το μισεί.»*

### Finding him

His cabin is at the Oldwood's edge, roughly a two-hour walk from the village on a rough hunters' trail. **Survival DC 13** to find it without a guide. Any of the Oldwood folk at Η Βρεγμένη Κουρούνα will point the way for the price of a drink.

Greek read-aloud — approaching the cabin:
> Η καλύβα βρίσκεται στην άκρη του δάσους, εκεί που τα δέντρα αρχίζουν να πυκνώνουν και η γη κάτω απ' τα πόδια σας μυρίζει σκοτεινή και υγρή. Είναι χαμηλή, σκοτεινή, χτισμένη από ξύλο που δεν έχει ποτέ δει μπογιά. Δέρματα ζώων κρεμονται να στεγνώνουν απ' τη στέγη.
>
> Δεν σας προσέχει κανείς. Αλλά ένας άντρας σας παρατηρεί από το μικρό παράθυρο πριν ακόμη τον δείτε. Κρατάει ένα τόξο. Δεν το υψώνει — ακόμη.

### Branches

#### A. Polite approach
He is wary but not hostile. **Persuasion DC 12**, or a visible glimpse of the compass (automatic), gets him to lower the bow and let them in.

#### B. Threatening or mocking approach
He puts an arrow into the doorframe between them. *«Φύγετε.»* Recovering the scene is very difficult.

#### C. Showing him the compass
**Automatic success.** His eyes change. He knows what it is, or knows what it *looks like*. He lowers the bow, opens the door, and says only: *«Μπείτε. Γρήγορα.»*

### Inside the cabin

Dim, smoke-stained, smell of resin and wet fur. He sits them down, offers bitter tea from a kettle, and speaks while cleaning a knife with a stone.

Greek read-aloud:
> Ο Κόρακας κάθεται απέναντί σας, καθαρίζοντας ένα μαχαίρι με μια πέτρα. Μιλάει χωρίς να σας κοιτάζει.
>
> «Υπάρχουν πράγματα στο δάσος τώρα που δεν πρέπει να 'ναι εκεί. Δεν έχουν πρόσωπα — όχι πραγματικά. Προσπαθούν να φτιάξουν ένα. Σε ακολουθούν. Σε ρωτούν πράγματα. Και αν τους δώσεις λάθος απάντηση, σου παίρνουν κάτι δικό σου για να γεμίσουν το κενό τους.»
>
> Κοιτάζει επιτέλους ψηλά. «Τα λέω τα χωρίς πρόσωπα. Δεν πάω μόνος μου βαθιά πια. Και αν έχετε μυαλό, ούτε κι εσείς.»

### The Woman in Two Places

If the party asks him about "strange women" or "a hooded traveler":

- He describes Vanya. Polite, tired, writing in a journal. **He saw her in the inn and in the woods simultaneously, two weeks ago.** He thought he was drunk. He was not. He has not told anyone until now.
- He will not name her — he does not know her name.

### What he gives them

- **A handful of iron nails**: *«Μερικές φορές τους κρατάνε έξω. Μερικές φορές όχι. Αλλά είναι καλύτερο απ' το τίποτα.»* (Gameplay: tossing a nail at an Unmade within 10 ft gives it disadvantage on its next attack. 6 nails. One-use each.)
- **A warning rhyme** — do not whistle in the Oldwood after dark.
- **The truth rule**: *«Αν σας ρωτήσει ένα απ' αυτά κάτι που δεν καταλαβαίνετε, μη φοβηθείτε. Απαντήστε με αλήθεια. Ακόμη κι αν είναι μια μαντεψιά. Είναι ό,τι πιο κοντά στο φάρμακο.»* **This is the key to Thread 7's grace-note resolution.**

### Payoff
- External validation of the party's experience. They are not alone.
- **Τα Άφτιαχτα named and explained** before the encounter.
- **The Woman in Two Places** — first external confirmation that Vanya is *wrong.*
- Six iron nails (mechanical consumable).
- The **truth rule** — critical for Thread 7.
- Ο Κόρακας as a potential future ally.

### Consequence if ignored
- By dusk, Ο Κόρακας packs a small kit and walks into the deep Oldwood. He does not return.
- Next session, finding him requires going deep into the wood, where Άφτιαχτα roam, **without** the iron nails, **without** the truth rule, and **without** his warnings. The first Άφτιαχτο encounter will be much harder, and the party may lose memories they would otherwise have kept.

### Skill DCs
| Check | DC |
|---|---|
| Survival (find the cabin) | 13 |
| Persuasion (open him up) | 12 |
| Showing the compass | automatic |

## Thread 7 — Το Πρώτο Άφτιαχτο

**Entry triggers** — any one of these activates it:

1. The party enters the Oldwood (hunting, seeking Ο Κόρακας deeper, chasing a rumor)
2. The party sleeps at the edge of the village with the compass actively out
3. **The dusk trigger**: At the end of the session's afternoon, as the light begins to fail, one of the party looks toward the treeline and sees *something.*

**Strongly recommended**: use trigger 3. Horror works best when you control the lighting. It also lets the encounter close the session at a time of relative safety, which fits the "soft close" direction.

### Read-aloud (Greek)

> Ο ήλιος πέφτει πίσω απ' τους Εννιά Αδελφούς και το Δρυόχωρο βυθίζεται σε μια υγρή μπλε σκιά. Καθώς περπατάτε — ή καθώς ο ένας από σας στέκεται στο κατώφλι και κοιτάζει το βουνό, ή καθώς ο βάρδος καπνίζει έξω απ' το πανδοχείο — κάτι κινείται στην άκρη του δάσους.
>
> Μια μορφή. Στέκεται ανάμεσα σε δύο δέντρα, ίσως πενήντα μέτρα μακριά. Δεν κινείται προς εσάς. Αλλά σας κοιτάζει — όσο μπορεί κάτι χωρίς πρόσωπο να κοιτάζει.
>
> Η πυξίδα ζεσταίνεται ξαφνικά. Τικ-τικ-τικ.

### What the party sees

- A humanoid shape, blurred at the edges as if seen through old glass.
- Face unformed — the suggestion of eyes, a mouth that shifts like wet clay trying to remember.
- It does not advance. It waits. It has been waiting.
- **Insight DC 13**: It is not hostile. It is *asking* for something.

### Branches — three paths

#### Path A — Attack (combat)

If the party attacks on sight, the Άφτιαχτο does not defend itself for one full round. It just *looks* at them. Any PC who strikes it in that round feels, for a heartbeat, a memory of *their own life* flicker and almost vanish — DC 12 Wis save, failure means they forget something small for the next hour (their childhood home's color, their mother's middle name — DM's choice). Reversible.

Then the creature defends itself.

**Stat block (CR 2, level-3 party of three: Medium-leaning-Hard as combat, designed to be winnable but costly)**:
- AC 13, HP 45, Speed 30 ft
- **Multiattack**: two Touch of the Unmade attacks per turn
- **Touch of the Unmade** (melee, +5 to hit, reach 5 ft): 1d8+2 psychic damage and target makes a DC 13 Wisdom save or takes 1 point of Wisdom drain (recovers on long rest)
- **Unmaking Gaze** (recharge 5–6, 1/combat): one creature within 30 ft makes a DC 13 Wisdom save or is frightened until the end of its next turn and loses one memory (see Path A text)
- **Resistances**: non-magical bludgeoning/piercing/slashing; psychic
- **Vulnerabilities**: radiant damage (double)
- **Compass aura**: within 10 ft of the compass, the Άφτιαχτο has disadvantage on all rolls and cannot flee
- **Iron nail**: a thrown nail within 10 ft gives disadvantage on its next attack (uses one of Rook's nails)

**Encounter math**: CR 2 ≈ 450 XP; for three PCs of level 3 this sits at Medium (450 threshold). The compass aura and radiant vulnerability (Divine Smite, radiant cantrips) give the party real tools — reward engagement. Without them, bump HP to 55 or call it Hard.

**Tactics**: it does not behave like a monster. It moves *toward* the bearer of the compass, slowly, as if drawn — the compass is the closest thing it has felt to *being remembered*. It will pass an unarmed PC to reach the bearer if forced to choose. Its Touch attacks aim for the face whenever possible — it does not understand that is monstrous; it is trying to learn what a face is. If radiant damage hits it, it flinches and pauses one round before attacking again — this is the closest it will come to fear. **It does not flee even at low HP** (the compass aura locks it). At ~10 HP it begins to lose coherence visually — its outline thins, its borrowed voice cracks. **Last-round opportunity for the grace note**: if a PC speaks to it kindly *as it is dying*, even mid-combat, you may switch to Path B in the moment. Reward this.

It fights to "death." On "death" it dissolves into fine gray ash that smells faintly of pine smoke and old rain. The party has killed a man who never quite was.

#### Path B — Speak (the grace note, strongly encouraged)

Greek read-aloud:
> Το πράγμα ανοίγει το στόμα του. Κάτι προσπαθεί να βγει — ένας ήχος που δεν είναι λέξη, που δεν θυμάται πώς να γίνει λέξη. Μετά, με μια φωνή δανεισμένη που είναι τρομαχτικά *γνώριμη* — η φωνή ενός περαστικού που είδατε χθες στο πανδοχείο, η φωνή ενός ξυλοκόπου που σας χαιρέτησε στο δρόμο — σας ρωτάει:
>
> *«Τι... ήμουνα... εγώ;»*

- **If the party answers with honesty and compassion** — even a guess — the thing *changes*. Its face resolves briefly. It remembers. Suggested dialogue:
  - *«Δεν ξέρουμε ποιος ήσουν. Αλλά κάποτε ήσουν κάποιος. Κάποιος σε αγαπούσε.»* → the thing trembles.
  - *«Ίσως ήσουν ξυλοκόπος. Ίσως δούλευες στον μύλο.»* → its face resolves into the face of a middle-aged man with a scar over one eyebrow. Its mouth opens. *«Ναι. Ναι, αυτό ήμουν.»*

- **If additionally a PC uses Lay on Hands, Healing Word, Bardic Inspiration, or any kind touch while speaking to it**: the restoration is complete for one full minute. The man thanks them in a voice they almost know. He tells them his name — *«Με έλεγαν... Άνδρο. Νομίζω.»* He remembers his wife. He weeps, once. Then he fades.

Greek read-aloud — the grace-note ending:
> Ο Άνδρος σας κοιτάζει με μάτια που είναι για μια στιγμή καθαρά. «Ευχαριστώ», λέει. «Νομίζω ότι... νομίζω ότι θυμάμαι. Πες στη γυναίκα μου — αν μπορέσεις να τη βρεις — πες της ότι δεν ήταν λάθος της.» Και μετά, σιγά σιγά, σαν ομίχλη στο πρώτο φως, ξεθωριάζει.

**Give the player who leads this beat inspiration.** This is one of the best moments available in Part 1, and whichever PC plays it well has *earned* a campaign-level character beat.

Note for later: **Άνδρος had a wife.** She is in the village. She does not remember him. This is a seed for Session 3 or 4.

#### Path C — Run

The thing does not pursue. But one PC (the one holding the compass least, or randomly) feels something briefly missing the next morning — a favorite color, a childhood song, a scar they don't remember getting or not getting. Reversible by deliberate remembering over time, or by reclaiming a similar moment.

### Payoff
- Signature monster debut.
- Players learn the Άφτιαχτα's mechanics through play.
- The compass's effect on them is revealed in action.
- **The grace note** — a moment of genuine dark-fantasy beauty. Worth more than any XP.
- A seed: Άνδρος's wife, somewhere in the village.

### Consequence if not triggered
- The Oldwood's population of Άφτιαχτα grows quietly.
- Next session, they are bolder: three of them at the treeline at dawn, and the party is no longer facing a solo encounter they can handle with roleplay. Combat becomes necessary.
- The grace note is *not available* with a group of three. The first time the party sees an Άφτιαχτο in grief is the only time it is this tender.

### Skill DCs
| Check | DC |
|---|---|
| Insight (read its intent) | 13 |
| Persuasion or Charisma (speak kindly effectively) | 12 |
| Religion (recognize it as a lost thing, not undead) | 13 |
| Wis save vs. memory flicker (if attacked) | 12 |

## Thread 8 — Τα Ημερολόγια του Βόσσου

**Entry**: Hardest to reach. Three ways in.

### Path A — Earn Κάλχας's trust directly

The party visits the mill, asks about the village's history, engages Κάλχας as a person rather than as a resource. He is suspicious, watchful, calculating — an outsider-wary man protecting the engine of his village.

Greek read-aloud (first meeting):
> Ο Κάλχας Βόσσος στέκεται στην αυλή του μύλου με τα χέρια σταυρωμένα. Είναι ένας μεγάλος άντρας, αργός στις κινήσεις αλλά γρήγορος στα μάτια. «Οι ξένοι που έσωσαν τον Θωμά», λέει ήρεμα. «Εντάξει. Τι θέλετε;»

- **Persuasion DC 16** to get him to speak about his family or his grandfather cold.
- **Persuasion DC 13** if they have a genuine in-world reason ("we think something is wrong with the village and we want to help").
- If they try to bribe him: **automatic fail.** He will not be bought.

### Path B — Μίρα vouches *(recommended, if Thread 2 went well)*

If the party helped Θωμάς with Thread 2 — actually helped, not just showed up — then Μίρα will, on her own initiative, send them to Κάλχας with a small handwritten note. *«Εμπιστέψου τους. Η Μίρα.»*

This drops the DC to **11** (or no check if they roleplay it well). Κάλχας, reading the note, goes very quiet for a moment, then says: *«Η Μίρα δεν εμπιστεύεται εύκολα. Καλώς. Ελάτε μέσα.»*

### Path C — Steal them

The mill house is Κάλχας's private residence attached to the mill. His study is on the second floor. Two foremen work nearby during the day; one sleeps in an outbuilding at night.

- **Stealth DC 15** to enter the house unseen
- **Sleight of Hand DC 14** to pick the study's door, **DC 16** to pick the chest itself
- **Failure cascade**: If caught, Κάλχας turns the foremen loose (non-lethal — they'll club and hold the intruders). Κάλχας closes the mill's doors to the party permanently. Word reaches the village by evening. Μίρα is horrified if she learns. Thread 2 may break depending on timing.

### The scene (if they earn access)

Greek read-aloud:
> Το γραφείο του Κάλχα μυρίζει καπνό πούρου και παλιό χαρτί. Ο ίδιος κάθεται πίσω από ένα βαρύ ξύλινο τραπέζι με τα χέρια σταυρωμένα. Σας μετράει για μια ολόκληρη μακριά στιγμή.
>
> «Ο παππούς μου κρατούσε ημερολόγια», λέει. «Τα βρήκα όταν πέθανε ο πατέρας μου, πριν από δεκαπέντε χρόνια. Δεν τα διάβασα όλα. Δεν τα ήθελα. Υπάρχει κάτι σε αυτά που... δεν μ' αρέσει. Αλλά αφού η Μίρα λέει πως μπορώ να σας εμπιστευτώ — πάρτε τα για μία νύχτα. Μόνο μία. Και μη ρωτήσετε άλλο τίποτα απ' το χωριό για τον παππού μου.»
>
> Ξεκλειδώνει ένα μακρύ ξύλινο σεντούκι. Μέσα, δεμένα με δέρμα, τρία χοντρά ημερολόγια με φθαρμένα εξώφυλλα.

### What the journals contain

Ninety years of spotty entries from Κάλχας's grandfather. Weather. Lumber prices. Family notes. And, interleaved, **descriptions of Ήσυχες Μέρες** that he witnessed and recorded.

Key findings, in order of difficulty to piece together:

1. **Easy (reading the first journal)**: The grandfather used a small circled X in the margin to mark any day that "felt wrong." There are dozens of them.

2. **Intelligence (Investigation) DC 14**: Charting the circled X's across all three journals reveals a **pattern of acceleration**. In the earliest journal (~90 years ago), Quiet Days appeared roughly every three years. By the middle journal, every year. In the current journal's most recent entries (~15 years ago), every three or four months. **The thin spot is bleeding faster.**

3. **Intelligence (History) DC 13**: Buried in one entry: *«Ήρθε ένας νέος άντρας από την Καίλεν σήμερα. Αριστόδημος τ' όνομά του. Ρώτησε καλές ερωτήσεις. Εξαφανίστηκε πριν μπορέσει να απαντήσει καμία.»*

4. **Investigation DC 13**: The last ten or so pages of the third journal have been **torn out.** The tear is recent — the paper edge is clean, not yellowed. **Κάλχας did not do this.** Someone has been in this chest within the last month.

5. **Hidden (Investigation DC 16, or a lucky player check)**: Tucked inside the back cover of the third journal, a single loose sheet of paper in a completely different hand, written recently:
   > *«Μην ψάξετε την ένατη πέτρα. Θα σας βρει εκείνη.»*

   Unsigned. The handwriting is unfamiliar. Whoever left this was warning someone — maybe the grandfather, maybe someone who came after him, maybe the party themselves.

### Payoff
- **Historical depth**: The thin spot has been bleeding for at least three generations. This isn't new.
- **Acceleration**: It is getting worse. Fast.
- **Αριστόδημος Κρέων confirmed** as a historical name (cross-references with Thread 5 if the party hit both).
- **New mystery**: Someone — not Κάλχας — is *actively interfering* with the Βόσσου family's records. A second fragment? A current villager complicit? The real Vanya? A third party the DM has not introduced yet?
- **The loose sheet** — an unknown ally, or an unknown enemy, warning someone off the ninth stone.

### Consequence if ignored
- Κάλχας does not think about the journals again. He is busy.
- **Next session**: if the party finally goes for them, the chest is *empty*. The journals are gone. Κάλχας swears on his father's grave he did not touch them. Someone — whoever has been interfering — has taken them. They are lost permanently as a resource.
- This is the thread with the *highest permanent-loss stakes*. Players who skip it lose a whole vein of lore.

# Scene 2 — Το Βράδυ *(soft close with a pull)*

- The in-world day is drawing to dusk anyway

### Read-aloud (Greek)

> Ο ήλιος έχει πέσει πίσω απ' τους Εννιά Αδελφούς. Το Δρυόχωρο ετοιμάζεται για ύπνο — καντήλια ανάβουν στα παράθυρα, ένα-ένα, σαν να απαντούν το ένα στο άλλο. Ο αέρας είναι κρύος και μυρίζει βρεγμένο καπνό. Ο Μυελός ακούγεται πιο δυνατά τώρα που όλα τα άλλα έχουν ησυχάσει.
>
> Κάθεστε [όπου η ομάδα έχει καταλήξει — πανδοχείο, σπίτι της Μίρας, καλύβα του Κόρακα, προσαρμόστε]. Η πυξίδα ήταν σιωπηλή το τελευταίο τέταρτο της ώρας.
>
> Και τότε χτυπάει. Μία φορά. Δυνατά. Σαν μικρή καμπάνα.
>
> Όποιος την κρατάει την αισθάνεται ξαφνικά — *ζεστή*. Πιο ζεστή απ' όσο ήταν όλη τη μέρα. Πιο ζεστή απ' όσο θα έπρεπε να είναι.
>
> Όταν την κοιτάξετε, η βελόνα — η βελόνα που δεν έχει δείξει τίποτα αφότου τη βρήκατε — *δείχνει*.
>
> Δείχνει βόρεια. Σταθερά. Πάνω, προς τους Αδελφούς. Προς το πέρασμα ανάμεσα στις δύο μικρές κορυφές.
>
> Προς το Μαύρο Δάχτυλο.

### DM beats

- **Let that sit.** Do not say anything more.
- If a player asks what they do: let them decide. The session ends here regardless. Anything they say is a declaration for *next session*.
- **No crisis.** No pursuit. No urgent timer. Just a direction.
- Next session opens with *what do we do about the Black Finger.*

### Optional additional hook (use only if pacing feels slow)

If the session has been too languid or the players seem disengaged, add this beat after the compass points:

> Ακούγεται ένα χτύπημα στην πόρτα. Όταν ανοίγετε, δεν υπάρχει κανείς — μόνο η Μικρή Ιώ που απομακρύνεται ήδη προς το σπίτι της. Κοντοστέκεται στη μέση του δρόμου, γυρίζει, και λέει, χωρίς να υψώσει τη φωνή:
>
> *«Η κυρία με τα κουρασμένα μάτια σας είδε σήμερα. Είπε να προσέχετε.»*
>
> Και μετά συνεχίζει τον δρόμο της σαν να μην είπε τίποτα.
