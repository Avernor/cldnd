*(The Reyne House. Self-contained mini-dungeon: empty upstairs + active witch's lab below. Real combat, real loot, the campaign's first hard intel on Vanya.)*

## Premise — what's actually going on

**Σίλας was relocated by Vanya's edit. He is gone.** No echo, no haunting, no hidden journal he kept. He was a widowed innkeeper raising a daughter alone, and Κόρη died years ago of fever. He never knew about the compass, the thin spot, or Vanya. **He was telling the party the truth in Session 1.** Vanya did not erase a fellow investigator — she erased a tired, grieving, *useful* man.

**Why she chose this house**: Σίλας was already isolated. Locals stopped visiting after Κόρη died. The house sits between the chapel and the river, away from the main square. Once Vanya relocated his death backward, **no one looks at this house anymore.** The village does not even register it as a place. It is, by design, **invisible.**

**What Vanya does here**: she has converted the cellar into a working laboratory. This is where she pushes fragments through the thin spot, prepares vessels, plans her edits. She is not here right now — fragments do not stay long. But the work is **active.** Tools laid out. Candles half-burned. A vessel-pair waiting on slabs.

The party is about to walk into the **only place in Δρυόχωρο where Vanya leaves physical evidence of herself.**

## Exterior

A small two-story timber house with moss-dark shingles. Windows boarded from outside. Overgrown garden — except a thin path through the weeds to the front door and around the side to the cellar door. **Recent.** Someone has been walking here.

### Compass behavior

Goes **cold** at the property line, then **warm** at the front door, then **cold again** when they walk around to the cellar door. The cellar reads as the strongest cold spot — the active edit zone. The party should notice the cellar is the interesting part *before they enter.*

### Local reactions

If a villager is asked about this house, they look at it, hesitate (struggling to focus on it), then say something vague: *«Άδειο. Από τότε που πέθανε ο Σίλας. Άδειο.»* Then change the subject. **Insight DC 10**: they are not lying. They genuinely cannot hold the house in their attention.

### Read-aloud (Greek) — first sight

> Το σπίτι είναι μικρότερο απ' όσο νομίζατε. Δύο πατώματα, καμινάδα στραβή, παράθυρα καρφωμένα με σανίδες. Ο κήπος μπροστά είναι γεμάτος αγριόχορτο.
>
> Μέσα στ' αγριόχορτο, ένα μονοπάτι. Πατημένο. Πρόσφατο.
>
> Όχι ένα μονοπάτι. **Δύο.** Ένα προς την μπροστινή πόρτα. Ένα τριγύρω προς τα πίσω, που χάνεται κάτω από τη γωνία του σπιτιού.

## Ground Floor (mostly empty, atmosphere + clues, NO combat)

The ground floor is the **lull before the basement.** Σίλας's life is here, frozen and dusty. Quiet, sad, normal-haunted. There is nothing alive on this floor.

### Foyer
- Cold, breath fogs (it's barely warmer than outside)
- Hat rack: a small child's coat (Κόρη's). Adult coat hooks empty
- Pair of muddy adult **woman's** boots by the door. *Fresh mud* — within 2-3 days. Small foot. **Not Σίλας's.** Plant.
- Family portrait on the wall: Σίλας, a woman (dead long before the campaign), a little girl (Κόρη, ~7). Dust covers the frame evenly. **No one is wiping this.** Σίλας really is gone.

### Sitting room
- Furniture under sheets. A long-dead hearth.
- A rocking chair, motionless. (No supernatural movement — Σίλας is not here.)
- A small bookshelf — children's stories, a hymnbook, an almanac. Nothing odd.
- **Investigation DC 13**: a single drinking glass beside the hearth, freshly washed and placed back. **Someone has been drinking water here recently.** Not eating. Just water.

### Kitchen
- Pantry mostly empty. A jar of preserved figs from 1-2 years ago, still sealed and edible.
- A child's drawing pinned to the wall — three stick figures: a man, a woman, a small girl. **Dust over it evenly.** No tampering. This is Κόρη's actual drawing of her family, dated by what she would have been able to draw.
- A bucket of water on the floor. **Fresh.** Someone is fetching water for use elsewhere in the house. Where?
- **Investigation DC 14**: the floor at the back of the kitchen, near the cellar door, has been **swept.** Other parts of the floor are dust-thick. The path between the side window, the kitchen, and the cellar door is the part being maintained.

### Cellar door (interior)
At the back of the kitchen. Iron-banded oak. **Locked.** Examined closely:
- **Arcana DC 13**: a small chalk symbol on the doorframe — newly drawn. A *«μην κοιτάς»* ward. Low-grade. Pulls eyes away from the door.
- The symbol is **the same** as one drawn in Vanya's hand in her journal at the tower — same maker (if they have other examples to compare later, plant this).
- The lock itself: **DC 17** thieves' tools (it's a proper one, not a household lock) or **DC 18 STR** to break (loud — the vessels wake immediately).
- **A key exists.** It is upstairs in Σίλας's bedroom dresser, where Vanya has been keeping it. DC 13 Investigation to find when they search his room.

## Second Floor (atmosphere, one optional encounter, NO required combat)

### Hallway
Three doors. Left to right: «Κόρη» (carved in child's hand), master bedroom, small office.

### Encounter — Echo of Κόρη (optional, grace-note only)

As they reach the top of the stairs, a small girl is sitting on the floor of the hallway playing with a rag doll. **Age ~7.** She does not look up. She is humming.

This is **not** a residue of Vanya's edits — Κόρη died years before any edit. This is a much older haunt, predating Vanya. It is **not Vanya's work.** The party doesn't know that yet.

She is an Άφτιαχτο in flavor, but mechanically: **she is not hostile, will not become hostile, and cannot be attacked usefully.** Attacks pass through her like she's smoke. She is a memory of the house that the house cannot let go of.

#### Read-aloud (Greek)

> Στη μέση του διαδρόμου, ένα κοριτσάκι κάθεται σταυροπόδι. Κρατάει μια κουκλίτσα. Σιγοτραγουδάει, χαμηλά, ένα τραγουδάκι που δεν αναγνωρίζετε.
>
> Δεν σηκώνει το βλέμμα όταν περνάτε. Δεν φοβάται. Δεν πειράζει.
>
> Η πυξίδα **κρυώνει** γι' αυτήν — αλλά ελάχιστα. Σαν να μη ξέρει αν είναι κάτι που χρειάζεται προσοχή.

#### What she gives if engaged
- She is friendly but not lucid. Talks like a 7-year-old.
- *«Ο μπαμπάς δεν είναι σπίτι. Δεν είναι σπίτι πολύ καιρό τώρα.»*
- If they ask when her mother is coming home: *«Η μαμά πέθανε. Το ξέρω. Είμαι μεγάλη.»*
- If they ask what happened to her father: *«Έφυγε. Όχι σαν τη μαμά. Άλλη φύγη.»* — she has a word for *erasure* that she did not learn from anyone living
- If they ask about the basement: she stops humming. Looks at the floor. *«Εκείνη πάει εκεί. Δεν μου αρέσει εκείνη.»* **«Εκείνη» = Vanya.** First in-fiction confirmation that a woman has been visiting this house regularly.
- If they show her the compass: she stares. *«Είναι σαν εκείνο που κρατάει. Αλλά πιο μικρό.»* — Vanya carries something *like* the compass. Plant.

#### Grace note (Lay on Hands / Healing Word / Bardic Inspiration + speak her name)
She smiles. *«Α. Καλά.»* And fades.
- **Reward**: she leaves the rag doll. If a player picks it up, it carries a small charm: **+1 to a single Wisdom save against thin-spot effects, one use.** (Like the Εστία charm — don't tell the players the mechanic.)
- **Inspiration** to whoever did the grace note.

#### If they don't grace-note her
She is still there when they come back. She is fine. The horror is that she will be there forever.

### Κόρη's bedroom
- **Locked from the inside.** DC 12 to force.
- Inside: a child's bedroom, dust-thick. Bed neatly made years ago, never disturbed since. Dolls on a shelf. A small wooden box under the bed.
- The box contains: a lock of dark hair tied with a faded red ribbon, a piece of paper with a date — Κόρη's actual death-date. **No discrepancy.** Her death is *real.* Σίλας's death is the edit, not hers.

### Σίλας's bedroom
- Bed unmade — **years ago.** Sheets musty. No one has slept here in a long time.
- An almost-empty wardrobe. Σίλας's clothes hang neatly. Two heavy work shirts missing — someone has taken them. **The mud-tracking woman wears one of these.**
- **Dresser, top drawer, under a folded undershirt**: a small iron key on a leather thong. **The cellar key.** Investigation DC 13. (Or DC 11 if they think to specifically look.)
- The room is otherwise empty of clues. **Important**: there are no investigation notes, no hidden journal, no secret attic. Σίλας was not investigating anything. Reinforce this by giving the party *normal* personal effects only.

### Σίλας's office
- A small writing desk. Drawers contain: ledger entries from the inn (kept here for tax purposes), receipts, an unfinished letter to a cousin in Καίλεν asking after his health, dated three years ago, never sent.
- This is **mundane.** A real person's leftover life. Let it sit.

## The Cellar — Vanya's Laboratory *(the meat of the dungeon)*

Lit by candles when they descend. The candles **are lit when the party arrives** — even if Vanya is not here. She does not bother to put them out. No one would see.

### Entry

If they use the key (found upstairs): the door opens silently. They have surprise on whatever is inside.
If they break the door: **the vessels wake immediately and stand.** No surprise, the fight starts as soon as they're on the stairs.

### Read-aloud (Greek) — descending

> Η σκάλα κατεβαίνει σε ένα δωμάτιο που δεν θα έπρεπε να ήταν εδώ.
>
> Το ταβάνι είναι χαμηλό. Οι τοίχοι είναι πέτρα — όχι ξύλο. Κάποιος έχει σκάψει πιο βαθιά απ' όσο είχε πραγματικά αυτό το σπίτι.
>
> Κεριά καίνε. Οχτώ, δέκα, ίσως δώδεκα. Όλα ίδιου ύψους. Άναψαν την ίδια ώρα. Πριν από λίγο.
>
> Στο κέντρο του δωματίου, στο πάτωμα, **ένας κύκλος.** Σχεδιασμένος με κιμωλία και αλάτι και κάτι σκουρότερο. Διπλός. Με σύμβολα μέσα του που πονάει το μάτι να κοιτάξει.
>
> Στους τοίχους, χάρτες. Σημειώσεις. Ένα σπασμένο καθρέφτη. Στον πάγκο, βάζα με υγρά που δεν θα έπρεπε να ισορροπούν.

### The vessels (combat encounter — **cannot be talked out of**)

**Two empty bodies** lie on stone slabs at the sides of the room. They are missing villagers Vanya has prepared as vessels — she intends to push fragments into them when she is ready. Currently they are **mindless, soulless, hostile to anything not Vanya.**

- **Slab A**: A man, mid-30s, builder's hands. **A player who has been to the Crossing or asked about missing people will recognize him**: he is **Στέφανος** — the "drowned" name from Ορέστης's grave list. He is not drowned. He is here.
- **Slab B**: A woman, ~50, gardener's hands. **Unfamiliar** — not on Ορέστης's list. From a neighboring valley, taken on a road. Plant: Vanya is operating beyond Δρυόχωρο too.

### When the fight starts

- If they entered with the key, the vessels remain still until disturbed. The party gets to investigate first. **When any PC touches a slab, the vessel sits up.** The other rises immediately after.
- If they broke the door, both rise as the party enters.

### Read-aloud (Greek) — first move

> Ο [Στέφανος] ανοίγει τα μάτια. Είναι **άδεια.** Όχι μαύρα, όχι λευκά — απλώς δεν υπάρχει κανείς πίσω τους. Σηκώνεται από την πλάκα σαν να μη ζύγιζε τίποτα.
>
> Δεν λέει τίποτα. Δεν αναπνέει. Κοιτάζει.
>
> Και μετά **κινείται.**

### Stat block — Vessel (CR 1)

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| AC | 12 |
| HP | 28 |
| Speed | 30 ft |
| STR / DEX / CON / INT / WIS / CHA | 14 / 12 / 12 / 1 / 6 / 1 |
| Saves | Wis +0, Cha -5 (cannot be charmed or frightened — has no self) |
| Damage resistance | Bludgeoning, piercing, slashing from non-magical attacks |
| Damage vulnerability | Radiant |
| Damage immunity | Psychic (no mind to harm) |
| Condition immunity | Charmed, frightened, exhaustion |
| Senses | Blindsight 30 ft, Passive Perception 8 |
| Languages | — |

**Actions**:
- **Multiattack**: Two **Empty Slam** attacks
- **Empty Slam**: Melee, +4 to hit, reach 5 ft. *Hit*: 1d8+2 bludgeoning. On a critical hit, target makes DC 12 Constitution save or be **grappled** — the vessel does not let go even after death.

**Special — Cannot be reasoned with**:
- These bodies have no identity, no name, no memory to name. **Grace-note paths do not work.** Speaking a name (even Στέφανος) does *nothing*. Healing magic *hurts* them like radiant.
- This is the design point. The party has had two grace-note victories already (Άφτιαχτο in Thread 4, Κόρη upstairs). **Vanya's vessels are the hard wall.** They must be fought.

**Combat difficulty for level 3 party of three**: tuned to a hard fight. ~CR 1 each, two of them. The party should feel pressure. A grappling vessel can pin a softer PC and tear them apart. Use the grapple aggressively.

**Loot from the vessels**: nothing they wear or carry is meaningful. They are wearing rough clothes Vanya found for them.

### After the fight

The vessels collapse. They do not get up again. The bodies are *almost human* — the party may want to bury them, return them, or burn them. Each option is meaningful:

- **Bury them**: Ορέστης will help with this. A real burial. He weeps.
- **Return them to the village**: causes panic. Probably bad idea.
- **Burn them**: practical, dark. Νεφέλη approves quietly: *«Δεν ήταν πια άνθρωποι. Σωστά κάνατε.»*
- **Leave them**: they are still there next session. Vanya will collect them. **The party loses this lead.**

## The Lab — Investigating Vanya's Workspace

After the fight, the party has ~15 minutes of in-fiction time before they should leave (see Closing pressure below). They can search the room. Make every item worth finding.

### Desk
- **Vanya's working notebook** (the major intel — see contents below)
- An ink bottle, half-empty. The ink is darker than normal. Smells faintly of iron.
- A folded square of black silk wrapping a sealed envelope. **The envelope has no name on it.** Inside: a single line in her hand: *«Όχι ακόμα.»* Meant for someone or something. Plant.
- Three quill pens. One has a strand of long dark hair caught in it.

### Workbench
- Glass jars containing: small bones (animal? small? unclear), a black liquid that flows uphill in the bottle, a folded handkerchief soaked in something rust-colored.
- A mortar and pestle with silver salt residue
- A set of small calipers and a lens on a brass arm — **the Λυχνία της Βάνια** (Vanya's Lens) — see Loot below.

### Walls
- A **map of Δρυόχωρο** with red marks on certain houses (overlapping Σίλας's investigation in Thread 5B, but more current and complete). Marked: Σίλας's house (red X), Marina's shop (red dot), the chapel (red dot), **Μίρα's house (red circle, fresh ink)**, the mill (red question mark), Νεφέλη's cottage (red **avoid** symbol — she will not edit Νεφέλη).
- A **larger regional map** of the valleys around Καίλεν. Multiple thin spots marked. Δρυόχωρο is the smallest dot. Καίλεν is marked with a circled crown. Another smaller spot is marked *«Η Πληγή»* (The Wound). **Major plant for Parts 2 and 3.**
- Charts of phases — possibly moon, possibly something stranger. *«Φάσεις του ρήγματος»* (phases of the rift). The next major phase is **in eight days.**

### Locked cabinet (DC 15 thieves' tools, DC 13 break)
- 47 gold pieces in a leather purse
- A silver ritual knife, plain hilt, sharp. **Sells for ~25gp**, or carry for ritual use.
- Three vials of a clear oily liquid labeled in an unfamiliar script. **Arcana DC 15**: a preserving agent — slows time on whatever it's applied to. (Mechanically: one application keeps food fresh indefinitely, OR keeps a wound from worsening for 24 hours, OR slows a poison.) Three doses.
- A small leather pouch of **silver salt** — ritual component, can be used to draw protective circles. ~5 uses.

### The Sealed Door — Inner Sanctum *(the puzzle)*

Behind a hanging cloth on the back wall of the cellar (Investigation DC 12 to notice the cloth is *too thick* for decoration) is **a second door.** Smaller. Iron. No handle.

In its center, set into the metal: a **brass disc** the size of a hand, with three concentric rings of fine engravings. The disc has no obvious mechanism to turn.

Beneath the disc, scratched into the iron in Vanya's hand:

> *«Τρία πράγματα ανοίγουν αυτό που έχω κλείσει:*
> *αυτό που γεννιέται,*
> *αυτό που βλέπει,*
> *αυτό που υπόσχεται.»*

#### What the rings show *(Arcana DC 12 or Investigation DC 13 to read clearly)*

- **Outer ring** — branching, vegetal, flower-and-vine motifs. Theme: **Birth / Creation / Manifestation.**
- **Middle ring** — open eyes, lenses, a single drop of liquid. Theme: **Sight / Insight / Knowing.**
- **Inner ring** — angular, sword-like marks, a knot binding two lines. Theme: **Vow / Bond / Conviction.**

Each ring is a separate seal. **When all three are satisfied, the disc clicks once and the door swings inward.**

#### How to solve each ring

The point of this puzzle is that **each PC has a class ability that is the perfect fit** for one ring — but no class is *required* to solve any specific ring. There are open paths for any combination of party choices.

##### Outer Ring — *«αυτό που γεννιέται»* (Creation)

| Approach | Result |
|---|---|
| **Bard — Performance of Creation** | Auto. The ring brightens as a manifested object — anything, even a flower — appears nearby. The ring *recognizes the act of bringing something into being.* This is the cleanest solve. |
| **Bard — Mote of Potential** | Auto. A summoned d6 effect counts as a manifestation. |
| Any caster summoning anything (Find Familiar, Conjure spell, etc.) | Auto. |
| Anyone drawing/carving a flower or plant motif on the door with **silver salt from her own supplies** | DC 13 Arcana to do correctly. The act of *creation* is what matters. |
| Religion DC 15 — invoking creation/birth deities | Pass: the ring brightens. |
| Anyone presenting **a freshly-made object they crafted today** (a knotted rope, a sketched note, even a clay figure from the kitchen pot) | DC 12 Persuasion *spoken to the door* — *«Αυτό το έφτιαξα.»* |
| **Brute force**: Arcana DC 18 | Disarm without invoking creation. Possible but hard. |

##### Middle Ring — *«αυτό που βλέπει»* (Insight)

| Approach | Result |
|---|---|
| **Fighter — Know Your Enemy** *(applied to the door as if it were a creature)* | Auto. Allow it. Battle Master flavor is *studying to understand* — Vanya designed her own puzzle, and the door responds to a mind that *truly looks.* The Fighter spends one minute analyzing the disc and the door clicks for the middle ring. **Best fit.** |
| **Paladin — Divine Sense** | Pass. There is faint *undead* aura behind the door (a preserved thing — they will not know what until they enter). The ring responds because someone has *seen* what is hidden. |
| **Vanya's Lens** (if found before solving the puzzle) | Auto. Looking through the lens at the disc reveals the rings have *labels* in a glowing script: this satisfies sight. |
| Investigation DC 16 or Perception DC 16 — methodically dissecting the disc | Pass. |
| Detect Magic, Identify, or any divination ritual | Pass. |
| Bard — Cutting Words deflated *toward the door* as an act of perception | DC 14 Persuasion. Cute and creative — allow it. |
| **Brute force**: Arcana DC 18 | Disarm. |

##### Inner Ring — *«αυτό που υπόσχεται»* (Vow / Conviction)

| Approach | Result |
|---|---|
| **Paladin — Vow of Enmity** *spoken aloud at the door* | Auto. **Best fit.** The Oath of Vengeance is *literally a sworn promise.* The Paladin invokes Vow of Enmity (does not need a target — the door accepts the channeling) and the ring brightens. The Paladin still recovers the Channel Divinity on their next short rest. |
| **Paladin — Channel Divinity: Abjure Enemy** | Auto. The act of channeling the oath satisfies the seal. |
| **Paladin — speaking their oath aloud as roleplay** | Auto. No Channel Divinity spent. The *Sacred Oath itself* is enough if the player speaks it. Reward this. |
| Any PC making **a real, character-binding promise spoken aloud** | DC 14 Persuasion + the player commits to it in-fiction. The DM holds them to it for the rest of the campaign. *«Ορκίζομαι να σώσω το παιδί της Μίρας.»* Allowed for any class — but the player must *mean it.* |
| Religion DC 16 + invoking a god of oaths, vows, or justice | Pass. |
| Sending the Paladin's holy symbol forward as a focused gesture | Pass on a DC 12 Religion check. |
| **Brute force**: Arcana DC 18 | Disarm. |

#### What happens if they try to brute-force the door without satisfying the disc

- Breaking it: AC 17, HP 30, resistance to bludgeoning/piercing/slashing, vulnerable to radiant. **Loud.** And worse: forcing it **triggers a backlash.** Whoever struck the door takes 3d6 psychic damage (DC 14 Wisdom save halves) and loses a flavor memory for 24 hours (player picks something small). This is Vanya's trap.
- **The backlash also alerts her.** Skip directly to the Vanya-mirror beat. She arrives "in" the mirror angry rather than annoyed.

#### Read-aloud (Greek) — when all three rings align

> Όταν ο τρίτος δακτύλιος δέχεται αυτό που του δίνετε, ο δίσκος στο κέντρο της πόρτας **κλικάρει.** Μία φορά. Αργά.
>
> Οι τρεις δακτύλιοι περιστρέφονται μαζί, ευθυγραμμίζοντας τα σύμβολά τους σε μια ενιαία γραμμή.
>
> Η πόρτα ανοίγει προς τα μέσα **χωρίς ήχο.** Σαν να την περιμένει.
>
> Από μέσα, μυρίζετε **κερί και κάτι παιδικό** — γάλα, πούδρα, κάτι μαλακό.

### The Inner Sanctum *(reward for solving the puzzle)*

A small windowless room, ~8 ft × 10 ft. Stone walls. One candle, lit, on a small altar against the back wall.

In the center of the room: **an empty wooden cradle.** Old. Plain. Carved with simple knotwork along the rim. **Veiled** with a fine white cloth that does not stir even when the door opens.

**Do not over-narrate this.** Let the players take it in.

#### What's on the altar

- A **small leather-bound journal**, well-worn — *Vanya's personal journal* (distinct from the working notebook and the experiment log below). This is her *life,* not her work. See contents below.
- A **glass vial** containing dark liquid. Investigation DC 13: it is blood. Arcana DC 14: it is **Vanya's own blood,** kept fresh by a preservation enchantment. She uses it for fragment-binding. *(Mechanically: keep this vial. It is a tool against her for Sessions 7+. Don't tell players the mechanic — they will find a use.)*
- A **single child's tooth**, milk-tooth, in a small clay dish. *Her dead child's. She carries it across timelines.* Emotional weight. No mechanical use.
- A **sealed letter** addressed to *«Στον Δάσκαλο»* (To the Teacher). Sealed in black wax with a sigil the party does not recognize. **They cannot break the seal here without alerting her** (the seal is bound to her). Carry it. #### The cradle (do not lift the veil quickly)

If a PC lifts the veil — any PC, regardless of class — they see the cradle is **empty.** But the mattress is **warm in the shape of a small body.** As if a baby was lying there moments ago.

The compass goes **completely silent** in this room. Not cold. Not warm. **Off.**

This is the most genuinely haunting beat in the dungeon. **Let it sit. Do not explain it.** *(DM truth: Vanya brings the cradle out and rocks it when she is alone here. The thin spot warms the bedding because she imagines her child in it. Heartbreak rendered in fabric.)*

#### What this changes
- **Vanya has a teacher.** Named only as *«Ο Δάσκαλος»*. A hand-off to Part 2 (Καίλεν) and Part 3 (Η Πληγή). Plant.
- **Her plan for Μίρα is explicit and personal.** *«Δεν θα είναι κλοπή. Θα είναι αγάπη.»* She intends to steal the baby into herself. The party knows the full stakes now.
- **The dead child was a boy.** A small detail. Players will remember it.
- **The Δάσκαλος sigil on the sealed letter** is now a thread for Part 2 — they will eventually find someone who recognizes it.

#### XP for solving the puzzle

- Each ring solved: **75 XP per PC**
- All three solved cleanly without backlash: **bonus 150 XP per PC** + Inspiration to whoever led the puzzle
- Class-fit solves (Bard/Creation, Fighter/Insight, Paladin/Vow): each one is a **moment.** The player should feel their character was *meant* for this. Inspiration to each.
- Reading the personal journal: **150 XP per PC** (the emotional and lore weight)

#### DM notes for the puzzle

- **Lean into the class fits.** If the Bard's player hesitates, prompt: *«Η Performance of Creation σου ίσως ανταποκρίνεται σε αυτόν τον κύκλο.»* If the Paladin's player is shy, prompt: *«Ο όρκος της Εκδίκησης είναι μια υπόσχεση. Αυτό ζητάει ο κύκλος.»* Give them the gift of *their character mattering.*
- **But honor creative solutions equally.** A player who carves a daisy with charcoal on the door has *invented creation.* That is just as good as Performance of Creation.
- **The brute-force option is real.** Some groups will smash the door. Let them. The backlash is the cost, and it is fair.
- **The veiled cradle is the emotional climax of the dungeon.** Slow down. Lower your voice. Do not joke. Let the players have this moment.

### Λυχνία της Βάνια (Vanya's Lens) — **magic item**

A small lens of obsidian-tinted glass on a brass arm, originally mounted on Vanya's workbench. **Wondrous item, uncommon, no attunement required.**

When a creature looks through the lens at another creature, place, or object: edited subjects appear with a faint **shimmer at the edges.** The lens shows whether something has been touched by the thin spot — not what was changed, just that *it has been.*

**Charges**: 3. Recharges 1d3 at dawn.
**Action**: a creature can use the lens as a bonus action to scan a 30-ft area, revealing all edited subjects in line of sight.

**This item changes the campaign.** It lets the party diagnose edits without having to find evidence of the original timeline. Use it carefully — it should not let them solve everything trivially. The lens cannot show *who* did the editing, only that something *was* edited.

## DM reminders for this thread

- **The upstairs is a slow burn. The cellar is the meat.** Do not let players bog down in the empty house too long if they're losing energy — speed them downstairs.
- **The vessels cannot be reasoned with.** This is the design choice. If players try to grace-note, Lay on Hands, Speak with Dead, etc., the answer is: *«Δεν υπάρχει κανείς εκεί μέσα. Είναι αδειασμένο.»* The grace note path is the *exception*, not the rule, in this campaign. This is the first time they meet that.
- **Vanya is not here.** She does not appear physically. The mirror beat is her *seeing through*, not her arriving. Do not let the party think they fought her.
- **The notebook and experiment log are the campaign-altering reward.** Do not skim them. Read them aloud. Let players sit with each entry.
- **Κόρη is not Vanya's edit.** This is important. If players assume she is, let it be a mistake they correct later — Νεφέλη will eventually tell them.
- **The cracked mirror is the most important item they may not understand yet.** Make sure they take it.
- **Burning vs burying the vessels matters.** Honor the choice with consequence.
