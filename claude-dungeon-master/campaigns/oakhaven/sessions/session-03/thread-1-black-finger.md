# Thread 1 — Το Μαύρο Δάχτυλο
*(The Pull. The compass destination. The lore dump of Part 1.)*

---

## Entry

The compass has been pointing north since Session 2's cliffhanger. Whichever stage of the day this fires, the journey is the same: ~5 hours uphill through the pass between two of the smaller Brothers, ending at the broken obsidian tower.

**The party has been here once before**, during the storm in Session 1. They walked into the cellar passively. This time they choose.

---

## Stage 1 — The Climb (1-2 hours from village)

### The standing stones (15 min)
If they did not return to the stones in Session 2's epilogue, they pass them now. The compass ticks irregularly. Eight stones countable. The ninth still hidden.

If a PC speaks **Ιώ's ninth-line gibberish** (or the rhyme up to the ninth line, even haltingly), the compass goes silent for ~10 seconds, then gives one clean tick. **No further effect.** Foreshadow: the rhyme is a key. They will need it later.

### The Oldwood edge (30 min in)
The trail skirts the edge of Ο Παλαιόδρυμος. The trees thin and thicken in patches that don't match the map. **Passive Perception 13**: they are being followed. A shape at the edge of vision. It does not approach. It does not retreat. It *paces them.*

This is the Άφτιαχτο from Thread 4 — keeping its distance for now. Drop it explicitly when you want the combat to fire. Atmospheric until then.

### Read-aloud (Greek)

> Ο δρόμος βγαίνει απ' το χωριό και ανηφορίζει. Η ομίχλη της κοιλάδας μένει πίσω σας — εδώ πάνω ο αέρας είναι καθαρότερος, αλλά πιο κρύος. Τα δέντρα του Παλαιοδρύμου σας βλέπουν να περνάτε.
>
> Κάποια στιγμή — δεν μπορείτε να πείτε πότε ακριβώς — αρχίζετε να αισθάνεστε ότι δεν είστε μόνοι. Δεν υπάρχει θόρυβος. Δεν υπάρχει σκιά. Μόνο μια αίσθηση που θα μπορούσε να είναι παράνοια αν δεν ήταν τόσο σταθερή.
>
> Η πυξίδα στο στήθος του [Fighter] *ζεσταίνεται.*

## Stage 2 — The Pass (2-3 hours)

The trail climbs into a saddle between two Brothers. Exposed. No shelter. Wind. Late autumn — could be sleet, could be cold rain, could be the first snow.

### Survival check — DC 13
Pass: they reach the tower without exhaustion.
Fail: one level of exhaustion on whoever rolled, or on the first PC to walk point if they're not navigating deliberately.

### What they see
The tower becomes visible mid-climb. A black sliver against grey sky, broken at the top. From up here, **the village looks small.** Smoke rising from chimneys. The Marrow a thin line of silver. They can see *all* of Δρυόχωρο from this angle for the first time.

### Read-aloud (Greek) — first sight of the tower from the pass

> Το πέρασμα ανοίγει και τον βλέπετε.
>
> Ένα μαύρο δάχτυλο που υψώνεται απ' τη βραχώδη γη — γυαλιστερό, σπασμένο στη μέση, αρχαίο. Δεν είναι μεγαλύτερος από όσο τον θυμόσασταν, αλλά εδώ, στο φως της ημέρας, βλέπετε για πρώτη φορά πόσο **σπασμένος** είναι. Η κορυφή του είναι σχισμένη — όχι από τον καιρό. Από κάτι.
>
> Η πυξίδα στο στήθος σου σταματάει να τικ-τακάει.
>
> Αρχίζει να **πάλλεται.** Σαν δεύτερη καρδιά.

---

## Stage 3 — Approach (1 hour)

The descent into the tower's depression. The wind dies. Sound becomes muffled. **The rain (if any) does not wet the obsidian walls** — it slides off without leaving a trace. They saw this in Session 1 but were too disoriented by the storm to mark it. Now it's eerie.

### What's new (vs. Session 1)
- **The upper tower damage is deliberate.** They can see it clearly now. Hammer-strike marks. Someone climbed up there centuries ago and tried to break the structure.
- **There are no birds.** The whole approach is silent. Even the wind doesn't whistle here.
- **The compass is hot.** Almost too hot to wear next to skin. Fighter may need to pull it out and hold it in hand.

### Read-aloud (Greek) — at the foot of the tower

> Στέκεστε στη βάση του πύργου. Το μαύρο γυαλί είναι κρύο και λείο, και όταν τον ακουμπάτε νιώθετε ότι **τραγουδάει** πολύ χαμηλά — τόσο χαμηλά που το νιώθετε στα δόντια σας πριν το ακούσετε.
>
> Η πόρτα του υπογείου είναι όπως την αφήσατε.

## Stage 4 — The Cellar (if Session 3 has time, OR for Session 4)

### What they find this time

In Session 1 they were too storm-shocked to look properly. Now, with the compass on, with the field in their bones, with the skeleton's image fresh in their minds, they see *everything.*

#### The ritual hall
- A circular chamber, ~30 ft across
- **Five stone pillars** in a ring around a central depression
- Four pillars: ancient, carved with concentric runes, intact
- **The fifth pillar: defaced.** Chiseled. Someone took a hammer to the runes. The damage is centuries old. The intent was clear: break the binding.
- The central depression: a shallow basin, dry, made of the same obsidian as the tower

#### The four frozen scholars
**Bury this beat.** Don't rush it. Greek read-aloud:

> Στη μέση του δωματίου, στέκονται **τέσσερις άντρες.**
>
> Όχι πτώματα. Όχι σκελετοί. Άντρες — με δέρμα, με ρούχα, με αναπνοή. Ή σχεδόν με αναπνοή. Ένας κρατάει ένα όργανο στο χέρι του. Ο άλλος έχει το στόμα ανοιχτό σε μια λέξη που ποτέ δεν τελείωσε. Η τρίτη — μια γυναίκα — κοιτάζει κάτι που δεν υπάρχει εκεί που κοιτάζει.
>
> Φοράνε τα ίδια ρούχα με τον σκελετό σας.
>
> Όταν τους πλησιάζετε, βλέπετε ότι αναπνέουν. Πολύ αργά. Μία φορά κάθε ίσως δέκα λεπτά.

DC 13 Wisdom save or be **shaken** (disadvantage on next ability check) for 1 minute. The horror is in the *near-life,* not death.

#### The fifth slot
Empty. **A footprint of dust** where the fifth scholar should have stood. The skeleton from the mine was the fifth. He left.

#### The conductor seed (if they search)
Investigation DC 14 in the central basin: silver wire, fragments of leaded glass, a small brass funnel. *Pieces of an instrument that was never finished — or was disassembled in haste.* They don't know what it does yet.

#### Interaction with the items they brought
- **The compass placed in the central basin**: pulses harder. The four scholars' breaths *almost* synchronize. Don't let the players solve this. Just plant the question.
- **The watch placed in the basin**: the four scholars' eyes — closed until now — *flutter once.* No more. They do not wake.
- **If they brought the skeleton's bones**: laying them in the fifth slot causes one scholar (the woman) to slowly turn her head 1° over the course of a minute. Then she stops. The ritual is incomplete. Without the conductor and a sixth willing participant, the scholars cannot wake.

## Stage 5 — The Observatory (above)

### Access
The interior stairs are intact for the first half. Above that, broken — the upper tower's damage. Climbing requires a DC 12 Athletics check, two failures = falling (1d6 damage). Or rope and patience: no roll.

### What they find
A small circular room. Glass dome (cracked). Brass instruments long oxidized. **An iron lockbox**, deliberately placed in the center of the floor.

Lockbox is not magically sealed — just a heavy padlock. Picking: DC 14 (thieves' tools) or breaking: STR check DC 16 / damage threshold.

### Inside the lockbox

A single sheet of thick paper, in neat academic Greek hand, twenty years old:

> *Στο όνομα της Ακαδημίας των Ωρών της Καίλεν, και ενώπιον όποιου διαβάσει αυτό:*
>
> *Με λένε Αριστόδημο Κρέοντα. Ήρθα εδώ ψάχνοντας τις πέτρες των Εννιά Φυλάκων και βρήκα κάτι μεγαλύτερο. Στον υπόγειο αυτού του πύργου, τέσσερις ζουν χωρίς να ζουν. Η πέμπτη θέση είναι κενή. Διάβασα τις σημειώσεις τους — όσες μπόρεσα — και κατάλαβα: ήταν Πέντε. Ένας Έφυγε. Το έργο τους δεν ολοκληρώθηκε.*
>
> *Δεν μπορώ να τους ξυπνήσω μόνος. Χρειάζομαι έναν **έκτο** — έναν που να θέλει να σταθεί στον κύκλο γνωρίζοντας ότι ίσως δεν φύγει ζωντανός. Πηγαίνω στην Ακαδημία να βρω τέτοιον άνθρωπο. Αν διαβάζετε αυτό και εγώ δεν έχω επιστρέψει — η απάντηση είναι στην Καίλεν. Ζητήστε την Ακαδημία των Ωρών. Πείτε τους:*
>
> ***«Ο κύκλος είναι ανοιχτός και κάποιος το ξέρει.»***
>
> *Αριστόδημος Κρέων*

### What this gives the party
- A name: **Αριστόδημος Κρέων**
- A destination: **Καίλεν** and **η Ακαδημία των Ωρών**
- A code phrase: *«Ο κύκλος είναι ανοιχτός και κάποιος το ξέρει.»*
- The bridge from Part 1 (Δρυόχωρο) to Part 2 (Καίλεν)

He never made it back. They will eventually find his fate — but not this session.

## DM notes — what NOT to reveal yet

The party should **not** learn this session:
- That the cellar's stasis field is what kept their original-timeline memories intact in Session 1
- That the compass is one of the Circle's instruments
- That the conductor was the second instrument — the missing one — and that the fifth scholar took it when he fled
- That **Vanya's predecessor** is the one who defaced the fifth pillar centuries ago
- That a sixth willing participant would have to *die* for the ritual to complete

**Save these for Sessions 4-6.** The tower is the spine of Part 1. Do not blow the load.

## Consequence if Thread 1 is ignored entirely

Unlikely — the compass is on the Fighter and is pointing. But if they refuse to go:

- The compass grows progressively warmer over 24 hours. After 48 hours it is uncomfortable to wear. After 72 hours it burns. The party will go.
- If they actively *take it off* and leave it: it works its way back, the way it works out of a pack. It will be on the Fighter's neck by morning, no matter where he left it.
- Vanya escalates her probing of Mira's pregnancy if they don't visit. Each day they delay = one degradation step on Mira (Thread 2).
