## Recent Events (Session 1)
- Group traveling to Kaelen is intercepted by a messenger bearing a letter addressed to the bard. Written in the bard's own hand, sealed with the dwarf's clan seal: *"Come to Oakhaven, time is breaking."*
- Unnatural storm forces shelter in a ruined obsidian tower. In its cellar laboratory they find a ticking, warm **compass** that points to nothing. A **field** forms around them (unknown to them: it insulates them from timeline rewrites).
- Arrive at Oakhaven. Nobody recognizes the letter or the compass.
- At the inn: barkeep **Sylas** (hard, angry). A hooded woman — **Vanya** — writes in a journal, polite but distant. She leaves.
- Morning: a **pregnant woman** begs them to find her husband **Thomas**, missing from the lumber mill crew.
- They enter the **dwarven mine** above the mill. Rescue Thomas. Find an ancient skeleton with a perfectly preserved **steel watch** running backward.
- **Time slip**: they entered by day, exit at night. Villagers act as if nothing is odd.
- Return Thomas to his wife. Sleep at the inn.
- **Cliffhanger**: woken by the compass shrieking/ticking, now hot and spinning. Downstairs the inn is *different*. Sylas is gone — an old man claims Sylas died ten years ago in a barfight. A letter-sized rift opens. **The bard begins to fade from existence**. The party writes the letter — *"Come to Oakhaven, time is breaking"* — to send to their past selves. Session ends.

## Recent Events (Session 2)
- Rift closes. Bard snaps back into reality. Altered inn accepted them. They walk the village briefly — nothing else visibly changed — then sleep.
- Morning: go directly to Θωμάς. Μίρα opens the door. Θωμάς is remembering *both* timelines simultaneously — Sylas alive two nights ago and Sylas dead ten years ago, with equal clarity. Compass proximity eases his state.
- Νεφέλη visited. She acts strange, speaks in riddles, senses something is wrong but cannot name it. Refuses to touch compass or watch. Party grows suspicious of her (she is not a villain). Party forces the items on her anyway — she gets scared, urges them to leave. They bring Θωμάς to her. Her presence also stabilizes him.
- Party goes to Τα Σπλάχνα. Compass reacts to the skeleton — goes cold on contact. They bring the skeleton back to the village.
- Villagers notice the skeleton being carried through town. Ορέστης is fetched.
- Skeleton left at Νεφέλη's cottage. She examines it: *"died from something that had no name when it killed him."* Will not touch the watch.
- Party meets with Ορέστης. He trusts them immediately and is told everything. They dig Sylas's grave together: **10-year-old headstone, 2-day-old fresh body inside.** Relocation confirmed physically.
- They dig the unnamed baby grave: **fake — bundles made to look like a baby, clay head.** An *invented* person — Vanya created a person who never existed. Ορέστης is sick and shaken.
- Party heads uphill to Οι Εννιά Φύλακες at dusk. Reach the stones. Try to count — always land on 8. **Session ends here.**
- **Missed this session**: compass pointing to Το Μαύρο Δάχτυλο (carry to Session 3 opening). Ο Κόρακας (Thread 6), Άφτιαχτα (Thread 7), Βόσσου journals (Thread 8), Γερόντισσα Εστία (Thread 5) all untouched.

### Three Truths — Oakhaven
1. **Obvious**: A quiet logging village on the road to Kaelen
2. **Investigable**: Time doesn't behave here. Old folk misremember dates. Graves whose lichen age doesn't match their carved dates. Nine standing stones above the village that children can count to nine and adults cannot
3. **Secret**: Oakhaven sits on a **drop of a dying god's blood** — the smallest known thin spot. Nine Watchers were placed over it to contain it; one of them hides herself from mortal sight, and the pattern is failing. Vanya chose it because it is small enough to experiment on without losing the whole village (yet)

### Three Truths — Vanya
1. **Obvious**: A quiet woman at the inn with a journal
2. **Investigable**: She's a scholar of some kind; asks strange questions about local history
3. **Secret**: She has already lived this month dozens of times. She is trying to find the exact sequence of changes that brings her child back without collapsing the timeline

### Tone Reminders
- Dark, gritty, sensory. Humour through the bard and absurd time-logic moments, not slapstick
- Death is real. Foreshadow danger. Honor player creativity
- NPCs remember. The world moves when the party isn't looking
