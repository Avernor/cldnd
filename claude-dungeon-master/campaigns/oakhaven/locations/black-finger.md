# Το Μαύρο Δάχτυλο (The Black Finger)

The ruined obsidian tower in a pass between two of the smaller Brothers. The party already visited once, during the storm in Session 1, entering only the cellar laboratory and leaving with the compass. The compass's needle points here at the end of Session 2. This file preps the **return visit** — expected Session 3 or 4.

---

## What the party already knows

- It is a collapsed tower of black volcanic glass (obsidian), ~40 ft tall before it broke, now maybe 25 ft of jagged ruin.
- It stands alone in a high pass between two of the smaller Nine Brothers, roughly 5 hours' hard walking from Δρυόχωρο through a trail that is not on any common map.
- In its cellar was a **laboratory** — workbenches, broken glassware, ancient chalk diagrams, a single preserved steel compass that ticks and is warm.
- When they touched the compass, **a field formed around them** — a soft, contained wrongness they could feel on their skin. They did not know what it was. (DM: it insulated them from the Vanya edit that erased/relocated Σίλας hours later at the inn. Without the visit to this cellar, they would remember nothing.)
- The storm outside was unnatural and broke shortly after they left.

## What they did *not* see on the first visit

- The tower has **three levels**, not one. They saw only the cellar.
- The cellar itself has a second chamber they did not find — a hidden inner sanctum behind a collapsed shelf.
- There are **other bodies** in the tower. Five in total — a Circle of Five scholars who tried to tap the thin spot.
- The field is still *on.* They walked through a suspended moment of time without understanding it.

---

## Geography & approach

### Getting there
- ~5 hours' hard hike from Δρυόχωρο. **Survival DC 12** to find the pass without getting lost in the foothills; DC 10 if Ο Κόρακας drew them a map (Thread 6 connection).
- The trail climbs steeply for the last hour. Footing is loose scree. **Athletics DC 10** or advantage on a forced march check; failure means exhaustion 1 on arrival.
- **Weather**: the Brothers make their own weather. A 60% chance of sudden cold rain or sleet regardless of valley conditions. The party should feel the *wrongness* of the weather as they climb — it is not the season for this.

### First sight (Greek read-aloud — arrival)
> Το μονοπάτι ανοίγει σε ένα πέρασμα. Ο άνεμος εδώ πάνω μυρίζει σαν μέταλλο και κρύα πέτρα. Και εκεί στέκεται — όπως το θυμόσαστε, και όπως δεν το θυμόσαστε. Το Μαύρο Δάχτυλο. Ένας πύργος από μαύρο γυαλί, σπασμένος στη μέση, γυαλιστερός ακόμα κι εδώ όπου ο ουρανός είναι γκρίζος. Η πέτρα του είναι λεία και κρύα σαν παγωμένο νερό. Η βροχή κυλάει πάνω της χωρίς να τη βρέξει.
>
> Η πυξίδα στο χέρι όποιου την κρατάει *τραβάει.* Όχι δυνατά. Αλλά σταθερά. Σας θέλει μέσα.

## Level -1a — The Cellar Laboratory *(already visited)*

As the party left it, minus one compass. The field is *still on*. Stepping across the threshold, they feel the same soft wrongness on their skin as last time. Anything they leave here is preserved. Anything they say here lingers in the air a half-beat too long.

### New things to notice (now that they know what they are looking at)

- **The diagrams on the walls** are not alchemical. They are **temporal** — concentric rings of Greek letters and numerals tracking *displacement* in time and space. **Arcana DC 14** or **History DC 13**: this is proto-chronomancy. **Arcana DC 17**: one ring is a schematic of the compass itself. This was built here.
- **Five workstations, but two eras.** Last time the party noticed one and grabbed the compass. Now: five in total. Four are ancient — obsidian desks crusted with old reagents, tools set down mid-use, notes in four different centuries-old hands. The fifth workstation is obviously **much more recent** (**Investigation DC 12** to notice the difference) — better-preserved paper, ink that still smells faintly of oak gall, a travel lantern with a wick that would burn if you lit it. It sits in what was once the Circle's fifth slot, reused by a later visitor.
- **The notes** on the recent station are a journal fragment. The name on the inside cover: ***Αριστόδημος Κρέων.*** (This is the hook that ties the tower directly to Thread 5 of Session 2 — the scholar from Η Ακαδημία των Ωρών in Καίλεν.) The party now knows that Αριστόδημος *came here alone* centuries after the Circle had already been frozen, camped in their lab, read their work, and left to fetch help. He is **not** one of the Circle. He is their reader. This is the bridge to Part 2.
- **Four of the workstations are... busy.** At first glance the tools are just lying where they were set down. On a second look (Perception DC 14) the *positions shift* between looks. A quill ink-line that was halfway across a page is now three-quarters across. A candle that was at 2 inches is now at 1.9 inches. Nothing is happening — except it *is*, very slowly, at the speed of the field.

### Hidden: the inner sanctum door

**Investigation DC 15** along the north wall reveals that a collapsed shelf of obsidian is not *quite* flush with the stone behind it. Behind it is a low door. **Strength DC 14** to clear the rubble, or **Thieves' Tools** to bypass a simple mechanical catch the builders left for themselves.

## Level -1b — The Inner Sanctum *(new)*

A small round chamber, maybe 15 ft across. In the center, **four figures in scholars' robes**, standing in a loose ring facing inward. Preserved. Not dead. Not alive. The field is at its strongest here — the party's breath comes slow, their own heartbeats sound as if through cloth.

### Greek read-aloud — first entry
> Η αίθουσα είναι μικρή και στρογγυλή. Τέσσερις μορφές στέκονται στη μέση, ντυμένοι με ραβδωτές ρόμπες μελετητών. Δεν κινούνται. Δεν αναπνέουν — ή μάλλον, αναπνέουν, αλλά τόσο αργά που πρέπει να τους κοιτάξετε για ένα ολόκληρο λεπτό για να είστε σίγουροι. Τα μάτια τους είναι ανοιχτά. Κοιτούν κάτι ανάμεσά τους που εσείς δεν βλέπετε. Το πάτωμα κάτω από τα πόδια τους είναι χαραγμένο με έναν κύκλο — και ο κύκλος είναι σπασμένος σε ένα σημείο, εκεί ακριβώς που λείπει μια πέμπτη μορφή.

### The four scholars

The Circle of Five, mid-experiment, caught in the field they themselves created. The fifth member — the one who fled — was the man the party found in Τα Σπλάχνα with the backward watch. His absence *broke the ring*, and the field collapsed into stasis around the remaining four instead of discharging.

Each scholar is a ready-to-run NPC for if the party wakes one:

1. **Ισμήνη η Καλκάντου** — middle-aged woman, sharp features, ink on her hands. *Led the circle.* If woken (see below), she is calm, clear, and immediately aware that something has gone very wrong. Her first question: *«Πόσος χρόνος;»* (How long?)
2. **Φίλων ο Μέγας** — huge man, arms like a smith's, robes ill-fitting. The circle's *body* — protector and ritual anchor. If woken, disoriented and terrified. Will try to leave.
3. **Νικαίος ο Τυφλός** — small, thin, his eyes milky. Blind in life; the circle's *ear* — listened for the thin spot's voice. If woken, the only one who *already knows*. Whispers: *«Το ακούω ακόμα. Δεν σταμάτησε ποτέ.»*
4. **Μυρτώ η Νέα** — young, maybe nineteen when the field took her. Apprentice. Her notes are the cleanest on the workstations upstairs because she wrote carefully. If woken, she asks for her mother. Her mother has been dead for two hundred years.

### How to wake one

The field will not release them on its own. A deliberate act breaks it for one:
- **Lay on Hands** used on a scholar (at least 5 HP spent) — **wakes them cleanly**.
- **Any radiant damage** touching a scholar — wakes them, but with 1 level of exhaustion.
- **Dispel Magic** (DC 15) — wakes them all.
- **Removing the compass from the sanctum** — the field weakens; roll a d6 at the end of each round the compass is gone, on a 6 one scholar wakes randomly.

**The woken scholar begins to age.** At 1 hour per round once they leave the sanctum, they are catching up on two hundred years of real time. Unless the party *returns them to the circle* (deliberately, with the compass present), they will reach their natural end within five minutes of conversation and crumble to dust. Most will choose to talk anyway — they know they are already dead and want to be *useful.*

### What a woken scholar will tell the party (prioritized)

- The thin spot under Δρυόχωρο is a **drop of a dying god's blood.** They knew. Αριστόδημος brought the theory from the Ακαδημία des Καίλεν. They came here to prove it.
- Their plan was to build an **anchor** (the compass) that would let a mortal stand inside the thin spot without dissolving, and a **conductor** (something else — a second instrument, *the party will learn the name now*) that would let the anchor's bearer *speak to* whatever was bleeding. They built the anchor. They built the conductor. The experiment was the speaking.
- **The conductor is missing.** The fifth scholar took it with him when he fled. He died in the mine. The conductor is either still in Τα Σπλάχνα, somewhere the party did not look, or someone has already taken it.
- **Vanya is not the first.** Someone else tried this — the scholars do not know who — before them. The previous attempt cracked the containment. Theirs was meant to heal it. It *almost* worked. They could not finish because their fifth broke ring.
- **The thin spot has a voice.** Νικαίος ο Τυφλός heard it. It sounds like a woman weeping. It is *not* Vanya. It is older. It is *what she drank from to learn.*

## Level -2 — Το Πηγάδι (The Well)

Reached through a stone hatch in the floor of the sanctum, bound shut with iron bands that still hold. **Strength DC 16** to force, or **Thieves' Tools DC 15**, or the bands release on their own if a woken scholar gives the party the word — *«Ανοίξου»* — in the language Γερόντισσα Εστία sings.

Below: a smooth-walled obsidian shaft descending maybe 20 ft to a circular floor chamber. In the exact center of the chamber floor, a **ring of pale silver inlay** about 3 feet across. Inside the ring, the obsidian is not obsidian. It is something that is *not a surface* — the eye cannot quite settle on it. Looking at it too long: **Wisdom save DC 13, failure = 1 level of exhaustion and a brief taste of salt in the mouth.**

### What it is

This is the **contact point.** The thin spot exposed. A single drop of a dying god's ichor held in the silver ring, beneath the compass-field, at the bottom of the tower built to contain it. The compass points here because it is the nearest open drop of the same blood it was made from.

**If the party steps into the ring** (not recommended this session, but possible):
- The one in the ring hears a woman's weeping that is not Vanya.
- A single question is asked, without words: *«Τι θέλεις να αλλάξεις;»* (What do you want to change?)
- Any honest answer — even a refusal — is *accepted*. The thin spot notes the speaker. *The real Vanya will feel this.* She will know a new player has announced themselves, and she will move accordingly. Handle with full weight.
- Dishonest answers are rejected. The speaker is flung back out of the ring, takes 2d6 psychic damage, and loses one small memory (DM's choice, reversible).

**The compass's behavior inside the ring**: it stops ticking entirely and grows *cold.* Returns to normal when carried back up.

## Level 1 — Ritual Hall *(the broken ground floor)*

If the party climbs up through the collapsed stairwell instead of (or after) going deeper. **Athletics DC 12** to climb the unstable rubble.

A large circular hall open to the sky where the upper tower is missing. Obsidian pillars ring the chamber. Defaced symbols are carved into their faces — old, deliberate defacement. Someone struck at these with a hammer before the tower fell. **Arcana DC 15**: the original symbols were *binding runes*. Something broke them on purpose.

- **History DC 14**: the defacement is much older than the Circle of Five. Whoever did it was working against whoever originally built this tower. The Circle moved in later and tried to *fix* a tower that had already been *attacked*.
- **This is the nine/nine mirror payoff**: the defaced pillars number *nine*. Eight are cracked. One is whole. The one whole pillar corresponds to the one *missing* Watcher stone in the village and the one *missing* Brother in the range. Three absences, one pattern.

## Level 2 — The Observatory *(optional reward)*

Further **Athletics DC 14** climb through the broken ceiling. The upper chamber is open to the sky, half its walls missing. A single intact iron frame stands at the east edge — once an astronomical instrument. A few loose pages under a rock weighed down by the circle's fifth scholar before he fled.

- The pages are **star charts** annotated with dates the party does not yet understand. **Investigation DC 13**: the charts align the Nine Watcher stones with nine *astronomical* points — the Brothers in the sky, if you will. One point is marked *«αυτή που κρύβει τον εαυτό της»* (she who hides herself). This is the missing ninth.
- Also here: a small iron lockbox, unlocked. Inside, a single folded letter in Αριστόδημος's hand, addressed *«Προς όποιον το διαβάσει μετά από εμένα»* (To whoever reads this after me). The letter is a confession: he has read the Circle's notes, understood what they tried to do, and realized the ritual cannot be completed without a **sixth member who knows, in advance, that they are going to die in the ring** — the Circle's fifth fled because they discovered this too late. Αριστόδημος will not ask it of a stranger. He is leaving for Καίλεν to bring back someone from the Ακαδημία who will understand the cost. **Whether he ever made it home is an open question.** His notes may survive in the capital. His body may not. **This is the signature bridge-piece from Part 1 to Part 2.** Καίλεν is where the trail goes cold and hot.

## Threats on this visit

The tower itself is not hostile. The dangers:

1. **Structural collapse** — climbing checks as noted. A critical fail (natural 1) drops a PC 10 ft for 1d6 damage.
2. **The field** — cumulative exposure. After 1 hour in any field-affected space without the compass nearby, Wisdom save DC 12 or lose one small memory (reversible). The compass protects within 10 ft. Party should feel this.
3. **Τα Άφτιαχτα in the pass** — if Session 2's grace note did not happen, one or two follow the party up. They will not enter the tower itself (the field confuses them) but wait in the pass on the return trip. Good ambush opportunity.
4. **If the party steps into the ring** — they wake *something* that will matter later. Not a fight in this session. A consequence for the next.
5. **No combat encounter scripted for this location.** Session 3 should be a slow, dread-heavy exploration. Save combat for what walks out of the tower *with* them on the way home.

## DM notes

- **Do not run Level -2 this session** unless the players are clearly itching. The Well is a Session 4 or 5 payoff. If they rush to it, the iron bands hold and the door does not open this time. That is okay. Narrate the weight of it, let them feel it is there, pull them back to Level -1b.
- **Do not kill the party's Session 1 memory of this place.** They have earned the "we've been here" feeling. Reward it — let them recognize details, let them feel smart.
- **The Circle of Five are a finite resource.** Each scholar the party wakes dies. Make each one count. Do not wake all four — wake **Ισμήνη** first because she leads, and let the party choose whether to wake any others.
- **Leave the tower feeling bigger on the way out than on the way in.** They came for answers; they leave with three new questions for every answer. That is correct.
